[ Dougherty County Georgia Dougherty County Georgia.png 225 1853 Albany Albany 334.64 329.60 5.04 1.51% 2010 94565 292 113 Eastern -5 -4 www.albany.ga.us/county_gov.htm ] Dougherty County is a county located in the U.S. state of Georgia. As of the 2010 census, the population was 94,565. [ United States Census Bureau 2010 Census Data http://2010.census.gov/2010census/data/ United States Census Bureau 9 February 2012 ] It is included in the Albany, Georgia Metropolitan Statistical Area. The county seat and largest city is Albany. [ 6 ] [ History ] [ May 2010 ] The county was created by the Georgia General Assembly on December 15, 1853, from a part of Baker County and was named after Charles Dougherty, a respected judge and lawyer from Athens, Georgia. [ Geography ] According to the 2000 census, the county has a total area of [ 334.64 sqmi ], of which [ 329.60 sqmi ] (or 98.49%) is land and [ 5.04 sqmi ] (or 1.51%) is water.
