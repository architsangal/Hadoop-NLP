[ 1948 11 26 yes ] { Hawarden } { British } { Alan Keen } { Labour } { 2 sons, 1 daughter } { Brentford and } { Waterloo } { University of Surrey } { Nursing } Ann Lloyd Keen (née Fox, born 26 November 1948) is a British Labour Party politician, who was the Member of Parliament (MP) for Brentford and Isleworth from 1997, until she was defeated by Conservative candidate Mary Macleod in 2010. [ Early life ] Keen is the daughter of steelworker John Lloyd Fox and Ruby Hughes. She went to Elfed Secondary Modern School in Buckley, Clwyd, then gained a PGCEA (Postgraduate Certificate in the Education of Adults) from the University of Surrey. She worked in the National Health Service (NHS) before training as a registered nurse at Ashford General Hospital in Ashford, Middlesex, [ http://www.ashfordstpeters.nhs.uk/press-releases-2007/165-press-releases-2007/263-health-minister-returns-to-her-roots Health Minister returns to her roots Communications Team Ashford and St. Peters Hospitals NHS Trust 3 August 2007 31 December 2009 ] and won prizes as Nurse of the Year and Children&apos;s Nurse of the Year. She later became a district nurse. From 1989 –1993, she was Head of the Faculty of Advanced Nursing at Queen Charlotte&apos;s College in Hammersmith (now part of Thames Valley University ) and she also served as General Secretary of the Community and District Nursing Association. [ http://news.bbc.co.uk/hi/english/static/vote2001/candidates/candidates/0/09005.stm Vote 2001: Ann Keen BBC News 31 December 2009 ] [ Parliamentary career ] Keen stood for the Brentford &amp; Isleworth seat in 1987 and 1992 though was unsuccessful on both occasions. [ http://news.bbc.co.uk/democracylive/hi/representatives/profiles/25461.stm Democracy Live: Ann Keen BBC News 31 December 2009 ] For the 1997 election she was again selected, on this occasion through an all-women shortlist. { http://www.parliament.uk/commons/lib/research/rp2001/rp01-075.pdf } This method of selection was declared unlawful in January 1996 as it breached sex discrimination laws. [ http://www.independent.co.uk/news/labour-blow-as-allwomen-lists-outlawed-1323046.html Labour blow as all-women lists outlawed The Independent 9 January 1996 London John Rentoul Stephen Ward Donald MacIntyre 25 April 2010 ] Despite the ruling she remained in place as the candidate for the 1997 general election, when she became an MP defeating Conservative MP Nirj Deva. [ http://www.guardian.co.uk/politics/constituency/756/brentford-and-isleworth London The Guardian Brentford and Isleworth Conservative: Mary Macleod ] Keen&apos;s first role in Parliament came in 1999 when she was appointed as Parliamentary Private Secretary to Frank Dobson, Secretary of State for Health, though this appointment lasted less than a month. [ http://www.guardian.co.uk/politics/person/2832/ann-keen Ann Keen: Electoral history and profile The Guardian 31 December 2009 London ] She then moved to the Treasury, later becoming Parliamentary Private Secretary to the then Chancellor of the Exchequer, Gordon Brown. In 2000 Keen became well known for campaigning on gay rights, her interest in the issue sparked when she was reunited with her gay son who she had given up for adoption. [ http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/2055642.stm BBC News Ann Keen 21 October 2002 25 April 2010 Labour Mp ] In the same year she also successfully ran a campaign to elect Michael Martin as Speaker to the House of Commons. She was defeated at the 2010 general election by Conservative candidate Mary Macleod on a swing of 6%. [ http://www.publicwhip.org.uk/division.php?date%3D2009-01-28%26amp%3Bnumber%3D26%26amp%3Bmpn%3DAnn%20Keen Heathrow Third Runway — rethink the plans — rejected The Public Whip 2 January 2010 ] Keen had claimed to be opposed to expansion at Heathrow for many years; her website stated in 2007 that &quot; one of her most successful campaigns was against the Third Runway at Heathrow. &quot; [ http://web.archive.org/web/20070506042601/http%3A//www.annkeenmp.org.uk/news/campaigns.php Campaigns Ann Keen 6 May 2007 ] Friends of the Earth said she had &quot; betrayed her constituents. &quot; [ http://www.yourlocalguardian.co.uk/news/local/hounslowchiswick/4087031.Hounslow_MPs__betrayed__constituents_in_Heathrow_vote/ Keens vote in favour of third runway at Heathrow 29 January 2009 Houndslow &amp; Chiswick Times ] [ Expenses ] [ United Kingdom Parliamentary expenses scandal ] Keen and her MP husband Alan Keen used their combined second homes allowances to buy an apartment in an up-market development at Waterloo on the South Bank of the River Thames, claiming £175,000 over five years. The Waterloo apartment is nine miles from their constituency home in Brentford, a 30-minute drive from Westminster. The Fees Office agreed with the couple&apos;s argument that this was claimable because it was used to raise equity for the flat. The mortgage also included the cost of &quot; compulsory &quot; life insurance attached to the mortgages, a practice which is now banned. Their claims caused some newspapers to nickname the couple &quot; Mr and Mrs Expenses &quot;.
