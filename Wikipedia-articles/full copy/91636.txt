[ Carson County Texas Map of Texas highlighting Carson County.svg 250 1876 Panhandle 924 923 1 0.10% 2010 6182 3 250 www.co.carson.tx.us ] Carson County is a county located in the U.S. state of Texas. It is part of the Amarillo metropolitan area. As of the 2010 census, its population was 6,182. [ United States Census Bureau 2010 Census Data http://2010.census.gov/2010census/data/ United States Census Bureau 3 January 2012 ] Carson is named for Samuel Price Carson, the first secretary of state of the Republic of Texas. The seat of the county is Panhandle [ 6 ]. It was founded in 1876 [ Geography ] According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the county has a total area of [ 924 sqmi 1 ], of which [ 923 sqmi 1 ] is land and [ 1 sqmi 1 ] (0.10%) is water. There were 2,470 households out of which 35.80% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 65.30% were married couples living together, 8.10% had a female householder with no husband present, and 23.70% were non-families. 22.30% of all households were made up of individuals and 11.30% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. In the county, the population was spread out with 27.90% under the age of 18, 6.20% from 18 to 24, 26.30% from 25 to 44, 23.90% from 45 to 64, and 15.70% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 39 years. For every 100 females there were 95.80 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 92.20 males. The median income for a household in the county was $40,285, and the median income for a family was $47,147. Males had a median income of $34,271 versus $23,325 for females. The per capita income for the county was $19,368. About 5.40% of families and 7.30% of the population were below the poverty line, including 8.90% of those under age 18 and 9.40% of those age 65 or over. [ History ] [ Native Americans ] Prehistoric hunter-gatherer were the first inhabitants, followed by the Plains Apache. Modern Apache tribes followed them and were displaced by Comanches. The Comanches were defeated by the United States Army in the Red River War of 1874. [ Abbe Donald R Carson County, Texas http://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/online/articles/hcc06 Handbook of Texas Online Texas State Historical Association 16 December 2010 ] [ Early Explorations ] Spanish conquistador Francisco Vázquez de Coronado explored the Llano Estacado in 1541. [ Lourie Peter On the Texas Trail of Cabeza De Vaca 2008 Boyds Mills Press 978-1-59078-492-1 46 ] [ Donoghue David Francisco Vázquez de Coronado http://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/online/articles/fvawt Handbook of Texas Online Texas State Historical Association 16 December 2010 ] [ County Established and Growth ] Carson County was established in 1876 from Bexar County. The county was organized in 1888. Panhandle, the only town at the time, became the county seat. [ Panhandle, Texas http://www.texasescapes.com/TexasPanhandleTowns/PanhandleTexas.htm Texas Escapes Texas Escapes - Blueprints For Travel, LLC 16 December 2010 ] Ranching began to be established in the county in the 1880s. The JA Ranch encompassed over a million acres (4,000  km²) within six adjoining counties. Richard E. McNalty established the Turkey Track Ranch in 1878. [ Anderson H Allen Turkey Track Ranch http://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/online/articles/apt08 Handbook of Texas Online Texas State Historical Association 16 December 2010 ] One of the early failed attempts came in 1882 when Charles G. Francklyn purchased [ 637440 acre km2 ] of railroad lands in adjoining counties to form the Francklyn Land and Cattle Company. The lands were later sold to the White Deer Lands Trust of British bondholders in 1886 and 1887. [ Anderson H. Allen Francklyn Land and Cattle Company http://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/online/articles/dsf02 Handbook of Texas Online Texas State Historical Association 16 December 2010 ] [ Duncan Ranch History http://www.cattlenet.com/history.htm The Duncan Ranch 16 December 2010 ] Railroads began to reach the county by 1886 when the Atchison, Topeka and Santa Fe Railway subsidiary Southern Kansas Railway extended the line into Texas, making Panhandle City a railhead in 1888. In 1889, the Fort Worth and Denver Railway linked Panhandle City with Washburn in Armstrong County. In 1904 the Chicago, Rock Island and Gulf bought the line. In 1908 the Southern Kansas of Texas extended its line from Panhandle City to Amarillo, thus making the Kansas-Texas-New Mexico line a major transcontinental route. The Choctaw, Oklahoma and Texas Railroad built across the southern edge of the county. [ The Atchison, Topeka and Santa Fe Railway, Santa Fe All The Way! http://www.american-rails.com/atchison-topeka-and-santa-fe.html American-Rails.com 16 December 2010 ] [ The Chicago, Rock Island and Pacific Railroad, Route of the Rockets! http://www.american-rails.com/chicago-rock-island-and-pacific.html American-Rails.com 16 December 2010 ] Pumping underground water with windmills resolved the issue of bringing water from Roberts County via the railroad. [ Windmills http://www.texasescapes.com/ClayCoppedge/Windmills.htm Texas Escapes Texas Escapes - Blueprints For Travel, LLC 16 December 2010 ] White Deer in 1909 became home to Polish Catholic immigrants, who had first settled Panna Maria in Karnes County before migrating to Carson County. [ White Deer, Texas http://www.texasescapes.com/TexasPanhandleTowns/WhiteDeerTexas.htm Texas Escapes Texas Escapes - Blueprints For Travel, LLC 16 December 2010 ] [ Panna Maria, Texas http://www.texasescapes.com/TexasGhostTowns/PannaMariaTexas/PannaMariaTx.htm Texas Escapes Texas Escapes - Blueprints For Travel, LLC 16 December 2010 ] Experimental drilling by Gulf Oil Corporation led to the county&apos;s, and the Panhandle&apos;s, first oil and gas production in late 1921. Borger field was discovered in 1925, sparking much oil exploration and production of the Panhandle area. By the end of 2000 more than [ 178398900 oilbbl ] of petroleum had been produced from county lands. [ Warner C A Texas Oil &amp; Gas Since 1543 2007 Copano Bay Press 978-0-9767799-5-7 Thompson, Ernest O 256 ] [ Borger, Texas http://www.texasescapes.com/TOWNS/Borger/borger.htm Texas Escapes Texas Escapes - Blueprints For Travel, LLC 16 December 2010 ] In September 1942 the Pantex Ordnance Plant was built on [ 16076 acre km2 ] of southwestern Carson County land, to pack and load shells and bombs in support of the World War II effort. Operations ceased August 1945, and in 1949 the site was sold to Texas Tech University at Amarillo for agricultural experimentation. Pantex reopened in 1951 as a nuclear weapons assembly plant. In 1960, Pantex began high explosives development in support of the Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory in California. Pantex has a long-term mission to safely and securely maintain the nation’s nuclear weapons stockpile and dismantle weapons retired by the military.
