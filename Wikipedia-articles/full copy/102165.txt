Torndirrup National Park has many impressive rock formations on the coast. These include the Gap, Natural Bridge and the Blowholes all shaped from the local granite. The park is along the coast on the west side of King George Sound and consists of a range of cliffs, gullies, blowholes, beaches and promontories. [ History ] The area is composed of three major rock type, one of these being gneiss. The oldest of these was formed 1300-1600 million years ago. This rock type can be seen along the cliff walls of the Gap. The granite s were formed later as the Australian Plate collided with the Antarctic Plate 1160 million years ago as molten rock rose to the surface. These granites are visible in the tor s atop Stony Hill. The park was gazetted in 1918, one of the first in Western Australia. It was later named in 1969 taking the name Indigenous Australian clan that lived in the area. The first ranger was appointed in 1973. [ http://www.australianexplorer.com/torndirrup_national_park.htm Australian Explorer - Torndirrup National Park 2009 2010-05-16 ] [ Flora and fauna ] A large array of floral species can be found within the park including the Woolly-bush, peppermint tree, Swamp Yate, various Banksia s and Karri forest. Coastal plants such as native rosemary, banjine and thick leafed fanflower are found in the heath. The park is also home to the very rare Albany Woolly-bush [ http://www.environment.gov.au/cgi-bin/sprat/public/publicspecies.pl?taxon_id%3D8811 Department of Environment - Species profile and threats database 2010 2010-05-16 ] and the critically endangered Blue Tinsel Lily of which only a single population exists. [ http://www.environment.gov.au/cgi-bin/sprat/public/publicspecies.pl?taxon_id%3D7669 Department of Environment - Species profile and threats database 2010 2010-05-16 ] Fauna such as kangaroo s, bush rat s, pygmy possum s and short nosed bandicoot s are found within the park. Many reptile species are also to be found including Tiger snake s, bardick, children&apos;s python and Dugite s. In 1876 the rare Dibbler was found in the park. [ http://www.dec.wa.gov.au/component/option%2Ccom_hotproperty/task%2Cview/id%2C103/Itemid%2C755/ Parkfinder - Torndirrup National Park 2009 2010-05-16 ] Birdlife includes honeyeater s, wattlebird s, Button Quail, Western Rosella and a variety of sea birds. [ http://www.pleasetakemeto.com/australia/torndirrup-national-park/information Peasetakemeto - Torndirrup National Park 2009 2010-05-16 ] Whale s and seal s can be seen from the cliffs of the park in the correct season. The rare and ancient Main&apos;s assassin spider, currently listed as threatened, was found to inhabit the park during a survey conducted in 2008. Access to the park is via Frenchmans Bay Road which is sealed and well sign posted. Access to most features is via sealed roads. No other facilities exist within the park but barbecues, tables, shops and toilets can be found nearby. The park has numerous walks mostly of a distance of less than [ 1500 m ft 0 ], including the Jimmy Newhills walk and the Stony Hill Heritage Trail walk. The longest trail is the [ 10 km on ] Bald Head Walk along Flinders peninsula finishing at Bald Head at the eastern edge of the park.
