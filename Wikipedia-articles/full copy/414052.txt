[ 1936 2 18 ] { Chicago, } { Illinois, } { USA } { Novelist } { American } { University of Portland (MBA &apos;76) } { 1980-present } { Pre-historical } { fiction } { Earth&apos;s Children series } { Ray Bernard Auel } { 5 } Jean Marie Auel ( [ icon ˈ dʒ iː n _ _ m ə ˈ r iː _ ˈ aʊ l ]; born February 18, 1936) is an American writer. She is best known for her Earth&apos;s Children books, a series of novels set in prehistoric Europe that explores interactions of Cro-Magnon people with Neanderthals. As of 2010 her books have sold over 45 million copies worldwide. { Publisher&apos;s Weekly } [ Early years ] Born Jean Marie Untinen on February 18, 1936 in Chicago, Illinois, [ http://www.bluebook.state.or.us/notable/notauel.htm Notable Oregonians: Jean Auel - Writer Oregon Blue Book January 2009 2010-05-03 ] she is of Finnish descent, the second of five children of Neil Solomon Untinen, a housepainter, and Martha Wirtanen. Auel attended Portland State University. [ May 2010 ] While a student, she joined Mensa, [ They&apos;re Accomplished, They&apos;re Famous, and They&apos;re Mensans Mensa Bulletin American Mensa ISSN 0025-9543 2004 July 476 27 ] and worked at Tektronix, as a clerk (1965–66), a circuit board designer (1966–73), technical writer (1973–74), and a credit manager (1974–76). [ May 2010 ] At one time, she shared a secretary with the author Ursula K. Le Guin. [ February 2007 ] Auel earned a master of business administration from the University of Portland in 1976. [ http://www.up.edu/alumni/default.aspx?cid%3D7480%26amp%3Bpid%3D2850 Alumni: Distinguished Alumni Awards University of Portland 2010-05=03 ] She has also been awarded honorary degrees from her alma mater, as well as the University of Maine and the Mount Vernon College for Women. [ May 2010 ] [ Career as novelist ] In 1977, Auel began extensive library research of the Ice Age for her first book. She joined a survival class to learn how to construct an ice cave, and learned primitive methods of making fire, tanning leather, and knapping stone from the aboriginal skills expert Jim Riggs. { The Valley of Horses - Acknowledgements } The Clan of the Cave Bear was nominated for numerous literary awards, including an American Booksellers Association nomination for best first novel. { Jean M. Auel :: Author Q &amp; A from } { Random House } It was also later adapted into a screenplay for the film of the same name. After the sales success of her first book, Auel has been able to travel to the sites of prehistoric ruins and relics, and also to meet many of the experts with whom she had been corresponding. Her research has taken her across Europe from France to Ukraine, including most of what Marija Gimbutas called Old Europe. In 1986 she attended and co-sponsored a conference on modern human origins at the School of American Research, Santa Fe. { Stringer, Christopher &amp; Gamble, Clive In Search of the Neanderthals plate 96 (1993, Thames and Hudson, London) ISBN 0-500-27807-5 } She has developed a close friendship with Dr. Jean Clottes of France who was responsible for, among many other things, the exploration of the Cosquer Cave discovered in 1985 and the Chauvet Cave discovered in 1994. { Jean M. Auel :: Video Interviews from } { Random House } { &quot; An Evening With Jean Auel &quot; from donsmaps.com } In October 2008, Auel was named an Officer of the Ordre des Arts et des Lettres by the French Minister of Culture and Communication.
