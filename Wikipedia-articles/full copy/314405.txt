[ 2000 ] { –present } { Interscope, } { Fiction, } { Modular, } { Polydor } { Native Korean Rock, } { Head Wound City } { Karen O } { Nick Zinner } { Brian Chase } Yeah Yeah Yeahs are an American indie rock band formed in New York City in 2000. The group is composed of vocalist and pianist Karen O, guitarist and keyboardist Nick Zinner, and drummer Brian Chase. [ Phares Heather http://www.allmusic.com/artist/yeah-yeah-yeahs-p522831/biography Yeah Yeah Yeahs AllMusic 2002-05-17 2011-10-09 ] They are complemented in live performances by second guitarist David Pajo, who joined as a touring member in 2009 and replaced Imaad Wasif who had previously held this role. According to an interview that aired during the ABC network&apos;s Live from Central Park SummerStage series, the band&apos;s name was taken from modern New York City vernacular. The band has recorded three studio albums; the first, Fever to Tell, was released in 2003. The second, Show Your Bones, was released in 2006 and was named the second best album of the year by NME. Their third studio album, It&apos;s Blitz!, was released on 31 March 2009 in the US and on 6 April 2009 in the rest of the world. [ History ] [ Formation (2000–2002) ] Karen O and Brian Chase first met as students at Oberlin College in Ohio in the late &apos;90s, where Chase was a jazz student at the conservatory. Karen then transferred to New York University, and while in New York met Zinner in a local bar, where they formed an &quot; instant connection &quot;. During this time they also shared a loft in Manhattan with future members of the band Metric. [ http://ladygunn.com/music/emily-haines-of-metric-interview YYYs shared a loft with Metric Ladygunn.com 2011-10-09 ] The two formed an acoustic duo called &quot; Unitard &quot;, but soon decided to &quot; shake things up a bit &quot; by forming a &quot; trashy, punky, grimy &quot; band modeled after the art student, avant-punk bands Karen O. was exposed to at Oberlin. [ http://www.guardian.co.uk/music/2006/feb/26/popandrock.yeahyeahyeahs Lynsey Hansley talks to Yeah Yeah Yeahs Lynsey Hanley February 26, 2006 The Guardian 2009-04-15 ] After the drummer they recruited initially bowed out, Chase joined the line-up. The band wrote a slew of songs at their first rehearsal and soon wound up supporting The Strokes and The White Stripes, earning a significant buzz for their arty and garage punk scene. In late 2001, the Yeah Yeah Yeahs released their self-titled debut EP, which they recorded with Boss Hog &apos;s Jerry Teel, on their own Shifty label. [ http://www.discogs.com/artist/Jerry%2BTeel Jerry Teel Discogs 2009-04-15 ] Early the next year the band stepped into the international spotlight, appearing at South by Southwest, touring the U.S. with Girls Against Boys and Europe with Jon Spencer Blues Explosion, and headlining their own U.K. tour. Wichita Recordings distributed the group&apos;s EP in the U.K. and Touch and Go reissued it in the States. [ http://www.mtv.com/music/artist/yeah_yeah_yeahs/artist.jhtml Yeah Yeah Yeahs mtv.com MTV 2009-04-15 ] [ Fever To Tell (2003–2004) ] In 2003, the band released their debut album, Fever to Tell, which received several strong critical reviews and sold more than 750,000 copies worldwide. The album&apos;s third single, &quot; Maps &quot;, received significant airplay on alternative radio. In 2010, Rolling Stone ranked &quot; Maps &quot; as 386th in their list of the 500 Greatest Songs of All Time. The video for their 2004 single &quot; Y Control &quot; was directed by Spike Jonze. In October 2004, the band released their first DVD, Tell Me What Rockers to Swallow. The DVD included a concert filmed at The Fillmore in San Francisco, all of the band&apos;s music videos to date, and various interviews. In November 2009, the NME rated Fever to Tell the #5 Best Album of the Decade. [ http://www.nme.com/news/the-strokes/48412 The Strokes&apos; &apos;Is This It&apos; tops NME albums of the decade list 2009-11-17 November 17, 2009 NME News NME ] [ Show Your Bones (2005-2006) ] Karen O live at the Tim Festival in 2006 Their second album, Show Your Bones, was released on 27–28 March 2006. Karen O told online zine Drowned in Sound, &quot; Show Your Bones is what happens when you put your finger in a light socket, &quot; crediting &quot; 9 year old antigenius wonder-kid Drake Barrett for the insight. &quot; [ Colin Roberts http://drownedinsound.com/news/562122 Yeah Yeah Yeahs LP and single details, yeah Drowned In Sound January 11, 2006 2008-11-19 ] The first single from the album, &quot; Gold Lion &quot;, was released on 20 March 2006, reaching number 18 in the Official UK Singles Chart. It has been noted by Leah Greenblatt that &quot; Gold Lion &quot; sounds startlingly similar to &quot; No New Tale To Tell &quot; from 1980s alternative band Love and Rockets. [ Greenblatt Leah http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0%2C%2C1204700%2C00.html Did The Red Hot Chili Peppers copy Tom Petty? Entertainment Weekly June 16, 2006 883 2008-11-19 ] The band toured throughout Europe and the United States during much of 2006, and also helped to curate an edition of the British All Tomorrow&apos;s Parties festival. In December 2006, the album was named the second best album of the year by NME magazine, as well as &quot; Cheated Hearts &quot; being voted the 10th best song. Rolling Stone magazine named it the 44th best album of 2006, while Spin magazine ranked it number 31 on their 40 best albums of 2006. [ Is Is EP (2007) ] Yeah Yeah Yeahs&apos; third EP, titled Is Is, was released on 24 July 2007. It includes 5 previously unreleased songs and a short film, which was recorded and filmed at the GlassLands Gallery in Brooklyn, NY. The songs were written in 2004, during the Fever To Tell tour, and performed live often. (2008–2009) ] The next album by the band was released in March 2009 and is titled It&apos;s Blitz!. [ http://pitchfork.com/news/34436-new-yeah-yeah-yeahs-album-iits-blitzi/ New Yeah Yeah Yeahs Album: It&apos;s Blitz January 21, 2009 Pitchfork Media Breihan, Tom 2009-04-15 ] The band says the album sounds different from their previous ones but &quot; still [sounds] like Yeah Yeah Yeahs &quot;. The album was originally set to be released 13 April, but following the leak to the Internet on 22 February [ http://diditleak.co.uk/?paged%3D2 diditleak.co.uk 2009-04-15 ] the band&apos;s label, Interscope, pulled the release date closer to reduce the impact of the online leak. [ http://www.nme.com/news/yeah-yeah-yeahs/43188 Yeah Yeah Yeahs to release album early due to leak March 3, 2009 NME 2009-04-15 ] The album spawned three singles: &quot; Zero &quot;, &quot; Heads Will Roll &quot; and &quot; Skeletons &quot;. It&apos;s Blitz! was named as the second best of 2009 by Spin Magazine and third best of 2009 by NME along with &quot; Zero &quot; from the album listed as the best track of the year by both. [ http://www.spin.com/gallery/40-best-albums-2009?page%3D39 The 40 Best Albums of 2009 December 7, 2009 Spin 17 December 2009 ] [ http://www.nme.com/list/50-best-albums-of-2009/159978/ 50 Best Albums of 2009 NME 13 December 2009 ] [ http://www.nme.com/list/50-best-tracks-of-2009/159979 50 Best Tracks of 2009 NME 13 December 2009 ] [ http://www.spin.com/articles/20-best-songs-2009 The 20 Best Songs of 2009 Spin Staff December 7, 2009 Spin 17 December 2009 ] [ Music style ] Their style has been described as &quot; an art-rock trio who made an edgy post-punk, dancefloor-friendly racket that mixed up Blondie with Siouxsie and the Banshees. &quot; [ http://entertainment.timesonline.co.uk/tol/arts_and_entertainment/music/article6485717.ece Yeah Yeah Yeahs: why fans of the art-punk trio can&apos;t say no McLean Craig June 13, 2009 Times Online 2 January 2010 ] [ Touring ] Alongside performing headlining tours across the world, the band has supported artists such as Björk, The White Stripes, The Strokes, Liars, and Jon Spencer Blues Explosion. The band became one of the first western rock acts to play in China when they headlined the Modern Sky Festival in Beijing in 2007.
