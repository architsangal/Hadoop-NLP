[ Heidekreis Lower Saxony Bad Fallingbostel 1873.5 03358 142,200 2003 76 HK (until 31.7.2011: SFA) Landkreis Soltau-Fallingbostel CoA.svg Lower_Saxony_HK.svg Manfred Ostermann Locator map SFA in Germany.svg DE938 23 Vogteistraße 19 29683 Bad Fallingbostel independent ] Heidekreis ( &quot; Heath district &quot; ) is a district (Landkreis) in Lower Saxony, Germany. It is bounded by (from the north and clockwise) the districts of Harburg, Lüneburg, Uelzen, Celle, Hanover, Nienburg, Verden and Rotenburg. [ History ] Historically the region belonged to the Duchy of Brunswick-Lüneburg and its successor state s. The district was established in 1977 by merging the former districts of Soltau and Fallingbostel as Soltau-Fallingbostel ( [ ˈzɔltaʊ falɪŋˈbɔstəl ] ). On 1 August 2011 it was renamed to Heidekreis. [ Geography ] The district includes the western half of the Lüneburg Heath (Lüneburger Heide). Since this landscape is so characteristic for the district, it calls itself &quot; the Heath District &quot;. The capital is Bad Fallingbostel, although it has only 11,800 inhabitants and is only the fifth largest town in the district.
