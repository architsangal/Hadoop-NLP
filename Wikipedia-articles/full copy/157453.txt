Known for his passing ability and free spirit, Longley drew comparisons to fellow redhead Bill Walton upon his entry into the NBA. [ Biography ] Luc Longley was born 19 January 1969 [ https://secure.ausport.gov.au/__data/assets/pdf_file/0003/338412/wais_annual_report_2007-2008.pdf pdf Hall of Champions 2007/2008 WAIS Yearbook and Annual Report Western Australian Institute of Sport 2008 19 April 2010 ] in Melbourne, Victoria. At age sixteen Luc was a member of the Australian Under-19 side and the following year, 1986, he joined the Perth Wildcats, with whom he played two games. [ http://thewildcatsjungle.tripod.com/id2.html Wildcats Honour Roll Perth Wildcats 19 April 2010 ] Longley was recruited out of Scotch College, Perth by the University of New Mexico &apos;s basketball coach, Gary Colson, who went to Perth to recruit Andrew Vlahov, who attended Stanford University. [ http://sportsillustrated.cnn.com/vault/article/magazine/MAG1136016/2/index.htm Cool Hand Luc Sports Illustrated Kirkpatrick Curry 24 December 1990 20 April 2010 ] He attended college at the University of New Mexico, between 1987–1991, where he averaged 19.1 points, 9.2 rebounds and 3.6 assists in his senior year. [ http://www.golobos.com/sports/m-baskbl/spec-rel/010810aaa.html Ex-Lobo Luc Longley Returns To His College Roots Official Athletics site of The University of New Mexico University of New Mexico 7 January 2010 19 April 2010 ] At nineteen he was a member of the national team for the Seoul Olympics, where they came fourth, the best result an Australian senior men&apos;s basketball team has achieved in Olympic competition. Longley was drafted 7th overall by the Minnesota Timberwolves in 1991. In 1992 he again represented Australia at the Barcelona Olympics. After two plus mediocre seasons with the struggling franchise, the 7&apos;2 &quot; (218  cm) center was traded to the Chicago Bulls for Stacey King late in the 1993-94 season. He won three straight championships with the Bulls from 1996 to 1998 as a teammate of Michael Jordan, Scottie Pippen, Toni Kukoč and Dennis Rodman. Following the breakup of the championship Bulls in 1998, Chicago did a sign-and-trade deal with Longley, sending him to the Phoenix Suns [ http://articles.chicagotribune.com/1999-02-09/sports/9902090028_1_luc-longley-bulls-summers Longley&apos;s Exit Takes Him To A New Place With The Suns Chicago Tribune Smith Sam 9 February 1999 20 April 2010 ] for Mark Bryant, Martin Müürsepp, Bubba Wells, and a conditional first-round draft pick. [ http://www.nytimes.com/1999/01/24/sports/plus-pro-basketball-phoenix.html Phoenix 24 January 1999 New York Times 20 April 2010 ] Longley spent two lackluster seasons with Phoenix, where he gained less attention for his play than for being stung twice by a scorpion while sitting on the floor of his home sorting through his CD collection. &lt; sup &gt; 1 &lt; /sup &gt; (Longley also had difficulties with bodysurfing - he managed to separate his shoulder and miss 18 games in 1997. ) He was traded to the New York Knicks prior to the 2000–01 NBA season in what was only the second four-team trade in NBA history. The Suns acquired Chris Dudley as part of the deal together with a first-round draft pick from New York and an undisclosed amount of cash, while New York received Longley, Glen Rice, Travis Knight, Vladimir Stepania, Lazaro Borrell, Vernon Maxwell, two first-round draft picks (from the Los Angeles Lakers and the Seattle SuperSonics ) and two second-round draft picks from Seattle. Seattle received Patrick Ewing and the Lakers received Horace Grant, Greg Foster, Chuck Person and Emanual Davis. [ http://www.nytimes.com/2000/09/21/sports/knicks-send-ewing-to-sonics-as-4-team-deal-ends-an-era.html Knicks Send Ewing to Sonics As 4-Team Deal Ends an Era New York Times Broussard Chris 21 September 2000 20 April 2010 ] Longley spent one year with New York before retiring, due to a degenerative condition in his left ankle. [ http://www.nytimes.com/2001/07/13/sports/pro-basketball-longley-has-not-yet-made-it-official-but-it-looks-like-end-line.html Longley has not made it official, but it looks like the end of the line New York Times Broussard Chris 13 July 2001 20 April 2010 ] [ http://www.nytimes.com/2001/09/26/sports/pro-basketball-knicks-cut-longley-and-pick-up-burrell.html Knicks cut Longley and pick up Burrell 26 September 2001 New York Times 20 April 2010 ] Longley averaged 7.20 points and 4.90 rebounds per game in his 567 regular season game NBA career. Longley played more NBA games than any other Australian.
