[ Grammy Award for Best Reggae Album quality reggae albums Grammy.jpg 100px A gold gramophone trophy with a plaque set on a table Gilded gramophone trophy presented to Grammy Award winners National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences United States 1985 2012 ] The Grammy Award for Best Reggae Album is an award presented at the Grammy Award s, a ceremony that was established in 1958 and originally called the Gramophone Awards, [ http://theenvelope.latimes.com/awards/grammys/env-grammy_awards_info%2C0%2C5279018.htmlstory?track%3Dcenter Grammy Awards at a Glance Los Angeles Times Tribune Company April 24, 2010 ] to recording artists for quality works in the reggae music genre. Honors in several categories are presented at the ceremony annually by the National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences of the United States to &quot; honor artistic achievement, technical proficiency and overall excellence in the recording industry, without regard to album sales or chart position &quot;. [ http://www2.grammy.com/Recording_Academy/ Overview April 24, 2010 National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences ] Originally called the Grammy Award for Best Reggae Recording, the honor was presented to artists for eligible songs or albums. The Jamaican group Black Uhuru received the first award in 1985. Beginning with the 1992 ceremony, the name of the award was changed to Best Reggae Album. Starting in 2002, awards were often presented to the engineers, mixers, and/or producers in addition to the performing artists. According to the category description guide for the 52nd Grammy Awards, eligible works are vocal or instrumental reggae albums &quot; containing at least 51% playing time of newly recorded music &quot;, including roots reggae, dancehall and ska music. [ http://www2.grammy.com/PDFs/Recording_Academy/52guide.pdf 52nd OEP Category Description Guide November 16, 2010 5 PDF National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences ] Stephen Marley holds the record for the most wins in this category, with six wins total (three times as a member of the band Ziggy Marley and the Melody Makers ). Similarly, Ziggy Marley has been presented the award four times total, three times as the leader of his eponymous band. Bunny Wailer has received the award three times, and two-time recipients include Burning Spear, Damian Marley, and Shabba Ranks. Jamaican artists have been presented with the award more than any other nationality. Buju Banton &apos;s nomination for the 2010 award sparked controversy and protest from the Gay &amp; Lesbian Alliance Against Defamation due to homophobic lyrics within his music. [ 2010 controversy ] { Buju Banton, performing in 2007 } Buju Banton &apos;s (real name Mark Anthony Myrie) nomination for the 2010 award sparked controversy and protest due to homophobic lyrics within his music. [ http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/music_blog/2010/01/buju-bantons-grammy-nomination-angers-gay-rights-groups.html Buju Banton&apos;s Grammy nomination angers gay rights groups Los Angeles Times Tribune Company January 29, 2010 November 27, 2010 August Brown ] Banton&apos;s most controversial song, released in 1988, is &quot; Boom, Bye Bye &quot;, which &quot; promote[s] the murder of gay men by shooting or burning &quot;. [ http://www.nydailynews.com/gossip/2009/12/14/2009-12-14_reggae_big_in_coke_bust.html Grammy-nominated Jamaican reggae star Buju Banton faces 20 years after 11-pound cocaine bust Jane H. Furse December 14, 2009 November 27, 2010 Daily News New York City, New York Mortimer Zuckerman ] Following the artist&apos;s nomination, the Gay &amp; Lesbian Alliance Against Defamation and the Los Angeles Gay and Lesbian Center placed an advertisement in the Daily Variety encouraging Grammy officials to denounce music that &quot; promotes or celebrates violence against any group of people &quot;. The advertisement, which took the form of a letter signed by gay rights and civil rights activists, asserted that honoring Banton was awarding &quot; extraordinary hateful work &quot;. The National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences responded by insisting that artists are honored for quality music &quot; regardless of politics &quot;. Banton has been quoted as saying that he sees &quot; no end to the war &quot; between himself and gay men. [ http://www.advocate.com/News/Daily_News/2010/01/31/Grammy_Doesnt_Honor_Buju_Banton/ Grammy Doesn&apos;t Honor Buju Banton January 31, 2010 November 27, 2010 The Advocate Here Media ] The 2010 award was presented to Stephen Marley. Banton was nominated in 2011 for the album Before the Dawn. Other reggae musicians that have been accused of promoting anti-gay lyrics include Beenie Man, Elephant Man, Vybz Kartel, Shabba Ranks and Sizzla.
