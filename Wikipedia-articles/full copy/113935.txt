It is a suburb of Iowa City and part of the Iowa City Metropolitan Statistical Area. As of the 2010 census, the city population was 13,374. A special census taken by the city in 2004 counted 7,224 residents, [ United States Census Bureau Special Census Certified Counts for Governmental Units in Iowa (2004) http://www.census.gov/field/www/specialcensus/files/iowa_2004.htm 2007-10-25 ] [ September 2010 H3llBot ] while the Census Bureau &apos;s estimated 2007 population for North Liberty was 10,982. From 2000 to 2010, North Liberty was the second-fastest growing town in Iowa. [ History ] The North Liberty area was first settled in 1838 by John Gaylor and Alonzo C. Dennison. It was originally known as &quot; Big Bottom &quot; or &quot; North Bend &quot; (in reference to its location near the bend of the Iowa River ) by its earliest settlers and was later known as &quot; Squash Bend &quot; before the city was platted as North Liberty in 1857. [ City of North Liberty North Liberty, Iowa http://www.northlibertyiowa.org/econdev/timeline-small.pdf 2007-10-25 PDF http://web.archive.org/web/20071108184855/http%3A//www.northlibertyiowa.org/econdev/timeline-small.pdf 2007-11-08 ] North Liberty incorporated on November 10, 1913, when its population was approximately 190. [ City of North Liberty Wastewater Department http://www.northlibertyiowa.org/city/wastewater.htm 2007-10-25 ] Population growth accelerated in the 1960s, and the Iowa City Community School District opened Penn Elementary there in 1961. A 1967 special census placed the city&apos;s population at 782. The population grew to over 2,000 by 1980, and in response in 1984 the North Liberty Community Library was founded and Penn Meadows Park, the city&apos;s largest, was opened. By 2000, the population of the city was over 5,000 and the city had established its own police and fire departments, and built a community center with library and aquatic park, as well as a city administration building. The city has since continued to grow, with a population of 7,224 in a 2004 special census. In 2007, the population was estimated to be 10,982, making North Liberty the second fastest growing city in Iowa. [ October 2008 ] [ Demographics ] { James Van Allen Elementary } [ http://factfinder.census.gov United States Census Bureau American FactFinder ] { and Iowa Data Center } As of the census [ 2 ] of 2010, there were 13,374 people, and 5,492 households residing in the city. The racial makeup of the city was 96.7% White, 0.5% African American, 0.2% Native American, 1.8% Asian, 0.0% Pacific Islander, 0.9% from other races, and 2.3% from two or more races. [ Infrastructure ] The sewage from the city is treated at a modern and sophisticated treatment plant using Membrane Bio-Reactor(MBR) technology followed by UV sterilization. It is designed to be able to discharge more than 2 million gallons of high quality effluent to Muddy Creek each day. [ VLBA Node ] The radio telescope located northeast of North Liberty ( [ 41 46 17.13 N 91 34 26.88 W ]; designation &quot; NL &quot; ) on Mehaffey Bridge Road is one of ten dishes comprising the Very Long Baseline Array (VLBA).
