[ Government ] The city is governed by a mayor-council form of government. The current mayor of Gonzales isBarney Arceneaux. [ Climate ] Gonzales has hot summers, mild winters, and warm springs and falls. The area experiences rain and severe weather all year long. Snowfall is rare for the city but it has snowed December 2004, December 2008, December 2009, and February 2010. Hurricanes also pose a huge threat to the area due to its close location to the coastline of southeast Louisiana. The last hurricane to strike the area was Hurricane Gustav of September 2008. Gustav brought 80+ winds to the area with gusts over 100  mph. [ History ] Early settlers of the area which would eventually become Gonzales were, for the most part, of Spanish and French ancestry, settling amidst the Houma Indians who lived in the area. By 1851, the small settlement had only ten residents. A school was established in 1855 by Adlard Gautreaux for the children in the area. By 1886, the settlement had grown to where the citizens of the community elected &quot; Big &quot; José Gonzáles as their sheriff. Joseph&apos;s son, &quot; Tee-Joe &quot; Gonzales, opened a general store and post office in 1887. Because the post office was known as the Gonzales Post Office, a few years later the Railroad Commission ordered the local railroad to change the name of the community from Edenborn to Gonzales to match the post office name. The epicenter of Gonzales, known as Chips Grocery, came into being in the late 70s. Previous epicenter was known as the &quot; Fish N Pic &quot;. Made famous by its clientlle of infamous hooligans. The lead Hooligan, who is now a &quot; respected &quot; retired teacher is best known for his acrobatic miscalculations in left field. Gonzales is now becoming famous for their police Chiefs infamous high speed chase through the town. Otherwise known as the &quot; Sherman Chase &quot; The railroad assisted in the growth of Gonzales. It was officially incorporated as a village in 1922 by then Louisiana Governor John Parker with Joseph Gonzales becoming the first mayor. The village continued to grow with the construction of Airline Highway, and Earl K. Long proclaimed Gonzales a town in 1952 during his term as governor. The first Jambalaya Festival was held in 1968, and Governor John J. McKeithen marked the occasion by proclaiming Gonzales the &quot; jambalaya capital of the world. &quot; Following the growth of industrial corporations in the area, the town finally became a city in 1977 following a proclamation by Governor Edwin Edwards. Gonzales became better known in 2005, when Hurricane Katrina struck. The HSUS and other animal rescue groups along with the National Guard made a fairground in Gonzales their main search and rescue headquarters, as well as the location of storing pets picked up from New Orleans and many other locations. Some people were reunited with their lost pets at this same location. [ National Guard ] Gonzales is home to the 922nd Horizontal Engineer Company which is part of the 769th Engineer Battalion headquartered in Baton Rouge, Louisiana. This company does &quot; horizontal &quot; construction missions which in military parlance means it is tasked with building and/ or fixing roads. Both these units belong to the 225th Engineer Brigade which is located at Pineville, Louisiana on Camp Beauregard.
