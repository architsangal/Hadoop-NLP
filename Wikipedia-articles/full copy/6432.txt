Its name means &quot; the chisel &quot; in Latin, and it was formerly known as Caelum Scalptorium, &quot; the engraver&apos;s chisel &quot;. { Star Tales – Caelum } It is the eighth smallest constellation, with an area just less than that of Corona Australis. [ Notable features ] [ List of stars in Caelum ] Caelum is a faint constellation, having no star brighter than fourth magnitude. Its brightest star is the magnitude 4.45 α Caeli. A small telescope splits γ Caeli into a magnitude 4.5 red giant and a magnitude 6.34 white giant. Larger telescopes are needed to see several NGC objects, all galaxies, but none brighter than magnitude 11.5 or larger than 3 arcminutes. { Caelum at SEDS.org } Most notable galaxies are NGC 1571, NGC 1679 and IC 2106. { Caelum } [ Namesakes ] USS Caelum (AK-106) was a United States Navy ship named after the constellation.
