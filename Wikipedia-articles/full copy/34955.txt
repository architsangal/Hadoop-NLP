[ Events ] [ January &amp; amp;ndash;March ] January 15 – The University of Notre Dame receives its charter from Indiana. February 27 – The Dominican Republic gains independence from Haiti. { February 28 : } { USS &apos;&apos;Princeton&apos;&apos; deaths. } February 28 – A gun on the USS &apos;&apos;Princeton&apos;&apos; explodes while the boat is on a Potomac River cruise, killing 2 United States Cabinet members and several others. March 8 – King Oscar I ascends to the throne of Sweden-Norway. March 12 – The Columbus and Xenia Railroad, the first railroad planned to be built in Ohio, is chartered. March 21 – The Bahá&apos;í calendar begins. March 23 – Edict of Toleration, allowing Jews to settle in the Holy Land. [ April &amp; amp;ndash;June ] April – The Fleet Prison for debtors in London is closed. May 1 – Hong Kong Police Force, the world&apos;s second, Asia&apos;s first modern police force is established. May 23 – Persian Prophet The Báb privately announces his revelation to Mullá Husayn, just after sunset, founding the Bábí Faith (later revolving to the Bahá&apos;í Faith as the Báb intended ) in Shiraz, Persia (now Iran). Contemporaneously, on this day in nearby Tehran, was the birth of `Abdu&apos;l-Bahá; the eldest Son of Bahá&apos;u&apos;lláh, Prophet-Founder of the Bahá&apos;í Faith, the inception of which, the Báb&apos;s proclaimed His own mission was to herald. `Abdu&apos;l-Bahá Himself was later proclaimed by Bahá&apos;u&apos;lláh to be His own successor, thus being the third &quot; central figure &quot; of the Bahá&apos;í Faith. May 24 – The first electrical telegram is sent over the telegraph by Samuel F. B. Morse from the U.S. Capitol in Washington, D.C. to the B &amp; O Railroad &quot; outer depot &quot; in Baltimore, Maryland, saying &quot; What hath God wrought &quot;. June –July – The Great Flood of 1844 hits the Missouri River and Mississippi River. June 6 – George Williams founds the Young Men&apos;s Christian Association ( YMCA ) in London. { June 15 : } { Charles Goodyear. } June 15 – Charles Goodyear receives a patent for vulcanization, a process to strengthen rubber. June 22 – Influential North American fraternity Delta Kappa Epsilon is founded at Yale University. June 27 – Joseph Smith, Jr., founder of the Latter Day Saint movement, and his brother Hyrum, are killed in Carthage Jail, Carthage, Illinois. John Taylor, future president of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints is severely injured but survives. [ July &amp; amp;ndash;September ] { July 3 : } { Great Auk. } The United States signs The Sino-American Treaty of Wanghia with the Chinese Government (the first diplomatic agreement between China and the United States in history). November 3 – Giuseppe Verdi &apos;s I due Foscari debuts at Teatro Argentina, Rome. November 6 – The Dominican Republic drafts its first Constitution. December 4 – U.S. presidential election, 1844 : James K. Polk defeats Henry Clay. December 21 – The Rochdale Pioneers commence business at their cooperative in Rochdale, England. [ Date unknown ] Swedish chemistry professor Gustaf Erik Pasch invents the safety match. Carlos Antonio López becomes dictator of Paraguay. The anonymously written Vestiges of the Natural History of Creation is published and paves the way for the acceptance of Darwin &apos;s book The Origin of Species. The Free Church Institution is established by Reverend Alexander Duff in Calcutta, India. This is later merged with the General Assembly&apos;s Institution to form the Scottish Church College, one of the pioneering institutions that ushers in the Bengal Renaissance.
