[ Summers County West Virginia Summers County wv seal.png Map of West Virginia highlighting Summers County.png 225 1871 Hinton 368 361 7 1.78% 2010 13927 15 summerscountywv.org/ ] Summers County is a county located in the U.S. state of West Virginia. As of the 2010 census, the population was 13,927. Its county seat is Hinton [ 6 ]. Summers County was created by an act of the West Virginia General Assembly on February 27, 1871 from parts of Fayette, Greenbrier, Mercer and Monroe counties. It was named in honor of George W. Summers (1804-1868). [ Geography ] According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the county has a total area of 368  square miles (952  km²), of which 361  square miles (935  km²) is land and 7  square miles (17  km²) (1.78%) is water.
