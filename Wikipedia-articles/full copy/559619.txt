[ ACC Men&apos;s Basketball Player of the Year Atlantic Coast Conference logo.png The official logo of the Atlantic Coast Conference: The eastern coast of the United States is centered in a vertical oval with the initials &quot; ACC &quot; in the spot where the Atlantic Ocean would be just off the coast of Georgia. 125px the most outstanding male basketball player in the Atlantic Coast Conference Atlantic Coast Sports Media Association United States 1954 Tyler Zeller, North Carolina ] The Atlantic Coast Conference (ACC) Men&apos;s Basketball Player of the Year is a basketball award given to the men&apos;s basketball player in the Atlantic Coast Conference voted by members of the Atlantic Coast Sports Media Association as the most outstanding player. The award was first given following the 1953–54 season, the first year of the conference&apos;s existence, to Dickie Hemric of Wake Forest. [ Sumner Jim Looking Back... Dickie Hemric&apos;s Record-Setting Career http://www.theacc.com/sports/m-baskbl/spec-rel/020409aae.html 4 February 2009 theACC.com 2 September 2009 ] Two players have won the award three times: David Thompson of North Carolina State and Ralph Sampson of Virginia. [ Basketball in the ACC Porterfield Jason 2008 Rosen Publishing New York 978-1-4042-1380-7 6, 28–35 http://books.google.com/books?id%3Dl7ta0nJhEn4C%26amp%3Blpg%3DRA1-PA29%26amp%3Bdq%3D%22%20ACC%20Player%20of%20the%20year%22%26amp%3Bpg%3DPA1948#v%3Donepage%26amp%3Bq%3D%22%20ACC%20Player%20of%20the%20year%22%26amp%3Bf%3Dfalse 2 September 2009 ] Hemric, Len Chappell, Larry Miller, John Roche, Len Bias, Danny Ferry, Tim Duncan and J. J. Redick have won the award twice. There has been one tie in the award&apos;s history, which occurred at the end of the 2000–01  season, when Joseph Forte of North Carolina and Shane Battier of Duke shared the award. [ Battier, Forte Tie For ACC Player of The Year Award theACC.com 13 March 2001 http://www.theacc.com/sports/m-baskbl/spec-rel/031301aam.html 3 September 2009 ] Twelve players have received either the Naismith or Wooden National Player of the Year awards in the same year that they received an ACC Player of the Year award. [ Note 1 a a ] North Carolina&apos;s Tyler Hansbrough is the most recent player to achieve this; the consensus National Player of the Year in 2008, he won every major national award in addition to the conference Player of the Year award. Duke and North Carolina have the most ACC Players of the Year winners with 14 each. Each of the original 1953  ACC members have had at least one of its students win the award. Florida State, Miami (FL), and Virginia Tech are the only schools without a winner. As of 2012, the award has been given 60  times in 59  seasons. Forty-seven different players from ten schools have received the award: thirty-three seniors, twenty-two juniors, six sophomores, and no freshmen.
