{ UK } { 9 June 1983 } { 7 June 2001 } { &apos;&apos;Constituency Established&apos;&apos; } { Iris Robinson } { Northern Ireland } { European } { 10 June 1979 } { 15 June 1989 } { Parliament Established } { Jim Nicholson } { South Tyrone } { Parliament of Northern Ireland Northern Ireland } { 25 November 1965 } { 30 March 1972 } { William Frederick McCoy } { Parliament Abolished } [ 1937 12 24 yes ] { British } { Ulster Unionist Party } { Mary Todd } { Queen&apos;s University Belfast } John David Taylor, Baron Kilclooney, PC (NI) (born 24 December 1937), is a former Ulster Unionist Party MP and a life peer. He was deputy leader of the UUP from 1995 to 2001, and a member of the Northern Ireland Assembly. [ Career and family ] Taylor was educated at The Royal School, Armagh, and The Queen&apos;s University of Belfast (B.Sc). He married Mary Todd in 1970 and has six children. Lord Kilclooney owns Alpha Newspapers which operates local newspaper titles in Northern Ireland and the Republic. Member of the Farmers Club, London and the County Club, Armagh City. Lord Kilclooney&apos;s political career began as MP for South Tyrone in the Northern Ireland House of Commons between 1965 and 1972 and served in the Cabinet as Minister of State at the Ministry of Home Affairs. In February 1972, he survived an assassination attempt by the Official Irish Republican Army. Two men, including Joe McCann (who was himself shot dead some months afterwards whilst evading arrest) raked his car with bullets, hitting Taylor five times in the head. Taylor survived, but needed extensive reconstructive surgery on his jaw. Despite this, Taylor soon re-entered politics. He represented Fermanagh &amp; South Tyrone in the short-lived Northern Ireland Assembly elected in 1973 and dissolved in 1974, following the collapse of the power-sharing Executive. He became a Member of the European Parliament for Northern Ireland in 1979, remaining an MEP until 1989. On 20 January 1987, [ http://cain.ulst.ac.uk/othelem/chron/ch87.htm A Chronology of the Conflict - 1987 1 June 2009 Conflict Archive on the Internet University of Ulster 7 March 2010 ] Taylor left the European Democrats, with whom the Conservatives sat, to join the controversial European Right group. [ John Taylor: Profile BBC News 30 January 2001 http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/northern_ireland/1144830.stm ] He was elected to the Northern Ireland Assembly in 1982 for North Down. He then became MP for Strangford in 1983, until 2001. He was also a member of Castlereagh Borough Council from 1993-1997. He appears on the list of speakers at the Annual Conference of the Conservative Monday Club &apos;s Young Members&apos; Group, at the United Oxford &amp; Cambridge Club in Pall Mall, on 18 November 1989, when he spoke on The Union and Northern Ireland. Following the 2001 General Election he was created a life peer as Baron Kilclooney of Armagh in the County of Armagh. He sat on the Northern Ireland Policing Board between 1998 and 2006. He continued to sit as a member of the Northern Ireland Assembly until his retirement prior to the elections in March 2007. He remains the only active politician to have participated in all levels of government in Northern Ireland, from local council, the Parliament of Northern Ireland, Westminster, Europe, all previous failed Assemblies and Conventions and the current incarnation of the Assembly. In January 2012, Taylor wrote to The Scotsman newspaper claiming that Scotland should be subject to partition, depending on the outcome of the Scottish independence referendum.
