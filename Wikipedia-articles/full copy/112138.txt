[ Government ] The government consists of a mayor and a city council. The mayor is elected in citywide vote. The city council consists of seven members. Five are elected from individual districts. Two are elected at-large. [ Politics ] Former Mayor Jim Acton ( Democratic Party ) did not file to run for a fifth term, which left the 2007 election open to new candidates. The last mayoral election occurred in November 2007. The candidates were: Republican John Lasley, President of the Lebanon City-Council, Democrat Roger Neal, Lebanon Community School Corporation School Board member and former Lebanon Parks and Recreation Director, and independent candidate George Piper who formerly was an editor at The Lebanon Reporter, which is Boone County&apos;s largest newspaper. Republican City Council President John Lasley won the election with 48% of the vote, to Democrat Roger Neal&apos;s 27% and Independent George Piper&apos;s 25%. { http://www.boonecounty.in.gov/Default.aspx?tabid=364 } 30% of registered voters cast votes in the 2007 election. { http://www.boonecounty.in.gov/Default.aspx?tabid=364 } Lasley died on May 2, 2009. He was battling a recurrence of cancer since December. City Council President Dick Robertson assumed the mayoral duties until the Republican Party precinct chairpersons met to choose Harold &quot; Huck &quot; Lewis as his successor. Despite having recently elected Democratic mayors, Lebanon can be considered to be highly Republican when it comes to national and state politics. In the 2008 election, Boone County (the county in which Lebanon is located) voted 62% for Republican presidential candidate John McCain and more than 80% for Republican gubernatorial candidate Mitch Daniels. { http://boonecounty.in.gov/LinkClick.aspx?fileticket=OOTk3gGdcZw%3d &amp; tabid=456 &amp; mid=1387 } [ Schools ] Lebanon Community School Corporation has six schools under its jurisdiction: four elementary schools, a middle school and a high school.
