[ April 2012 ] [ April 2012 ] [ yes 1963 1 7 ] { Coventry, England } { Classical } { Contemporary classical } { Electronic } { Alternative } { Musician, composer } { 1981–present } { Pop Will Eat Itself } [ single Clint Mansell Lux Aeterna.ogg Ogg Clint Mansell – &quot; Lux Æterna &quot; ] Clinton Darryl &quot; Clint &quot; Mansell, (born 7 January 1963) is an English musician, composer, and former lead singer and guitarist of the band Pop Will Eat Itself. After the disbanding of Pop Will Eat Itself in 1996, Mansell was introduced to film scoring when director Darren Aronofsky, hired him to score his debut film, π. { Pete Paphides Clint Mansell: pop ate itself, and out popped Hollywood } { The Times Retrieved 14 May 2010 } Mansell then wrote the score for the next Aronofsky film, Requiem for a Dream, which has been well received. { Requiem for a Dream – Clint Mansell } { Allmusic Retrieved 31 January 2011 } Its main composition &quot; Lux Aeterna &quot; has become extremely popular, appearing in a wide variety of advertisements and film trailer s. { Sundance 2009 Composers – Clint Mansell } { BMI Retrieved 31 January 2011 } Mansell&apos;s composition for The Fountain was nominated for Best Original Score at the 64th Annual Golden Globe Awards. His other notable film scores include Moon, Smokin&apos; Aces, The Wrestler, and Black Swan. [ Career ] [ Pop career ] Mansell was the lead singer and guitarist of Pop Will Eat Itself. When PWEI signed to Nothing Records, Mansell became close friends with Trent Reznor, { Clint Mansell: Aronofsky, Reznor and Me, Part One } and he performed backing vocals on the 1999 album The Fragile by Nine Inch Nails. [ Soundtrack composer ] Mansell&apos;s score for Darren Aronofsky &apos;s π was followed up by his score for Aronofsky&apos;s next film, Requiem for a Dream, which became a cult hit. Since their first collaboration in 1996, Mansell has composed the music to every Aronofsky film. { Bray, Elisa (28 January 2011) Duo Darren Aronofsky and Clint Mansell score on pointes } { The Independent Retrieved 31 January 2011 } Other notable achievements include the theme for the film The Hole, the music for the pilot episode of CSI: NY, and the score for Aronofsky&apos;s later films The Fountain, which was nominated for Best Original Score in the 64th Annual Golden Globe Awards, and The Wrestler. { 64th Golden Globe Awards nominations } Mansell has also contributed the score to HBO &apos;s Voyeur. His most recent work has been on the soundtrack for Black Swan in 2010. { McNulty, Bernadette (20 January 2011) Clint Mansell interview for Black Swan soundtrack } { The Telegraph } and the video game Mass Effect 3 [ Stephen Lambrechts http://xbox360.ign.com/articles/114/1148798p1.html Film Composer Clint Mansell to Score Mass Effect 3 9 February 2011 IGN.com 30 March 2011 ] [ These Are The People Composing Mass Effect 3&apos;s Soundtrack http://www.gameinformer.com/b/news/archive/2011/12/19/these-are-the-people-composing-mass-effect-3s-soundtrack.aspx GameInformer.com 19 December 2011 19 December 2011 ] [ Film trailers ] The trailer for The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers contained a rearranged version of the track &quot; Lux Æterna &quot;, using a full orchestra and choir. The piece, named &quot; Requiem for a Tower &quot;, was recorded especially for the trailer. The orchestration was arranged by Simone Benyacar, Dan Nielsen, and Veigar Margeirsson. { Requiem for a Tower Dream – Single } { iTunes } &quot; Lux Æterna &quot; has since become popular, with both the original and the &quot; Requiem for a Tower &quot; version having appeared in a wide variety of advertisements and trailers, including the trailer for the Red Sox – Yankees games in the 2007 Major League Baseball season, and trailers for the films Zathura, The Da Vinci Code, { The Da Vinci Code Trailer Retrieved 31 January 2011 } Sunshine, Babylon A.D. and the TV series Lost and Top Gear. It was also used on Sky Sports News and as the theme for Soccer Saturday from 2007–2009. In 2006, the theme was used in EuroSport LIVE trailers. The theme was also used by America&apos;s Got Talent as an introduction of the judges before being changed slightly. The new variant is now a regular piece on the show. The piece &quot; Death Is the Road to Awe &quot; from the score for The Fountain was featured in a trailer for the 2007 film I Am Legend, and the trailer for the film The Mist, as well as the trailer for the film Frost/Nixon, and toward the end of 2007 The Final Cut trailer for Blade Runner.
