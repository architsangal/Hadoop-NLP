It had coasts in the south and north and had dozens of towns and a few small cities which rank between the 20 and 40 largest of the island. [ Economy ] Much of the province&apos;s agriculture waes geared towards the production of food, primarily cattle, potatoes and fruit. Unlike much of Cuba, sugar and tobacco played only a small role in the province&apos;s economy. There was also much industrialization in the province, with numerous electricity plant s and sugar mills.
