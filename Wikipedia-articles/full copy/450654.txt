A town (町; chō or machi) is a local administrative unit in Japan. It is a local public body along with prefecture (&apos;&apos;ken&apos;&apos; or other equivalents), city (&apos;&apos;shi&apos;&apos;), and village (&apos;&apos;mura&apos;&apos;). Geographically, a town is contained within a prefecture. Note that the same word (町; machi or chō) is also used in names of smaller regions, usually a part of a ward in a city. This is a legacy of when smaller towns were formed on the outskirts of a city, only to eventually merge into it.
