[ History ] On January 28, 1915, President of Chile Ramón Barros Luco promulgated Law 2,977, [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D23639%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D1915-02-01 law 2,977 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] which arranged all the information insofar about holidays celebrated in Chile. It established or kept the following holidays: Sundays year-round. January 1 ( Feast of the Circumcision of Christ at the time; New Year in the late 20th and 21st centuries), June 29 ( Saint Peter and Saint Paul ), August 15 ( Assumption of Mary ), December 8 ( Immaculate Conception ), December 25 ( Christmas Day ), and the floating holidays of the Ascension of Jesus Christ and Corpus Christi. Fridays and Saturdays during Holy Week. September 18, Establishment of the First Government Junta in 1810. September 19 and May 21, in celebration of the glories of the Army and the Navy. The day of election of electors of the President of the Republic. The same law abolished, by omission, four religious holidays: Epiphany ( &quot; Adoración de los Santos Reyes &quot;, January 6), Carnival ( &quot; Carnaval &quot;, mobile), Annunciation ( &quot; Anunciación del Señor &quot;, march 25) and Nativity of the Virgin Mary ( &quot; Natividad de la Virgen &quot;, September 8). It also shortened the national independence holiday to two days (until 1914, they occupied September 18, 19 and 20). On November 14, 1921, President Arturo Alessandri Palma promulgated Law 3,810, [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D24262%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D1921-11-24 law 3,810 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] marking October 12 ( Columbus Day ) as holiday. On April 30, 1931, President Carlos Ibáñez del Campo promulgated the &quot; Decree Enforceable as Law &quot; ( &quot; Decreto con Fuerza de Ley &quot; ) 130, marking May 1 ( May Day ) as holiday. In 1932, Provisional President Bartolomé Blanche reinstated the September 20 holiday, { Decree-law 636, published in the 17/09/1932 edition of the Diario Oficial. } which existed until 1944. { Law 8.223, published in the 13/09/1945 edition of the Diario Oficial. } In 1968 the holidays of Saint Peter and Saint Paul, Ascension of Jesus Christ and Corpus Christi were eliminated by Article 144 of Law 16,840. [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D28681%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D1979-12-29 law 16,840 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] The Catholic Church agreed to celebrate them on the nearest Sunday. September 11 (the day of the military coup in 1973 ) was established as a holiday (Día de la Liberación Nacional, &quot; Day of National Liberation &quot; ) by the military regime in 1981. [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D29453%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D2002-03-06 law 18,026 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] In 1998 it was replaced by the Día de la Unidad Nacional ( &quot; Day of National Unity &quot; ), [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D127036%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D2002-03-06 law 19,588 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] to be observed the first Monday in September. In march 2002 the latter was abolished. [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D195243%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D2002-03-06 law 19,793 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] The holiday on June 29 ( Saint Peter and Saint Paul ) was reestablished in September 1985. [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D29840%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D1985-09-17 law 18,432 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] In 1987, during a visit by Pope John Paul II, Corpus Christi was reestablished [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D29998%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D1987-04-14 law 18,607 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] (although the Church continued to observe the holiday on the nearest Sunday [ http://www.jesus.cl/iglesia/paso_iglesia/documentos/conf/documentos_sini.ficha.php?mod%3Ddocumentos_sini%26amp%3Bid%3D370%26amp%3Bsw_volver%3Dyes%26amp%3Bdescripcion%3D Declaración sobre feriado de Corpus Christi (01/06/1987) Jesus.cl November 14, 2011 ] ). On March 10, 2000, Law 19,668 [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D160270%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D2007-01-06 law 19,668 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] moved the Saint Peter and Saint Paul, Columbus Day and Corpus Christi holidays to the preceding Monday, if they were to fall on a Tuesday, Wednesday or Thursday, or to the following Monday, if they were to fall on a Friday. Additionally, the designation of Columbus Day was changed from Aniversario del Descubrimiento de América to Día del Descubrimiento de Dos Mundos ( &quot; Discovery of Two Worlds&apos; Day &quot; ), an obvious corruption from the then-recent ubiquitous &quot; Encuentro de dos mundos &quot; ( &quot; The Meeting of Two Worlds &quot; ) slogan. Una perspectiva polaca. } On January 6, 2007 Law 20,148 replaced Corpus Christi with Our Lady of Mount Carmel, to be observed July 16, starting that year. On September 14, 2007 Law 20,215 declared holidays all September 17s falling on a Monday and all September 20s falling on a Friday. On October 11, 2008 Law 20,299 [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D279294%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D2008-10-11 law 20,299 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] established Reformation Day on October 31 as a national holiday, starting that year. This holiday is moved to the preceding Friday if it falls on a Tuesday, or to the following Friday if it falls on a Wednesday. Besides yearly holidays, since 1901 presidential elections are declared holidays, as mandated by successive versions of the General Elections law (law 18,700 [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D30082%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D2008-10-04 law 18,700 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] being the current one; see laws 1,464, [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D22671%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D1901-06-22 law 1,464 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] 1,752, [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D22892%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D1905-09-02 law 1,752 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] 2,977, decree-law 542, [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D6173%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D1925-09-23 decree-law 542 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] 6,250, [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D25329%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D1938-09-14 law 6,250 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] 6,834, 9,334 and 12,891). Since the late XX century, parliamentary and municipal elections are also public holidays (see law 18,700 ). Censuses, held every ten years, are also declared holidays since 1982; that year&apos;s census and 1992&apos;s were so due to ad-hoc laws; [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D29540%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D1982-04-16 law 18,116 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] [ http://www.leychile.cl/Navegar?idNorma%3D30483%26amp%3BidParte%3D%26amp%3BidVersion%3D1992-01-29 law 19,116 Leychile.cl November 14, 2011 ] censuses taken from 1992 onwards are declared holidays due to a reform in the Census law.
