[ 1947 ] { Wilmington, } { Delaware, U.S. } { Ed Rendell (1971–2011) } { Jesse } { University of Pennsylvania } { Georgetown University } { Villanova University } Marjorie ( &quot; Midge &quot; ) Osterlund Rendell (born 1947) is a federal judge on the United States Court of Appeals for the Third Circuit and a former First Lady of Pennsylvania. { &quot; Biography of Hon. Marjorie O. Rendell, &quot; Kimmel Center for the Performing Arts, last accessed Oct. 31, 2010 } In 2003, she was named to the PoliticsPA list of &quot; Pennsylvania&apos;s Most Politically Powerful Women &quot;. [ Pennsylvania&apos;s Most Politically Powerful Women PoliticsPA The Publius Group 2001 http://www.politicspa.com/features/mostpoliticallypowerfulwomen.htm http://web.archive.org/web/20040209095936/politicspa.com/features/mostpoliticallypowerfulwomen.htm 2004-02-09 ] [ Personal background ] Rendell was born in Wilmington, Delaware. { &quot; History of the Federal Judiciary: Rendell, Marjorie O., &quot; Federal Judicial Center, last accessed Oct. 31, 2010 } Her father was employed as a DuPont executive and she attended Ursuline Academy. She married Ed Rendell, the former Governor of Pennsylvania, in 1971. On January 21, 2003, Judge Rendell administered the oath of office to her husband after he won the gubernatorial election in November, 2002. During her husband&apos;s campaigns for mayor and governor, Rendell was barred by the federal judicial ethics code from publicly campaigning on his behalf, as well as from taking part in some fundraisers. Media reports, on February 7, 2011, that a joint email from the couple announced that they have amicably separated. { &quot; Ex-Gov. Rendell Splits With Wife After 40 Years Of Marriage, &quot; Philadelphia Daily News, February 7, 2011. } They have one adult son, Jesse. { &quot; Midge Rendell: In a class by herself, &quot; Daily Pennsylvanian, June 12, 2003 } [ Professional background ] [ Private practice ] She received a Bachelor of Arts degree, cum laude, from the University of Pennsylvania in 1969 and a Juris Doctor from Villanova University School of Law in 1973. Afterward, she practiced as an attorney for 20 years as a partner at the Philadelphia firm of Duane, Morris &amp; Heckscher, where she focused her practice on bankruptcy and commercial litigation. She also served as a mediator for the United States District Court. While in private practice, Judge Rendell experienced sexism originating from both her clients and cohorts. At times, she was called &quot; honey &quot; by her male colleagues and would hide or downplay the existence of her then young son. { &quot; Federal Judge Marjorie Rendell Tells Penn Law Women about the &apos;Dark Ages for Women in the Law&apos;, &quot; Penn Law News, June 3, 2009 } [ U.S. District Court ] In 1994, she was appointed by President Bill Clinton and confirmed by the Senate to serve as a judge for the United States District Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania. [ U.S. Court of Appeals ] In 1997, she was once again nominated by President Clinton and confirmed by the Senate, this time for the United States Court of Appeals for the Third Circuit where she continues to serve as an appellate judge today. The district hears cases from Pennsylvania, New Jersey, Delaware, and the Virgin Islands. [ http://www.post-gazette.com/election/20021110midge1110p5.asp Pittsburgh Post-Gazette John M.R. Bull Rendell&apos;s wife couldn&apos;t campaign because she&apos;s a federal judge November 10, 2002 ] In 2008, Judge Rendell served as a part of a three-judge panel that overturned the Federal Communications Commission&apos;s indecency fine against CBS related to Justin Timberlake and Janet Jackson&apos;s infamous 2004 Super Bowl &apos;wardrobe malfunction&apos;. { &quot; Court tosses FCC &apos;wardrobe malfunction&apos; fine, &quot; New York Daily News, July 21st 2008 } [ Philanthropy ] In 1993, Rendell founded and managed Avenue of the Arts, Inc., whose purpose was to develop Philadelphia&apos;s Broad Street into a world-class artistic venue. She currently serves as one of the members of the board of directors. { http://www.avenueofthearts.org/about_facts.asp } She is also a trustee of the University of Pennsylvania.
