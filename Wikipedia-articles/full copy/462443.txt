Dan Quayle (R) Robert Byrd (D) Jim Wright (D) (until June 6, 1989) Tom Foley (D) 435 100 5 Democratic Party Democratic Party 1st January 3, 1989 November 22, 1989 2nd January 23, 1990 October 28, 1990 100th 102nd ] The One Hundred First United States Congress was a meeting of the legislative branch of the United States federal government, composed of the United States Senate and the United States House of Representatives. It met in Washington, DC from January 3, 1989 to January 3, 1991, during the first two years of the administration of U.S. President George H. W. Bush. The apportionment of seats in this House of Representatives was based on the Twentieth Census of the United States in 1980. Both chambers had a Democratic majority.
