[ February 2012 ] [ 1958 1 26 ] { Sacramento, California, U.S. } { Democratic } { Los Angeles, California, U.S. } { Carolina Reyes } { Stanford Law School ( } { J.D. ) } { Stanford University (B.A.) } { Attorney Politician } { Roman Catholic } Xavier Becerra ( [ hɑːviˈɛər bɨˈsɛrə ] &quot; HAH-vee-air &quot;; born January 26, 1958) is the U.S. Representative for [ CA 31 ], serving since 2003. He is a member of the Democratic Party. The district is based in Los Angeles. [ Early life, education, and early career ] Becerra was born in Sacramento, California, the son of working-class immigrants. He graduated in 1976 from C.K. McClatchy High School located in central city Sacramento. He went on to earn his B.A. in economics from Stanford University and his J.D. from Stanford Law School. He was initially a lawyer, working on cases involving individuals with mental impairment. He was also a staff member for California State Senator Art Torres and the Deputy Attorney-General of California. Becerra served one-term as a member of the California State Assembly before successfully running for Congress. [ U.S. House of Representatives ] [ Elections ] 1992 In 1992, after redistricting, incumbent Democratic U.S. Congressman Matthew Martínez of California&apos;s 30th congressional district decided to run in newly redrawn 31st district. Becerra, a Freshman Assemblyman, decided to run for congress in the Los Angeles based district. He won the Democratic primary with a plurality of 32% of the vote. { http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=207769 } In the general election, he defeated Republican nominee Morry Waksberg 58%–24%. { http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=27863 } 1994–2010 In 1994, he won re-election to a second term with 66%, { http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=28733 } the lowest re-election winning percentage of his career. After, he never won re-election with less than 72%. After redistricting, he ran for the 31st district. 2012 [ United States House of Representatives elections in California, 2012 ] After redistricting, he announced in July 2011 that he would run in the newly redrawn California&apos;s 34th congressional district which he lives in and contains most of his current district. { http://xavierbecerra.ngphost.com/node/100 } [ Tenure ] Becerra is a prominent member of the Congressional Hispanic Caucus, of which he served as chairman during the 105th Congress. He was featured on The Colbert Report s Better Know a District on August 17, 2006. On September 29, 2008 Becerra voted against the Emergency Economic Stabilization Act of 2008 because he &quot; wanted to see direct protections for responsible homeowners &quot; in the bill. [ http://www.house.gov/htbin/blog_inc?BLOG%2Cca31_becerra%2Cblog%2C999%2CAll%2CItem%20not%20found%2CID%3D081001_2449%2CTEMPLATE%3Dpostingdetail.shtml On the Emergency Economic Stabilization Act December 6, 2008 Becerra Xavier October 1, 2008 The Becerra Blog US House of Representatives ] Becerra considered running for Democratic Caucus Vice Chair for the 110th Congress; however, he deferred to John Larson when DCCC chairman Rahm Emanuel decided to run for Caucus Chair, the position Larson had been running for. Instead, Becerra was appointed assistant to the Speaker of the House for the 110th Congress. Before the opening of the 111th Congress, Emanuel accepted a position as White House Chief of Staff in the Obama Administration. Larson succeeded Emanuel as caucus chair, and Becerra won his bid to succeed Larson as Vice-Chair. He defeated Marcy Kaptur of Ohio by a vote of 175–67. [ http://thehill.com/leading-the-news/dems-back-off-leadership-challenges-2008-11-10.html Dems back off leadership challenges TheHill.com Allen Jared November 10, 2008 ] In 2011, Becerra successfully ran for a second-term as Vice-Chair to serve during the 112th Congress. During the 111th Congress and 112th Congress, Becerra served on several high profile committees. On March 24, 2010 Becerra was appointed to serve on the National Commission on Fiscal Responsibility and Reform (often called Bowles-Simpson/Simpson-Bowles). On August 11, 2011 Becerra was selected to serve on the Joint Select Committee on Deficit Reduction (also known as the Super Committee). And on December 23, 2011 Becerra was appointed to serve on a bicameral conference committee to find bipartisan solutions on the middle class tax cuts, unemployment insurance, and the Medicare physician payment rate. Mayor ] Becerra ran for mayor of Los Angeles in 2001. He finished with 6 percent of the primary vote, finishing behind businessman Steve Soboroff, Councilman Joel Wachs, former California State Assembly Speaker Antonio Villaraigosa, and the eventual winner, then-City Attorney James Hahn. [ Obama administration ] On January 27, 2008, Becerra formally endorsed U.S. Senator Barack Obama for President. [ Congressman Xavier Becerra Endorses Barack Obama http://www.americanchronicle.com/articles/50337 ] Becerra was considered for the position of US Trade Representative in the administration of President-elect Obama.
