[ 1958 12 1 y ] { Glastonbury, Connecticut } { Novelist } { Charles Askegard } { candacebushnell.com } Candace Bushnell (born December 1, 1958) is an American author and columnist based in New York City. She is best known for writing a column that was anthologized in a book, Sex and the City, which in turn became the basis for a popular television series and its subsequent film adaptations. [ Personal life ] Bushnell was born in Glastonbury, Connecticut. While attending high school there, she was accompanied to her senior prom by Mike O&apos;Meara, now a nationally syndicated radio host, who also dated Candace&apos;s sister, &quot; Lolly &quot;. She attended Rice University and New York University in the 1970s, and became known in New York City as a socialite and party-goer. She often frequented Studio 54. In 1995, she met publishing executive Ron Galotti, who became the inspiration for Sex and The City &apos;s Mr. Big { http://nymag.com/nymetro/news/media/features/n_10334/index4.html New York Magazine, May 2004 }. In 2002, Bushnell married Charles Askegard, a principal dancer with the New York City Ballet. { WEDDINGS: VOWS; Candace Bushnell, Charles Askegard. By Bob Morris, The New York Times, July 7, 2002. } { Don&apos;t mention Sex and the City. By Carl Quinn, www.theage.com, September 28, 2003. } The couple lived in Manhattan but have filed for divorce. [ Career ] At age 19, Bushnell moved to New York City and sold a children’s story to Simon &amp; Schuster, which was never published. She continued writing and worked as a freelance journalist for various publications, struggling to make ends meet for many years. { Candace Bushnell on her new TV series Lipstick Jungle By Stephen Armstrong, The Sunday Times, August 10, 2008. } Bushnell began writing for the New York Observer in 1993. She created a humorous column for the paper, which appeared from 1994 through 1996. Called &quot; Sex and the City, &quot; the column was based on her own personal dating experiences and those of her friends. In 1997, Bushnell&apos;s columns were published in an anthology, also called Sex and the City, and soon after became the basis for the popular HBO television series sharing the same name. The series aired from 1998 through 2004, and starred Sarah Jessica Parker as Carrie Bradshaw, a sex and lifestyles columnist partaking in and enjoying New York City nightlife, a character which Bushnell has stated was her alter ego. The series is now in syndication and was adapted for and made into two motion pictures, released in 2008 and 2010. In 2005, Bushnell served as one of three judges for the reality television show Wickedly Perfect on CBS. Bushnell began hosting a live weekly talk show on Sirius Satellite Radio in 2007. { Candace Bushnell on Sirius: Sex, Success And Sensibility. sassybean.com, November 5, 2007. } The show, called &quot; Sex, Success and Sensibility, &quot; was canceled in late 2008 after the merger of Sirius and XM Satellite Radio, when Bushnell was asked to continue the show with a 50% pay cut. She refused. { Sirius XM Drops Candace Bushnell, &apos;Maxim&apos; Radio. By Jeff Bercovici, portfolio.com, November 14, 2008. } Bushnell&apos;s 2005 novel, Lipstick Jungle, was adapted for television and aired on NBC in 2008. The series Lipstick Jungle starred Brooke Shields in the leading role, and was canceled after 20 episodes. In 2009, Bushnell announced that she was slated to write several &quot; webisode s &quot; of an online comedic series about women over 40 dealing with workplace issues, in partnership with the magazine publisher Meredith Corporation &apos;s Meredith 360 division. { Candace Bushnell web series in the works. By Lucia Moses, The Hollywood Reporter, July 24, 2009. } { After &apos;Sex&apos; and &apos;Lipstick,&apos; Bushnell Looks to the Web. By Dylan Stableford, thewrap.com, published August 12, 2009.] } The contract also included writing articles for Meredith&apos;s &apos;&apos;More&apos;&apos; magazine. Bushnell was contracted by HarperCollins in 2008 to write a series of two books for young adults, about the high school years of Sex and the City character Carrie Bradshaw. The first of these, The Carrie Diaries, was published in April 2010. { &apos;Sex and the City&apos; gets teen spin: Bushnell takes Carrie to high school. By Sam Thielman, Variety, September 17, 2008. } Next in the series, Summer and the City (Carrie Diaries Series #2), was published in April 2011.
