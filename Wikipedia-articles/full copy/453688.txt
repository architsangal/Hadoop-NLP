It is located on the island of Sulawesi, and borders the province of Gorontalo to the west (originally a part of North Sulawesi until 2001 when it seceded to become a province of its own). The islands of Sangihe and Talaud form the northernmost part of the province, which border Davao del Sur in the Philippines. The population at the 2010 Census was 2,265,937. In 2006, about 68% were Christian (predominantly Protestantism with a sizeable Catholic minority), one of the few exceptions in the predominantly Muslim Indonesia. There are also Muslim, Hindu, and Buddhist minorities. The largest ethnic group is Minahasa n. The capital and largest city in North Sulawesi is Manado. [ Demographics ] North Sulawesi recorded 2,265,937 people in the decennial 2010 Census, with 1,157,551 males and 1,108,378 females, growing at 1.41% over the previous census. { BPS } The slowest growing regency was that of the Sangihe Islands. The province&apos;s largest city is Manado with 408,354 people. [ Administrative areas ] North Sulawesi is divided into eleven regencies ( [ kabupaten ] ) and four independent cities ( [ kotamadya ] ).
