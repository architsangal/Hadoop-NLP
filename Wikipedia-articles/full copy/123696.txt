[ 2 ] { GNIS feature ID } { 0831379 } [ 3 ] Monowi ( [ icon ˈ m ɒ n ɵ w aɪ ] [ MON o-wy ] ) is a village in Boyd County, Nebraska, United States. The village is notable because according to the 2010 census, it has a population of just one person. [ http://factfinder2.census.gov/bkmk/table/1.0/en/DEC/10_PL/P1/1600000US3132550 US Census Bureau American FactFinder 2011-04-13 ] The only remaining resident of the village is Elsie Eiler. [ Overview ] Rudy&apos;s Library Monowi&apos;s peak years were in the 1930s, when it had a population of 150. [ Macuha Marhgil Elsie Eiler, The Lone Resident of Monowi Town in Nebraska http://www.batangastoday.com/elsie-eiler-the-lone-resident-of-monowi-town-in-nebraska/12707/ Batangas Today ] Like many other small communities in the Great Plains, it lost its younger residents to cities that were experiencing growth and offering better jobs. During the 2000 census, the village had a total population of 2; only one married couple, Rudy and Elsie Eiler, lived there. Mr. Eiler died in 2004, leaving his wife as the only remaining resident. In this capacity, she acts as Mayor, granting herself a liquor license and paying taxes to herself. She is required to produce a municipal road plan every year in order to secure state funding for the village&apos;s four street lights. [ http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/news/world/us_and_americas/article516099.ece Introducing the mayor of Monowi: (population: 1) Reid Tim 19 February 2005 The Times 7 July 2011 ] Although the village is nearly abandoned, it does have the 5,000-volume Rudy&apos;s Library, founded in memory of Mr. Eiler and maintained by Mrs. Eiler. In addition, Mrs. Eiler runs a tavern in Monowi and draws regular customers from as far as 80 miles away. Elsie and &quot; Rudy&apos;s Library &quot; were shown on the Today show on May 31, 2005. Elsie and the &quot; Monowi Tavern &quot; were featured on CBS Sunday Morning on October 29, 2006, and again on August 12, 2007. Elsie, the village, the tavern, and library were featured on an episode of Only in America with Larry the Cable Guy, where Larry announced on a radio show that he was having a hotdog roast that very day to help raise money for Elsie&apos;s village. Within an hour people began to arrive and over a thousand people showed up for the roast to help Elsie. It aired April 12 and May 7, 2011, on The History Channel. [ Demographics ] As of the census [ 2 ] of 2000, there was one married couple living in the village. Both were white and above 65, and their median age was 67 years. Their household income was $11,250, or $6,167 per capita. The population density was 9.4 people per square mile (3.7/km²). There were 4 housing units at an average density of 18.8 per square mile (7.4/km²).
