[ May 2012 ] [ May 2012 ] [ the British film composer the British composer and conductor David Arnold (conductor) ] [ 1962 1 23 y ] { Luton, } { Bedfordshire, } { England } { Ellie Pole } { Two } { Composer } { Clarinet, } { guitar, } { piano } { Film score } { 1993–present } David Arnold (born 23 January 1962) is an English film composer best known for scoring five James Bond films, the 1994 film Stargate, the 1996 film Independence Day, the 1998 film Godzilla and the television series Little Britain and Sherlock. [ Film and television career ] While attending a Sixth Form College in Luton, Arnold became friends with director Danny Cannon. [ http://www.moviemusicuk.us/darnold.htm David Arnold soundtrack.net 2008-02-26 http://web.archive.org/web/20080104145454/http%3A//moviemusicuk.us/darnold.htm 2008-01-04 ] Danny initially created short films for which David was asked to write the music. The two made their respective major film debuts with The Young Americans. &quot; Play Dead &quot;, a song from the film with singer Björk, charted #12 in the UK. The following year he scored Stargate and Last of the Dogmen, with excerpts from the former ranking third in the most commonly used soundtrack cues for film trailers. [ http://www.soundtrack.net/trailers/frequent Top 100 Frequently Used Cues soundtrack.net 2007-08-14 ] Arnold would then go on to compose music for Stargate director Roland Emmerich &apos;s next two movies, Independence Day and Godzilla, as well as four movies for director John Singleton. In addition, he has also scored various comedies, dramas, and nineteenth-century period pieces, as well as providing music for several British television shows including the 2000 remake of Randall and Hopkirk (Deceased) and Little Britain. During film production, his compositions are conducted by Nicholas Dodd. In 2010, he composed the music for Come Fly With Me, a British television series from the producers of Little Britain. He is a member of the British Academy of Songwriters, Composers and Authors (BASCA), [ http://www.unsignedbandreview.com/british-academy-of-songwriters-composers-and-authors.html The Unsigned band review British Academy of Songwriters, Composers and Authors Founder of UBR 18 May 2009 4 February 2012 ] [ James Bond ] Arnold was a Bond fan from an early age and also a fan of Bond composer John Barry. In 1997, Arnold produced Shaken and Stirred: The David Arnold James Bond Project, an album featuring new versions of the themes from various James Bond films. The album featured a variety of contemporary artists including Jarvis Cocker, Chrissie Hynde, Propellerheads and Iggy Pop; a version of You Only Live Twice by Björk was recorded but not included on the album. John Barry, the composer of many of the themes on the album, was complimentary about Arnold&apos;s interpretation of his work; &quot; He was very faithful to the melodic and harmonic content, but he&apos;s added a whole other rhythmic freshness and some interesting casting in terms of the artists chosen to do the songs. I think it&apos;s a terrific album. I&apos;m very flattered. &quot; [ Jon Burlingame Bonding with the Score: David Arnold blends the traditional with the modern in music for &apos;Tomorrow Never Dies.&apos; Los Angeles Times Times Mirror Company 1997-12-18 2007-01-01 ] Barry contacted Barbara Broccoli, producer of the then-upcoming Tomorrow Never Dies, to recommend Arnold as the film&apos;s composer. [ Macnee, Patrick (Narrator) The Bond Sound: The Music of 007 DVD (Documentary) ] Arnold was hired to score the installment, and he has since scored the four subsequent films; The World Is Not Enough, Die Another Day, Casino Royale and Quantum of Solace. [ http://commanderbond.net/article/4582 David Arnold Back For &apos;Bond 22&apos; commanderbond.net 2007-10-08 ] Arnold will not be scoring the 23rd James Bond film, Skyfall, with Thomas Newman taking his place. [ MI6-HQ Copyright 2011 http://www.mi6-hq.com/news/index.php?itemid%3D9922 James Bond News :: MI6 :: Exclusive: Thomas Newman to compose &apos;Skyfall&apos; score Mi6-hq.com 20 October 1955 2012-04-22 ] Arnold commented that Newman had been selected by Mendes because of their work together, rather than because of Arnold&apos;s commitment to working with director Danny Boyle as composer for the Opening Ceremony of the 2012 Summer Olympics. Arnold also co-wrote the main theme songs for The World Is Not Enough ( &quot; The World Is Not Enough &quot; by Garbage ) and Casino Royale ( &quot; You Know My Name &quot; by Chris Cornell ) as well as &quot; Surrender &quot; by k.d. lang which appears during the end credits of Tomorrow Never Dies. Arnold also contributed the main themes to Kevin Kiner&apos;s score for Activision&apos;s GoldenEye 007, the remake of the 1997 game of the same name. [ Other work ] He has collaborated with such musical acts as Cast, The Cardigans, Kaiser Chiefs, Massive Attack, and Pulp, and solo artists Natasha Bedingfield, Melanie C, Björk, Chris Cornell, Shirley Manson, Mark Morriss and in 2009 produced Shirley Bassey &apos;s album &apos; The Performance.&apos; In 2001, he provided a new arrangement of Ron Grainer &apos;s Doctor Who theme music for the Eighth Doctor audio dramas from Big Finish Productions. His version was used as the Eighth Doctor theme starting with 2001&apos;s Storm Warning until 2008, when it was replaced with a new version arranged by Nicholas Briggs starting with Dead London. Arnold is the second cousin of Irish singer-songwriter Damien Rice, and is an ambassador for aid agency CARE International in the UK. He has made minor appearances in two different episodes of Little Britain as separate characters. In February 2011, it was announced that he was appointed Musical Director for the 2012 Olympic Games and the 2012 Paralympic Games in London.
