[ Bulgaria Flag of Bulgaria.svg 110100 3:5 1879 (original version) 1991 (current version) A horizontal tricolour of white, green and red National ] The flag of Bulgaria ( [ знаме на България ], [ znɑmɛ nɑ bɤ̞ɫˈɡɑrijɐ ] ) is a tricolour consisting of three equal-sized horizontal bands of (from top to bottom) white, green, and red. The flag was first adopted after the Russo-Turkish War (1877–1878), where Bulgaria gained independence. The national flag at times was charged with the state emblem, especially during the People&apos;s Republic of Bulgaria. The current flag was re-established with the 1991 Constitution of Bulgaria and was confirmed in a 1998 law. [ History ] After the liberational Russo-Turkish War (1877–1878), the flag was described in the Tarnovo Constitution of 1879 as follows: [ http://www.infotel.bg/juen/arh/i4240002.htm Tarnovo Constitution of 1879 Juridical Encyclopedia Bulgarian 2007-09-15 ] From 1948 until 1990 the emblem of People&apos;s Republic of Bulgaria was placed on the left side of the white stripe. It contained a lion within a wreath of wheat ears below a red five-pointed star and above a ribbon bearing the years 681, the year of the establishment of the First Bulgarian Empire by Asparukh and 1944, the year when the Fatherland Front took power which lead to the establishment of the People&apos;s Republic of Bulgaria.
