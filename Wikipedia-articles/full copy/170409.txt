Firth gained wide public attention in the 1990s for his portrayal of Mr. Darcy in the 1995 television adaptation of Jane Austen &apos;s Pride and Prejudice. In 2011, Firth received an Academy Award for his portrayal of King George  VI in The King&apos;s Speech, a performance that also earned him the Golden Globe, BAFTA and the Screen Actors Guild Award for Best Actor, amongst others. The previous year, he received his first Academy Award nomination, for his leading role in A Single Man, a performance that won him a BAFTA Award. He received a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame in 2011. [ Early life ] Firth was born in England. His mother, Shirley Jean (née Rolles), was a comparative religion lecturer at King Alfred&apos;s College Winchester (now the University of Winchester), and his father, David Norman Lewis Firth, was a history lecturer (also at King Alfred&apos;s) and education officer for the Nigerian Government. He later attended the Montgomery of Alamein Secondary School (now Kings&apos; School ), a state comprehensive school in Winchester, Hampshire. Reflecting on his time at Barton Peveril College in Eastleigh, he said &quot; My two years at Barton Peveril were among the two happiest years of my life &quot;. [ http://www.fenews.co.uk/fe-news/college-saved-me-reveals-actor-colin-firth College &apos;saved me&apos;, reveals actor Colin Firth Jason Rainbow 2010-06-15 FE News 2012-04-26 ] [ Career ] [ Film career ] In 1983, Firth starred as Guy Bennett in the award-winning London stage production of Another Country. In 1984, he made his film debut in the screen adaptation of the play, taking the role of Tommy Judd (opposite Rupert Everett as Bennett). In 1986, he starred with Sir Laurence Olivier in Lost Empires, a TV adaptation of J. B. Priestley &apos;s novel, then in 1987 he appeared alongside Kenneth Branagh in the film version of J. L. Carr &apos;s A Month in the Country. In 1989, he played the title role in the film Valmont, and was co-lead in the film Apartment Zero. Firth and other young British actors who were becoming established film actors such as Tim Roth, Gary Oldman, Bruce Payne and Paul McGann were dubbed the &apos; Brit Pack &apos;. [ http://www.brucepayne.de/press/articles/facearticle1987.html The Brit Pack Brucepayne.de 3 May 2010 ] { Nanny McPhee &apos;&apos; London premiere in October 2005 } It was through the 1995 BBC television adaptation of Jane Austen&apos;s Pride and Prejudice that Firth gained wider renown. The serial was a major international success, and Firth gained heartthrob status [ April 2010 ] because of his role as Mr. Fitzwilliam Darcy, in which he emerged in a wet shirt after a swim. [ March 2011 ] This performance also made him the object of affection for fictional journalist Bridget Jones (created by Helen Fielding ), an interest which carried on into the two novels featuring the Jones character. In the second novel, Bridget Jones: The Edge of Reason, the character even meets Firth in Rome. As something of an in-joke, when the novels were adapted for the cinema, Firth was cast as Jones&apos;s love interest, Mark Darcy. [ April 2010 ] Continuing this in-joke, there was a dog called Mr Darcy in the film St. Trinian&apos;s, which Firth&apos;s character accidentally kills. [ April 2010 ] Firth had a supporting role in The English Patient (1996) and since then, has starred in films such as Fever Pitch (1997), Shakespeare in Love (1998), Relative Values (2000), Bridget Jones&apos;s Diary (2001), The Importance of Being Earnest (2002), Love Actually (2003), What a Girl Wants (2003), Hope Springs (2003), Girl with a Pearl Earring (2003), Bridget Jones: The Edge of Reason (2004), Nanny McPhee (2005), Where the Truth Lies (2005), Then She Found Me (2007) with Helen Hunt, The Last Legion (2007) with Aishwarya Rai, And When Did You Last See Your Father? (2008), the film adaptation of Mamma Mia! (2008), and Easy Virtue, which screened at the Rome Film Festival to excellent reviews. [ http://easyvirtuereview.blogspot.com/ Easy Virtue brings British humour to Rome Film Festival 27 October 2008 www.reuters.com ] In 2009, he starred in A Christmas Carol, an adaptation of Charles Dickens &apos;s novel, using the performance capture procedure, playing Scrooge&apos;s optimistic nephew Fred. He has also appeared in several television productions, including Donovan Quick (an updated version of Don Quixote ) (1999) and Conspiracy (2001), for which he received an Emmy nomination. { Colin Firth Emmy Award Winner } Colin Firth&apos;s most recent role is in the Toronto International Film Festival debuted film, Genova. { Colin Firth, Genova Interview. AOL Entertainment Canada } [ May 2010 ] At the 66th Venice International Film Festival in 2009, Colin Firth was awarded the Volpi Cup for Best Actor for his role in Tom Ford&apos;s A Single Man as a college professor grappling with solitude after his longtime partner dies. Fashion designer Tom Ford made his director&apos;s debut with this movie. This role has earned Firth career best reviews and Academy Award, Golden Globe, Screen Actors Guild, BAFTA, and BFCA nominations; he won the BAFTA Award for Best Actor in a Leading Role in February 2010. The royalties from the sale of this book go to the indigenous rights organisation, Survival International. [ Personal life ] { Livia Giuggioli in January 2011 } Firth resides in Chiswick, London. In 1989, he entered into a romantic relationship with actress Meg Tilly, his co-star in Valmont. In 1990, she gave birth to a son, William &quot; Will &quot; Joseph Firth, and they made their home near the Lower Mainland of B.C., Canada. Firth remains in contact with Will and with Tilly&apos;s two other children. In 1994, after he and Tilly had separated, Firth became involved with actress Jennifer Ehle, his co-star in Pride and Prejudice; however, the two broke up and in 1997 Firth married Italian film producer/director Livia Giuggioli, and now lives in both London and Italy. [ http://film.guardian.co.uk/interview/interviewpages/0%2C%2C465976%2C00.html Steiner Susie Twice Shy The Guardian 31 March 2001 20 May 2008 London ] They have two sons, Luca (born March 2001) and Matteo (born August 2003). [ February 2011 ] Firth started to learn Italian when he and Giuggioli began to date and he now is fluent in the language. Firth is a supporter of Southampton F.C.. [ http://www.survival-international.org/news/24 &apos;Love Actually&apos; star Colin Firth condemns Bushman evictions Survival International 27 February 2007 ] Speaking in 2001, he said, &quot; My interest in tribal peoples goes back many years... and I have supported [Survival] ever since. &quot; [ http://www.survival-international.org/news/audio Audio Survival International ] In 2003, during the promotion of the movie Love Actually, he spoke in defense of the tribal people of Botswana, condemning the Botswana government&apos;s eviction of the Gana and Gwi Bushmen from the Central Kalahari Game Reserve. He says of the Bushmen, &quot; These people are not the remnants of a past era who need to be brought up to date. Those who are able to continue to live on the land that is rightfully theirs are facing the 21st century with a confidence that many of us in the so-called developed world can only envy. &quot; He has also backed a Survival International campaign to press the Brazilian government to take more decisive action in defence of the Awá-Guajá people, whose land and livelihood is critically threatened by the actions of loggers. [ http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2012/apr/22/brazil-rainforest-awa-endangered-tribe &apos;They&apos;re killing us&apos;: world&apos;s most endangered tribe cries for help Chamberlain Gethin The Guardian 22 April 2012 22 April 2012 ] Firth has been involved in a campaign to stop the deportation of a group of asylum seekers, because he believed that they might be murdered on their return to the Democratic Republic of Congo. [ Colin Firth We must stop a deportation that is likely to end in murder http://comment.independent.co.uk/letters/article2305539.ece The Independent 26 February 2007 27 February 2007 London ] Firth argued that &quot; To me it&apos;s just basic civilisation to help people. I find this incredibly painful to see how we dismiss the most desperate people in our society. It&apos;s easily done. It plays to the tabloids, to the Middle-England xenophobes. It just makes me furious. And all from a government we once had such high hopes for &quot;. [ Firth&apos;s intervention saves nurse from deportation http://news.independent.co.uk/uk/legal/article2308458.ece The Independent 27 February 2007 27 February 2007 London Damian Spellman ] Firth has also been involved in the Oxfam [ http://www.efi-news.com/2011/11/kings-speech-star-to-auction-himself.html The King&apos;s Speech Star to Auction Himself for Charity EF News International ] global campaign Make Trade Fair, [ http://www.maketradefair.com/en/index.php?file%3Ddumped_colin.htm Make Trade Fair – Oxfam International maketradefair.com ] in which several other celebrities participated as well in order to bring more attention to the issues involved. [ http://www.oxfam.org/en/news/pressreleases2005/pr051212_bignoise Celebrities present 18  million-strong Make Trade Fair petition to World Trade boss in Hong Kong – Oxfam International Oxfam International ] The campaign has focused on several trade practices seen as unfair to third world producers especially, including dumping, high import tariffs, and labour rights such as fair wages. Firth remains deeply committed to this cause, making efforts such as supporting fair trade coffee in his daily life, as he believes &quot; [i]f you&apos;re going to sustain commitment to any of this,... [y] ou&apos;ve got to get involved on an ordinary every day basis. &quot; [ http://www.firth.com/articles/05indep_716.html Colin Firth Profile in the Independent firth.com ] He has further contributed to this cause by opening (with a few collaborators) an eco-friendly shop in West London, Eco.
