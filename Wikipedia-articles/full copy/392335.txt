Ross 1982 ] Marjorie Armstrong &quot; Markie &quot; Post (born November 4, 1950 [ The Almanac 2009-11-04 United Press International http://www.upi.com/Odd_News/2008/11/04/The-almanac/UPI-88221225787400/ ] ) is an American actress, best known for her roles as bail bondswoman Terri Michaels in The Fall Guy on ABC from 1982 to 1985, as public defender Christine Sullivan on the NBC sitcom Night Court from 1985 to 1992, and as Georgie Anne Lahti Hartman on the CBS sitcom Hearts Afire from 1992 to 1995. [ Early life ] Born in Palo Alto, California, Post is the daughter of scientist Richard F. Post. She grew up in Walnut Creek, California, and attended Las Lomas High School where she was a cheerleader. She later earned her Bachelor of Arts from Lewis &amp; Clark College in Portland, Oregon. [ September 2010 ] [ Career ] Prior to acting, Post worked on several game shows. She began her career with the production crew of the Tom Kennedy version of Split Second. She also served as associate producer of Alex Trebek &apos;s Double Dare and as a card dealer on the NBC Jim Perry version of Card Sharks. Post often was a celebrity player on various game show s, including Pyramid and Password. Her early acting credits include two episodes of The A-Team as two different characters in the 1983 episode &quot; The Only Church in Town &quot; and the 1984 episode &quot; Hot Styles &quot;, respectively. She appeared in the science fiction series Buck Rogers in the 25th Century and as Diane Chambers&apos; best friend in the sitcom Cheers, before eventually becoming a regular on the ABC action drama The Fall Guy.
