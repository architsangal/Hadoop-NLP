[ March 2011 ] [ 1752 ] [ 1752 ] { NOTOC } Year 1752 ( MDCCLII ) was a leap year starting on Saturday of the Gregorian calendar, and a leap year starting on Wednesday of the 11-day slower Julian calendar. [ Events ] [ January &amp; amp;ndash;June ] [ http://vagenweb.org/hening/vol06-12.htm Hening&apos;s Statutes at Large William Walter Hening 9 April 2011 ] January 1 – The British Empire adopts the Gregorian calendar (to take effect in September). February 11 – Pennsylvania Hospital, the first hospital in the U.S., is opened. February 27 – The Virginia Assembly passes a law making maiming a felony, in response to the practice of gouging. March 23 – The Halifax Gazette, the first Canadian newspaper, is published. June 6 – Fire destroys 18,000 houses in Moscow. June 15 – Benjamin Franklin proves that lightning is electricity, using a kite and a key. [ July &amp; amp;ndash;December ] September 2 – Great Britain and the British Empire adopts the Gregorian calendar, meaning the Julian date of Wednesday, September 2 was followed by the Gregorian date of Thursday, September 14. [ Date unknown ] Adam Smith transfers to professor of moral philosophy at the University of Glasgow.
