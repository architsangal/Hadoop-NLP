Taylor 48 213 3 Jackson Men Anti-Jackson Special March 4, 1825 March 9, 1825 1st December 5, 1825 May 22, 1826 2nd December 4, 1826 March 3, 1827 18th 20th ] The Nineteenth United States Congress was a meeting of the legislative branch of the United States federal government, consisting of the United States Senate and the United States House of Representatives. It met in Washington, D.C. from March 4, 1825 to March 3, 1827, during the first two years of the administration of U.S. President John Quincy Adams. The apportionment of seats in the House of Representatives was based on the Fourth Census of the United States in 1820. The Senate had a majority of Jackson Men, while the House had an Anti-Jackson (pro-Adams) majority. Preceding the names in the list below are Senate class numbers, which indicate the cycle of their election.
