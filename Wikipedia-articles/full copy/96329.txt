As of the 2010 Census the county had a population of 16,267. { http://quickfacts.census.gov/qfd/states/16/16049.html } The county seat is Grangeville [ 6 ]. Previous county seats were Florence (1864-75) and Mount Idaho (1875-1902). Idaho County was originally founded as a region of Washington Territory in 1861, named for a steamer called Idaho that was launched on the Columbia River in 1860. It was reorganized by the Idaho Territorial Legislature on February 4, 1864. In this context, the name of the county predates both the Idaho Territory and the State of Idaho. Idaho County is one of seven U.S. counties to share its name with its state (the others are Arkansas County, Hawaii County, Iowa County, New York County, Oklahoma County, and Utah County ).
