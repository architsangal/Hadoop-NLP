[ October 2010 ] [ 1961 6 9 ] { Edmonton, } { Alberta, Canada } { Tracy Pollan (1988–present; 4 children) } { Actor, author, producer, activist, voice-over artist } { 1973–present } Michael J. Fox, OC (born Michael Andrew Fox; June 9, 1961) is a Canadian American actor, author, celebrity, producer, activist and voice-over artist. With a film and television career spanning from the late 1970s, Fox&apos;s roles have included Marty McFly from the Back to the Future trilogy (1985–1990); Alex P. Keaton from Family Ties (1982–1989) for which he won three Emmy Award s and a Golden Globe Award; and Mike Flaherty from Spin City (1996–2000), for which he won an Emmy, three Golden Globes, and two Screen Actors Guild Awards. Fox was diagnosed with Parkinson&apos;s disease in 1991, and disclosed his condition to the public in 1998. Fox semi-retired from acting in 2000 as the symptoms of his disease worsened. He has since become an activist for research toward finding a cure. This led him to create the Michael J. Fox Foundation, and on March 5, 2010, Sweden&apos;s Karolinska Institutet gave him a honoris causa doctorate for his work in advocating a cure for Parkinson&apos;s disease. He has also released three books, Lucky Man: A Memoir (2002); Always Looking Up: The Adventures of an Incurable Optimist (2009); and A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Future: Twists and Turns and Lessons Learned (2010). He was inducted as an Officer of the Order of Canada on May 27, 2011, for his outreach and fundraising work. He intended to continue to use the name, but when he registered with the Screen Actors Guild, which does not allow duplicate registration names to avoid credit ambiguities, he discovered that Michael Fox, a veteran character actor, was already registered under the name. As he explained in his autobiography, Lucky Man: A Memoir, and in interviews, he needed to come up with a different name. He did not like the sound of &quot; Andrew &quot; or &quot; Andy &quot; Fox, so he decided to adopt a new middle initial and settled on &quot; J &quot;, as an homage to actor Michael J. Pollard. [ Acting career ] [ Early career ] { Tracy Pollan at the 40th } { Emmy Awards Michael J Fox Emmy Award Winner } { in August 1988 shortly after they were married } Fox&apos;s first feature film roles were in &apos;&apos;Midnight Madness&apos;&apos; (1980) and Class of 1984 (1982), credited in both as Michael Fox. Shortly afterward, he began playing &quot; Young Republican &quot; Alex P. Keaton in the show Family Ties which aired on NBC for seven seasons, from 1982 to 1989. Fox received the role only after Matthew Broderick was unavailable. Family Ties had been sold to the television network using the pitch &quot; hip parents, square kids, &quot; [ Haglund David http://www.slate.com/articles/arts/dvdextras/2007/03/reagans_favorite_sitcom.single.html Reagan&apos;s Favorite Sitcom: How Family Ties spawned a conservative hero Slate March 2, 2007 January 2, 2011 ] with the parents originally intended to be the main characters. However, the positive reaction to Fox&apos;s performance led to his character&apos;s becoming the focus of the show following the fourth episode. At its peak, the audience for Family Ties drew one-third of America&apos;s households every week. Fox won three Emmy awards for Family Ties in 1986, 1987 and 1988 respectively. He also won a Golden Globe Award in 1989. Brandon Tartikoff, one of the show&apos;s producers, felt that Fox was too short in relation to the actors playing his parents, and tried to have him replaced. Tartikoff reportedly said that &quot; this is not the kind of face you&apos;ll ever find on a lunch-box &quot;. After his later successes, Fox presented Tartikoff with a custom-made lunch-box with the inscription &quot; To Brandon, this is for you to put your crow in. Love and Kisses, Michael J. Fox &quot;. Back to the Future tells the story of Marty McFly (Fox), a teenager who is accidentally sent back in time from 1985 to 1955. He meets his parents in high school, accidentally attracting his mother&apos;s romantic interest. Marty must repair the damage to history by causing his parents to fall in love, while finding a way to return to 1985. Robert Zemeckis, the director, originally wanted Fox to play Marty but Gary David Goldberg the creator of Family Ties, which Fox was working on at the time, refused to allow Zemeckis to even approach Fox as he felt that as Meredith Baxter was on maternity leave at the time, Fox&apos;s character Alex Keaton was needed to carry the show in her absence. Eric Stoltz was cast and was already filming Back to the Future, but Zemeckis felt that Stoltz was not giving the right type of performance for the humor involved. { &quot; Back to the Future: Making the Trilogy: Chapter 1 (DVD Documentary) &quot; } Zemeckis quickly replaced Stoltz with Fox whose schedule was now more open with the return of Meredith Baxter. During filming, Fox would rehearse for Family Ties from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m, then rush to the Back to the Future set where he would rehearse and shoot until 2:30 a.m. This schedule lasted for two full months. Back to the Future was both a commercial and critical success. The film spent 8 consecutive weekends as the number-one grossing movie at the US box office in 1985, and eventually earned a worldwide total of $381.11 million. [ http://www.boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id%3Dbacktothefuture.htm Back to the Future Box Office Mojo Box Office Mojo August 25, 2010 ] Variety applauded the performances, arguing Fox and his co-star Christopher Lloyd imbued Marty and Doc Brown &apos;s friendship with a quality reminiscent of King Arthur and Merlin. [ Variety http://www.variety.com/review/VE1117788826 July 1, 1985 Back to the Future October 9, 2008 ] Two sequels, Back to the Future Part II and Back to the Future Part III, were released in 1989 and 1990, respectively. In The Secret of My Success, Fox played a recent graduate from Kansas State University who moves to New York City where he has to deal with the downs and ups of the business world. [ Spin City and later career ] { The Great Movie Ride at } { Walt Disney World &apos;s } { Disney&apos;s Hollywood Studios theme park. } Spin City ran from 1996 to 2002 on American television network channel ABC. The show was based on a fictional local government running New York City, originally starring Fox as Mike Flaherty, a Fordham Law graduate serving as the Deputy Mayor of New York. Fox won an Emmy award for Spin City in 2000, three Golden Globe Award s in 1998, 1999 and 2000 and two Screen Actors Guild Awards in 1999 and 2000. During the third season of Spin City, Fox made the announcement to the cast and crew of the show that he had Parkinson&apos;s Disease. During the fourth season, he announced his retirement from the show to focus on spending more time with his family. [ http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/609629.stm Fox quits Spin City BBC January 19, 2000 August 22, 2010 ] He announced that he planned to continue to act and would make guest appearances on Spin City (he made three more appearances on the show during the final season). After his diagnosis, Fox began drinking more heavily than in the past; however, he sought help and stopped drinking altogether. [ http://www.guardian.co.uk/lifeandstyle/2009/apr/11/michael-j-fox-parkinsons It&apos;s the gift that keeps on taking The Guardian April 11, 2009 June 29, 2009 London Emma Brockes ] In 1998, he decided to go public with his condition, and since then he has been a strong advocate of Parkinson&apos;s disease research. His foundation, The Michael J. Fox Foundation, was created to help advance every promising research path to curing Parkinson&apos;s disease, including embryonic stem cell studies. Fox manages the symptoms of his Parkinson&apos;s disease with the drug Sinemet, { &quot; Fresh Air &quot; interview by Terry Gross &quot; National Public Radio, April 2002. } and he also had a thalamotomy in 1998.
