This diverse group, which arose in the Triassic period, [ Palmer Douglas The Marshall Illustrated Encyclopedia of Dinosaurs &amp; Prehistoric animals Marshall Editions Developments Ltd 1999 London 3-8290-6747-X ] includes 20,000 extant species in about 40 orders; most living fishes are members of this group. { Miller, Stephen, and John P. Vertebrate Paleontology Chapman &amp; Hall 1990 London 0-412-54010-X ] [ Characteristics ] Teleosts have a movable maxilla and premaxilla and corresponding modifications in the jaw musculature. These modifications make it possible for teleosts to protrude their jaws outwards from the mouth. [ Telostei http://www.ucmp.berkeley.edu/vertebrates/actinopterygii/teleostei.html Museum of Paleontology, University of California, Berkeley Ben Waggoner 1995-07-17 2006-06-08 ] The caudal fin is homocercal, meaning the upper and lower lobes are about equal in size. The spine ends at the caudal peduncle, distinguishing this group from those in which the spine extends into the upper lobe of the caudal fin. [ Systematics and evolution ] The oldest teleost fossils date back to early Triassic. Teleosts are here divided into twelve superorders, but this system is unlikely to be entirely correct and is in the process of being studied.
