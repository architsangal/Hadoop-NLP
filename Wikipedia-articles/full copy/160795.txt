[ August 2011 ] [ December 2010 ] This is a list of notable events in music that took place in the year 1988. [ 1988 music ] { TOC } [ Events ] [ August 2008 ] [ January–March ] [ July 2011 ] [ Persall Steve June 16, 1988 A Pair of Sixxes? Rolling Stone Straight Arrow Publishers, Inc. 528 30 ] [ July 2011 ] [ July 2011 ] [ July 2011 ] [ July 2011 ] [ May 5, 1988 Jackson Rules Charts Rolling Stone Straight Arrow Publishers, Inc. 525 22 ] January 1 – André Rieu &apos;s Johann Strauss Orchestra plays its first concert. January 3 – The Cinemax television special Roy Orbison and Friends, A Black and White Night, recorded on September 30, 1987 at the Coconut Grove in Los Angeles, USA, is broadcast. January 20 – The Rock and Roll Hall of Fame ceremony inducts The Beach Boys, The Beatles, The Drifters, Bob Dylan and The Supremes. January 28 – A Tampa, Florida man files an unusual lawsuit against Mötley Crüe. Matthew John Trippe, who has a history of mental health issues and trouble with the law, claims that he was secretly hired to pose as Nikki Sixx and toured, wrote and recorded with the band for a time during 1983 and 1984. Trippe drops the lawsuit in 1993. February 13 – Michael Jackson purchases a ranch in Santa Ynez, California, USA. He called his new home &quot; Neverland &quot;. February 16 – Billy Vera receives a star on the Hollywood Walk Of Fame. February 24 – Alice Cooper announces that he is going to run for Governor of Arizona, USA. March 12 – Record producer Jimmy Iovine presents the Special Olympics with a donation of US$5 million, which is raised with the release of the A Very Special Christmas album. March 26 – &quot; Man in the Mirror &quot; by Michael Jackson from the Bad album tops the Billboard Hot 100. It&apos;s the first time in history a solo artist has had four Number One singles from the same album. [ April–June ] [ DeCurtis Anthony June 16, 1988 McGhee Placed on Probation Rolling Stone Straight Arrow Publishers, Inc. 528 29 ] [ Pond Steve June 30, 1988 Atlantic&apos;s Birthday Bash Rolling Stone Straight Arrow Publishers, Inc. 529 48–50, 66 ] [ July 2011 ] [ Ressner Jeffrey August 11, 1988 Motown Records Sold Rolling Stone Straight Arrow Publishers, Inc. 532 17 ] April 19 – Former rock and roll singer Sonny Bono is inaugurated as the Mayor of Palm Springs, California, USA. April 25 – Rock supermanager Doc McGhee is sentenced to five years probation after pleading guilty to charges of drug smuggling stemming from a 1982 seizure of nearly 40,000 pounds of marijuana entering North Carolina from Colombia. April 30 – The Eurovision Song Contest, held in the RDS Simmonscourt Pavilion, Dublin, is won by French-Canadian singer Celine Dion, representing Switzerland with the song &quot; Ne partez pas sans moi &quot;. May 14 – Atlantic Records stages a concert at Madison Square Garden celebrating its Fortieth birthday with performances by many of the label&apos;s greatest acts of the past. Artists include Crosby, Stills &amp; Nash, Iron Butterfly, Ruth Brown, Foreigner and Wilson Pickett, but the most talked-about performance is by a reunited Led Zeppelin with Jason Bonham on drums. May 27 – The Monsters of Rock Tour 1988 commences in East Troy, Wisconsin. Van Halen headlines with the other acts on the massive bill consisting of Metallica, Scorpions, Dokken and Kingdom Come. June 18 – Depeche Mode &apos;s final concert of their world tour takes place in the Rose Bowl stadium near Los Angeles with 60,000 spectators which is the significant moment in the bands career. June 27 – Motown Records is sold to MCA and an investment firm for $61 million. [ July–September ] [ July 2011 ] [ December 15, 1988 August Rolling Stone Straight Arrow Publishers, Inc. 541/542 84 ] [ July 2011 ] [ July 2011 ] [ Ressner Jeffrey November 3, 1988 James Brown Arrested After Car Chase Rolling Stone Straight Arrow Publishers, Inc. 538 27 ] July 1 – Quartetto Cetra play their last concert. July 2 – Michael Jackson with fifth single from Bad, &quot; Dirty Diana &quot;, he broke the record to have five consecutive charting singles from same album at the top of the Billboard Hot 100, is the first artist and only male in history to get this done. August 12 – Public Enemy garners publicity by staging a concert at Riker&apos;s Island prison for 250 inmates and 100 journalists. August 20 – Two people are crushed to death at the annual Monsters of Rock festival at Castle Donington, UK. during the Guns N&apos; Roses set. August 28 – While travelling down the Ventura Freeway in southern California, USA, the motorhome carrying the Charlie Daniels band bursts into flames. Daniels and his band members escape without injury. September 6 -9 – Elton John auctions off many items from his personal collection, including memorabilia and stage-worn clothing, at Sotheby&apos;s for a total of $8.5 million. John had been known for wearing flamboyant stage costumes during the glam rock era of the 1970s, but he increasingly abandoned them in later years. September 10 – Billboard magazine publishes its Hot Modern Rock Tracks chart for the first time. September 24 – James Brown faces a variety of charges after leading police on an interstate chase, after reportedly breaking into a seminar in an Augusta, Georgia building he owned an office in, waving a gun and demanding to know who had used his restrooms. Earlier in the year Brown had been arrested on drug and firearms-related charges. [ October–December ] [ July 2011 ] [ July 2011 ] [ Goldberg Michael January 12, 1989 Fogerty Wins Unusual Self-Plagiarism Suit Rolling Stone Straight Arrow Publishers, Inc. 543 15 ] [ Ressner Jeffrey January 12, 1989 Bono Less Than Boffo at Box Office Rolling Stone Straight Arrow Publishers, Inc. 543 16 ] [ July 2011 ] October 10 – Genesis&apos;88 stage their first illegal Acid House Party in the UK. November – The &quot; Guess The Eddies &quot; contest begins, inviting people to guess the identities of the six members of the rock band The Swirling Eddies. November 7 – John Fogerty wins a self-plagiarism lawsuit with Fantasy Records. The record label had contended that Fogerty&apos;s 1985 comeback hit &quot; The Old Man Down the Road &quot; was too similar to his 1970 Creedence Clearwater Revival song, &quot; Run Through the Jungle &quot;. November 12 – U2&apos;s Rattle and Hum hits the Number One spot on the U.S. charts, the first double album to do so since Bruce Springsteen&apos;s The River in 1980. December 4 – Singer Roy Orbison gives his last concert in Akron, Ohio, USA, before his death from a massive heart attack. December 28 – A few months before the release of her new album Like A Prayer, Madonna files assault charges against her husband, Sean Penn. Though she later drops the charges, the couple separate three days later and are divorced in the following year. December 31 – The seventeenth annual New Year&apos;s Rockin&apos; Eve special airs on ABC, with appearances by Natalie Cole, Taylor Dayne, DJ Jazzy Jeff &amp; The Fresh Prince, Richard Marx, Reba McEntire and Frankie Vallie and The Four Seasons.
