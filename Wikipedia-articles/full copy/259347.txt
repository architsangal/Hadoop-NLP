[ 1974 6 27 ] { Dallas, Texas } { Actor, singer/songwriter } { 1997–present } Christian Kane (born June 27, 1974) is an American actor and singer/songwriter of Native American descent. He currently stars as Eliot Spencer on the TNT series Leverage. He is best known for his roles in the television shows Angel and Into the West, and the movies Just Married and Secondhand Lions. He is the lead singer of the country - southern rock band Kane. On December 7, 2010 they released The House Rules, their third album and their debut for record label Bigger Picture Group. The album reached #25 on the Billboard Country Albums chart. The first single from the album, also titled &quot; The House Rules &quot;, debuted at #54 on the Billboard Country Songs chart. The second single, “Let Me Go”, was released on July 11, 2011. [ Early life ] Christian Kane was born in Dallas, Texas. He is of Native American descent [ http://www.eonline.com/uberblog/watch_with_kristin/b135115_christian_kane_lover_fighter_on_leverage.html Christian Kane Is a Lover and a Fighter on Leverage ] and is a cousin of country singer Branden Hart. [ http://www.imdb.com/name/nm1035143/bio Branden Hart bio on IMDB ] His parents participated in, and met at, the rodeo. The family moved around the South and Midwest because his father was in the oil business and finally settled in Norman, Oklahoma, when Kane was in 8th grade. While growing up in Texas and Oklahoma, Kane was a wrestler and played American Football (as a strong safety ). [ http://www.starpulse.com/news/index.php/2009/07/22/christian_kane_of_leverage_discusses_his CK discusses his Bad-Ass Character ] He studied Art History at the University of Oklahoma but decided he wanted to be an actor and headed to Los Angeles before finishing the degree. [ http://www.digitaljournal.com/article/295118 Christian Kane is singing a totally different tune on &apos;Leverage&apos; ] In LA, he worked at a talent management company where he delivered scripts in exchange for being sent on auditions. [ http://www.intrepidmedia.com/column.asp?id%3D4138 Portrait of an artist: Christian Kane ] [ Career ] [ Acting ] { Amy Acker at a signing in Santa Barbara. } Kane landed his first acting role in 1997 as one of the leads in MGM &apos;s television series Fame L.A.; in the series he played a country singer who moved to Los Angeles from Kansas. His television roles have included a recurring role in Joss Whedon ’s Angel as the lawyer Lindsey MacDonald, the Jerry Bruckheimer produced series Close to Home on CBS, the TNT television film Crossfire Trail in which he appeared alongside Tom Selleck, and he played the role of Abe Wheeler in the Steven Spielberg produced miniseries Into the West. Kane’s appearances on the big screen include the 20th Century Fox film Just Married and New Line Cinema &apos;s Secondhand Lions, as the younger version of Robert Duvall &apos;s Hub McCann, he has a scar on his upper lip from an injury he received while filming the movie. [ http://www.televisionwithoutpity.com/show/mondo_extra/the_christian_kane_interview.php?page%3D3 Kane and Able ] He also appeared in Taxi, Life or Something Like It, the Peter Berg directed Friday Night Lights, and the Warner Bros. baseball film Summer Catch. In 2007, Kane was featured in the Carrie Underwood music video, &quot; So Small &quot;, playing the role of one of the three converging stories. In 2009, he appeared in the psychological thriller, Hide, [ http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0827181/ Hide on IMDB ] in which he played the lead role of Billy, and The Donner Party which is based on the true story of the Donner Party. In 2010, he starred in the romantic drama, Not Since You. [ http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1072438/ Not Since You on IMDB ] [ http://www.screenspotlight.com/movie-reviews-mainmenu-47/37-drama/707-not-since-you Not Since You review ] Since late 2008, Kane appears on the TNT television series Leverage, which will return for its fifth season next summer. [ http://news.turner.com/article_display.cfm?article_id%3D5275 TNT Orders New Seasons of Three Hit Series: The Closer, Rizzoli &amp; Isles and Leverage ] Kane does his own stunts and fights as Eliot Spencer, a retrieval specialist who teams up with four other specialists to undo the injustices perpetrated upon the helpless. [ Music ] In 1997, Christian Kane met Steve Carlson, who had taken over his old job. They started writing songs together, and in 1998, they formed the Southern rock band Kane. They have toured the U.S. as well as England and Germany. The band has self-released two albums, their self-titled debut, Kane, in 2000 and a recording of a live acoustic set, Acoustic Live In London! in 2004. [ http://www.cityofangel.com/behindTheScenes/bts4/chrisSpot2.html Return of the Spirit Boy ] The band consists of Christian Kane ( lead vocals, guitar ), Steve Carlson ( rhythm guitar, backing vocals ), Jason Southard ( lead guitar ), Will Amend ( bass guitar ), and Ryan Baker ( drums ) and is signed to independent record label Bigger Picture Group (Zac Brown Band, Big Kenny, The Harters). Their label debut, The House Rules, was released December 7, 2010. It debuted at #1 on the Billboard Heatseekers album chart and #25 on the Country Albums chart. [ http://www.billboard.com/charts/country-albums#%2Fcharts%2Fheatseekers-albums%3Ftag%3Dchdrawer Billboard Heatseekers chart ] The album was produced by Bob Ezrin and Jimmie Lee Sloas. [ http://www.allaccess.com/country/artist-info/q/id/1451/christian-kane AllAccess Artist profile: Christian Kane ] The first single from the album, also titled &quot; The House Rules &quot; debuted at #54 on the Billboard Country Songs chart [ http://www.billboard.com/column/chartbeat/chart-highlights-alternative-country-songs-1004124105.story Billboard oct. 25th 2010 Chart Highlights ] and was the 7th most added song on Mediabase Country stations on its official impact date. The video for the single was directed by Timothy Hutton and premiered on CMT &apos;s Big New Music Weekend October 1, 2010. [ http://www.cmt.com/news/news-in-brief/1649107/eight-videos-debuting-during-cmt-big-new-music-weekend.jhtml CMT: Eight Videos Debuting During CMT Big New Music Weekend ] The second single, Let Me Go, was released on July 11, 2011. The video for the single was directed by Roman White, it premiered on CMT August 8, 2011 and reached #1 on their Today&apos;s Top Videos chart.
