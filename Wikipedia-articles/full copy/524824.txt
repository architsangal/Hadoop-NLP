[ voiceless bilabial nasal Talk:Palatal nasal#Distinguish from the voiceless palatal nasal April 2012 ] [ 114 109 Xsampa-m.png m m ] The bilabial nasal is a type of consonant al sound used in almost all spoken language s. The symbol in the International Phonetic Alphabet that represents this sound is ⟨ [ m ] ⟩, and the equivalent X-SAMPA symbol is ⟨m⟩. The bilabial nasal occurs in English, and it is the sound represented by &quot; m &quot; in map and rum. It occurs nearly universally, and few languages (e.g., Mohawk ) are known to lack this sound.
