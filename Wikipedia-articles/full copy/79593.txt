[ Stephin Merritt Stephin-merritt-at-cadogan-hall-crop.jpg 150 Stephin Merritt at Cadogan Hall, London, July 2008. solo_singer Indie pop Synth pop Singer-songwriter Guitar Ukulele Keyboard The Magnetic Fields The 6ths The Gothic Archies Future Bible Heroes ] Stephin Raymond Merritt (born 1966) is an American singer-songwriter based in Los Angeles (formerly in New York City ), best known as the principal singer and songwriter in the band The Magnetic Fields. He is known for his distinctive and untrained bass voice. [ Berlind William The Stephin Merritt Standard http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res%3D9F01E5D81E3FF937A15751C0A9649C8B63 The New York Times 24 February 2002 4 October 2011 ] [ Musical projects ] [ February 2011 section ] He created and played principal roles in the bands The Magnetic Fields, The 6ths, The Gothic Archies and Future Bible Heroes. He briefly used the name The Baudelaire Memorial Orchestra as an attribution for a song written for Lemony Snicket &apos;s A Series of Unfortunate Events, entitled &quot; Scream and Run Away &quot;. Further music was recorded for the audiobook versions of the series and is attributed to The Gothic Archies. The Tragic Treasury was released by Nonesuch Records in October 2006 along with the 13th and final book of the series. { Merritt, Snicket and the &apos;Tragic Treasury&apos;, All Things Considered December 3, 2006 } Under his own name, he recorded and released the soundtracks to the films Eban and Charley and Pieces of April. The soundtrack to the Nickelodeon show The Adventures of Pete &amp; Pete featured many of his songs. He and director Chen Shi-zheng have collaborated on three pieces of musical theatre; Orphan of Zhao (2003), Peach Blossom Fan (2004), and My Life as a Fairy Tale (2005) { Brantley, Ben. Exploring the Shadows of a Sunny Writer&apos;s Nightmare New York Times. July 29, 2005. } Select tracks from these works have been released on Nonesuch Records under the title Showtunes. Additionally, he is one-third of the infrequent, live-only musical extravaganza the Three Terrors ensemble, whose other principal members include 69 Love Songs album&apos;s Dudley Klute and LD Beghtol; past themes of these performances have included: French pop, movie themes (including the title song from Deep Throat ), intoxication, and New York. Kenny Mellman (of Kiki &amp; Herb ), James Jacobs, Daniel Handler, Jon DeRosa and others have performed with The Three Terrors at these sporadic gala events. Merritt wrote and sang &quot; I&apos;m In a Lonely Way &quot; in a television commercial for Volvo that aired in the summer and fall of 2007. He also performed &quot; The Wheels on the Car &quot;. Merritt penned the music and lyrics for a 2009 Off-Broadway stage musical of &quot; Coraline &quot;, based on the novel by Neil Gaiman. In the MCC Theater production, his music will be performed by a piano “orchestra” – complete with a traditional piano, a toy piano, and a prepared piano (a piano that has had its sound altered by attaching objects – such as tin foil, rubber bands and playing cards – to the strings). [ &quot; Coraline &quot; music MCC Theater http://www.mcctheatre.com/shows/08-09_season/coraline/music.html 2009-04-09 ] He produced a score for the silent film 20,000 Leagues Under the Sea that was performed at the Castro Theatre, San Francisco on May 4, 2010 as part of the San Francisco International Film Festival. [ Khanna Vish Exclusive: Stephin Merritt Promises to Return to the Synth on Next Magnetic Fields Album, Scores 20,000 Leagues Under the Sea http://www.exclaim.ca/articles/generalarticlesynopsfullart.aspx?csid1%3D140%26amp%3Bcsid2%3D844%26amp%3Bfid1%3D43998 exclaim.ca 2010-01-28 ] [ Songwriting topics ] [ section May 2011 ] Merritt is gay, { Hyperreal: Magnetic Fields Arch Duke Stephin Merritt Interviewed. } { The Quietus 20 Jan. 2010. } and his lyrics are known for bending and blurring gender lines; examples include the song &quot; When My Boy Walks Down the Street &quot;, which contains the lyric &quot; and he&apos;s going to be my wife, &quot; and on the single &quot; Andrew in Drag &quot;, from the album Love at the Bottom of the Sea, he sings, &quot; A pity she does not exist, a shame he&apos;s not a fag. The only girl I ever loved is Andrew in drag. &quot; [ Personal life ] Merritt has never met his father, folk singer Scott Fagan, [ http://www.jcornett.com/swf/conversations.htm Conversations With Scott Fagan ] who had a brief affair with Merritt&apos;s mother. Merritt attended progressive Massachusetts high school The Cambridge School of Weston and briefly attended NYU before moving back to Boston. He has worked as an editor for Spin Magazine and Time Out New York. Merritt has a chihuahua named Irving, after Irving Berlin. { Rule, Doug. Magnetic Mystery. Metro Weekly. 27 Jan. 2010. } Merritt is known for having a dry personality, embracing a persona and life that is very different from the traditional rock star image. In a September 2005 interview conducted by The Onion s The A.V. Club, alternative rock musician Bob Mould was reminded of an interviewer who once referred to Mould as &quot; the most depressed man in rock &quot;. Mould&apos;s response was, &quot; He&apos;s never met Stephin Merritt, obviously. &quot; [ Kyle Ryan Interview: Bob Mould http://www.avclub.com/content/node/40813/3 The Onion 2005-09-21 2008-08-27 ] Merritt suffers from a hearing condition known as hyperacusis; any sound heard louder than normal begins to &quot; feedback &quot; in his left ear at increasingly louder volumes. This has largely influenced the reserved live setup of The Magnetic Fields, which usually consists of acoustic instruments and little to no percussion. Merritt also wears earplugs during performances, and typically covers his left ear when the audience applauds.
