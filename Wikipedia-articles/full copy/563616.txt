[ http://www.ubisoftgroup.com/index.php?p%3D65%26amp%3Bart_id%3D At a glance Ubisoft 2009-04 2010-02-24 ] As of now, it is the third largest independent game publisher in Europe, and the third largest in the United States. The company&apos;s largest development studio is Ubisoft Montreal, which currently employs more than 1,700 people. { Questions about Ubisoft Montreal - Topic Powered by eve community } Yves Guillemot, a founding brother, was the chairman and CEO. As for 2008–2009 fiscal year, Ubisoft&apos;s revenue was €1.058 billion, reaching the 1 billion euro milestone for the first time in its history. Ubisoft has created its own film division called Ubisoft Motion Pictures which will create shows and films based on its games. [ Griffin McElroy Ubisoft launching film studio focused on game adaptations http://www.joystiq.com/2011/05/02/ubisoft-launching-film-studio-focused-on-game-adaptations/ Joystiq ] [ History ] The five brothers of the Guillemot family founded Ubisoft as a computer game publisher in March 1986 in France ( Brittany ). { &quot; Games, Games and More Games &quot; article from Start magazine volume 3 issue 8, March 1989; retrieved from Atarimagazines.com } Yves Guillemot soon made deals with Electronic Arts, Sierra On-Line, and MicroProse to distribute their games in France. By the end of the decade, Ubisoft began expanding to other markets, including the United States, the United Kingdom, and Germany. [ http://www.ubisoftgroup.com/index.php?p%3D66%26amp%3Bart_id%3D History Ubisoft 2010-02-24 ] In the early 1990 years, Ubisoft initiated its in-house game development program which led to the 1994 opening of a studio in Montreuil, France, which later became their headquarters. Ubisoft became a publicly traded company in 1996 and continued to expand to offices around the globe, opening locations in Shanghai and Montreal. In 2000, Ubisoft acquired Red Storm Entertainment. [ http://www.bluesnews.com/a/60 Ubi Soft Acquires Red Storm Entertainment Blue&apos;s News 2000-08-29 2010-02-24 ] In February 2001, they acquired Düsseldorf, Germany based Blue Byte Software. [ http://www.gamespot.com/news/2683947.html Ubi Soft acquires Blue Byte Software Jennifer Ho Gamespot 2001-02-09 2010-02-24 ] In March 2001, Gores Technology Group sold The Learning Company &apos;s entertainment division (which includes games originally published by Brøderbund Software, Mattel, Mindscape and Strategic Simulations, Inc. ) to them. The sale included the rights to IP s such as the Myst and Prince of Persia series. [ http://www.gamezone.com/news/03_07_01_11_45AM.htm Ubi Soft Acquires The Learning Company&apos;s Entertainment Division GameZone 2001-03-07 2010-02-24 ] In October 2001, they acquired Gamebusters and moved them to the German Offices. [ http://www.mobygames.com/company/ubisoft-entertainment-sa/history History for Ubisoft Entertainment SA MobyGames 2010-02-24 ] In the late 1990s and early 2000s, Ubisoft committed itself to online games by getting behind Uru: Ages Beyond Myst, The Matrix Online, and the Europe an and Chinese operation of EverQuest. The publisher established ubi.com as its online division. However, in February 2004, Ubisoft cancelled the online portion of Uru and backed out of the publishing deal on The Matrix Online. Nevertheless, a mere week later, the company announced its acquisition of Wolfpack Studios, developer of Shadowbane. In December 2004, a rival game corporation Electronic Arts purchased a 19.9% stake in the firm, an action Ubisoft referred to as &quot; hostile &quot; on EA&apos;s part. [ Curt Feldman Electronic Arts buys stake in Ubisoft in &quot; hostile &quot; act http://www.gamespot.com/news/6115370.html Gamespot 2004-12-20 2007-11-09 ] In March 2005, Ubisoft acquired part of MC2-Microïds (Microïds Canada) and integrated it into Ubisoft Montreal. Additionally, though Ubisoft is not acquiring the studio outright, the members of Driver developer Reflections Interactive became employees of Ubisoft. As a result, Reflections Interactive was subsequently renamed Ubisoft Reflections.
