[ April 2011 ] [ April 2011 ] [ 1981 8 8 y ] { Melbourne, } { Victoria } { Rock, } { pop, } { acoustic } { Singer-songwriter } { 1998–present } { Universal Music (2006–present) } { Sony BMG } Vanessa Amorosi (born 8 August 1981 [ bio @ Vanessa Amorosi&apos;s Official Management Website http://www.ralphcarr.com/web/rcm.htm ] ) is an Australia n singer-songwriter and recording artist. Her combined album and single sales have reached over 2 million worldwide. [ Life and career ] [ Early life ] Amorosi was born in Melbourne, Victoria. When Amorosi was 4, along with her younger sisters, she would go to tap, jazz and classical ballet classes, which were being run by her uncle. She attended Emerald Primary and Emerald Secondary School. { Emerald Primary School - History - Past Students } At the age of 12 Amorosi started to perform in shopping centers and local council concerts under the supervision of her family. Amorosi was discovered performing at Matrioshka, { News.com.au: Making tracks } a Russian restaurant in Carnegie, by Jack Strom and signed a recording contract in 1997. After being signed to MarJac Productions (co-owned by managers Mark Holden and Jack Strom) and recording a promo CD titled &quot; Get Here &quot;, she secured a deal with Transistor Music Australia. [ The Power (1999–2003) ] In 1999, Amorosi released her debut single &quot; Have a Look &quot; which became a Top 20 hit and went gold in Australia. This was followed by her successful second single &quot; Absolutely Everybody &quot;, an anthemic dance song, reached the Top 10 and became the longest charting Australian single by an Australian female artist. { Australian Music Online :: Artists :: Vanessa Amorosi } It remained in the ARIA Top 50 Singles Chart for over six months after its release. { australian-charts.com - Vanessa Amorosi - Absolutely Everybody } &quot; Absolutely Everybody &quot; was released internationally and reached the Top 10 in many European countries including the UK and Germany where it received a gold certification. Amorosi performed at many large events such as the NRL Grand Final, the opening of the Motorcycle Grand Prix at Phillip Island and the new millennium on &quot; New Year&apos;s Eve &quot; at Darling Harbour. Following the success of the first two singles Amorosi released two more singles in 2000 &quot; Shine &quot; and &quot; The Power &quot; became major Australian hits. Both were accredited gold by the ARIA Australian music industry. The rights to create a remixed version of &quot; Shine &quot; were purchased by the airline Ansett Australia, who used this version for a commercial. Vannessa Amorosi was one of many celebrities to appear in the commercial. Amorosi performing in 2002 Her album The Power reached number one on the Australian album charts. Amorosi received six ARIA nominations { ARIA Awards 2007 : History: Winners by Year } in the year 2000 awards and The Power was nominated { SONY BMG MUSIC ENTERTAINMENT :: Australia :: CD Releases } for &quot; Best Female Artist &quot; and &quot; Best Pop Release &quot; in the ARIA Music Awards of 2001.Her largest audience came in September 2000 with performances at the opening and closing ceremonies of the 2000 Sydney Olympics and at the Paralympic Games. At the opening ceremony of the aforementioned Olympics she sang the Olympic song titled &quot; Heroes Live Forever &quot;. Amorosi won the APRA Award { APRA Music Awards - Winners 2001 } in 2001 for &quot; Most Played Song &quot; on Australian Radio with &quot; Shine &quot; and received international acclaim in Germany where she won &quot; Best International Newcomer &quot; { Viva.tv - Events - COMET 2007: History - Die Gewinner } at the 2001 VIVA Comet Awards. Later that year she also made a special appearance at the Goodwill Games Opening and Closing ceremonies in Brisbane. In 2001 she performed at the AFL Grand Final. In August 2002 Amorosi traveled to Manchester where she was one of the stars of the Commonwealth Games Hand-Over Ceremony, performing her hit &quot; Shine &quot; and a new song written especially for the event, &quot; I&apos;ll always be a Melbourne girl &quot;. In February 2003, Amorosi performed her blues set { VanessaAmorosi.de: Melbourne Blues Festival (February 2003) } for the first time at the Melbourne International Music and Blues Festival and traveled to Germany to perform her latest European single &quot; True to Yourself &quot; on Top of the Pops and the hugely successful long-running show Wetten, dass..? which screens throughout Europe.
