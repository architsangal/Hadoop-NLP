[ England 53.988460 -1.140790 Nether Poppleton 2,077 Nether Poppleton City of York North Yorkshire Yorkshire and the Humber York Outer YORK YO26 YO 01904 SE564550 ] Nether Poppleton is a village and civil parish in the unitary authority of the City of York in North Yorkshire, England. It is situated by the West bank of the River Ouse adjacent to Upper Poppleton, and west of York close to the A59 road from York to Harrogate. The village is served by Poppleton railway station on the Harrogate Line. According to the 2001 census, the parish had a population of 2,077. Prior to 1996, it had been part of the Harrogate district. The name is derived from popel (pebble) and tun (hamlet, farm), and means &quot; Pebble Farm &quot;, due to the gravel bed upon which the village was built. [ http://www.surnamedb.com/Surname/Popplestone Origin of name 2010-11-14 ] The neighbouring village of Upper Poppleton has been referred to as &quot; Land Poppleton &quot; and Nether Poppleton as &quot; Water Poppleton &quot;, indicating the villages&apos; position relative to the river. The village is mentioned in the Domesday Book and the Anglo-Saxon Chronicles. It became a Conservation Area in 1993. The earthworks to the north and east of the parish church are designated as a Scheduled Monument( [ 53 59 23.12 N 1 8 25.95 W ] ). [ http://www.wessexarch.co.uk/reports/55756/nether-poppleton/ Scheduled Monument 2010-11-19 ] [ History ] In 972, the village was recorded as &quot; Popeltun &quot; in a list made for Archbishop of York Oswald of Church property lost in the wars earlier in the century [ http://www.channel4.com/history/microsites/T/timeteam/2005_pop.html Time Team 2012-02-08 ] { For video on 4OD see http://www.channel4.com/programmes/time-team/4od#2929881 }, and in the Domesday Book as &quot; Popeltune &quot;. The villages and lands were given by Osbern De Arches to the Abbot of St Mary&apos;s in York. It was, therefore, under the ecclesiastical rule of the Parish of St Mary-Bishophill Junior. [ Bulmer&apos;s Topography, History and Directory (Private and Commercial) of North Yorkshire 1890 1890 S &amp; N Publishing 1-86150-299-0 2010-11-08 ] During the reign of Richard II, the village was the scene of the murder of a Mayor of York. [ Bulmer&apos;s Topography, History and Directory (Private and Commercial) of North Yorkshire 1890 1890 S &amp; N Publishing 1-86150-299-0 2010-11-08 ] In 1644, the 25,000-strong Scottish and Parliament Armies, led by the Earl of Manchester, laid siege to the city of York. To facilitate communications, they built a &quot; Bridge of Boats &quot; at Poppleton. This bridge was eventually taken by Prince Rupert and his Royalist Forces, but he subsequently lost the battle at Marston Moor. [ http://www.british-civil-wars.co.uk/military/1644-york-march-marston-moor.htm Civil War 2010-11-14 ] The village benefitted from the growth in the railways in the 19th Century when the York, Knaresborough and Harrogate Railway routed its line through Poppleton and erected a station. [ The York, Knaresborough and Harrogate Railway 2001 C.T.Goode 1-870313-22-4 2011-11-14 ] [ Time Team Dig 2004 ] In June 2004, the British broadcaster Channel 4 made an episode of its archaeological programme Time Team in the village [ http://www.channel4.com/history/microsites/T/timeteam/2005_pop.html Time Team 2010-11-19 ] { For video on 4OD see http://www.channel4.com/programmes/time-team/4od#2929881 } in association with Yorkshire Wessex Archaeology. It was to investigate the origins of the village based near some of the earthenworks around the village, especially near the church and Manor Farm. In total, 12 trenches were dug in addition to 32 test pits dug by the local population. The dig found evidence that there had been a monastic building in the village that was dated between AD 450-850. [ Governance ] Nether Poppleton lies within the Rural West Ward of the City of York Unitary Authority. As of the 2011 elections, it is represented by Councillors Ian Gillies, Paul Healey and Chris Steward who are all members of the local Conservative Party. [ http://democracy.york.gov.uk/mgFindCouncillor.aspx?XXR%3D0%26amp%3BAC%3DWARD%26amp%3BWID%3D362 Councillors 2010-11-06 ] It is also a part of the UK Parliamentary Constituency of York Outer as well as the EU region of Yorkshire and the Humber Locally, there is a parish council with seven council members. [ http://www.poppleton.net/nppc/councillors.html Parish Council 2010-11-14 ] [ Economy ] Poppleton was formerly an agricultural settlement with many farms, but the modern village is mostly a dormitory for commuters to the nearby towns and cities. It has benefited from its good road and rail links. The village shares local retail facilities, including a Post Office, and some small enterprises, with Upper Poppleton. [ Demography ] In the 19th century, the population has varied between 254 and 346. The 2001 census recorded the population as 1,961.
