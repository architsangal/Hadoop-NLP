The population was 6,955 at the 2000 census. [ Geography ] Three Lakes is located at [ 25 38 7 N 80 23 57 W type:city ] (25.635372, -80.399031) [ 1 ]. According to the United States Census Bureau, the CDP has a total area of [ 3.8 sqmi km2 ], of which, [ 3.3 sqmi km2 ] of it is land and [ 0.5 sqmi km2 ] of it (14.17%) is water. [ Demographics ] As of the census [ 2 ] of 2000, there were 6,955 people, 2,463 households, and 1,889 families residing in the CDP. The population density was 2,131.4 people per square mile (823.7/km²). There were 2,627 housing units at an average density of 805.1/sq  mi (311.1/km²). The racial makeup of the CDP was 72.49% White (of which 31.6% were Non-Hispanic White,) [ http://www.muninetguide.com/states/florida/municipality/Three_Lakes.php Demographics of Three Lakes, FL MuniNetGuide.com 2007-11-11 ] 15.64% African American, 0.12% Native American, 3.87% Asian, 0.01% Pacific Islander, 4.10% from other races, and 3.77% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 47.76% of the population. There were 2,463 households out of which 44.8% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 56.4% were married couples living together, 15.8% had a female householder with no husband present, and 23.3% were non-families. 16.9% of all households were made up of individuals and 2.1% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.82 and the average family size was 3.19. In the CDP the population was spread out with 28.3% under the age of 18, 9.4% from 18 to 24, 40.8% from 25 to 44, 16.1% from 45 to 64, and 5.4% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 31 years. For every 100 females there were 90.4 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 85.5 males. The median income for a household in the CDP was $54,830, and the median income for a family was $58,424. Males had a median income of $37,194 versus $30,481 for females. The per capita income for the CDP was $22,832. About 6.7% of families and 8.0% of the population were below the poverty line, including 8.0% of those under age 18 and 13.8% of those age 65 or over. As of 2000, speakers of Spanish as a first language accounted for 51.87% of residents, while English made up 45.19%, and French was the mother tongue of 2.92% of the population.
