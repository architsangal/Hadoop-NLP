Elliott County, Kentucky courthouse.jpg Elliott County courthouse in Sandy Hook, Kentucky ] Elliott County is a county located in the U.S. state of Kentucky. It was formed in 1869. As of 2010, the population is 7,852. Its county seat is Sandy Hook, Kentucky [ 6 ]. The county is named for John Milton Elliott, U.S. Congressman; Confederate Justice of the Kentucky Court of Appeals. Elliott County is one of 55 counties in the Kentucky that is a dry county, meaning the sale of alcoholic beverages is prohibited everywhere in the county. It was formed on April 1, 1869, from parts of Morgan, Lawrence, and Carter counties. [ Geography ] According to the 2000 census, the county has a total area of [ 235.20 sqmi ], of which [ 233.96 sqmi ] (or 99.47%) is land and [ 1.24 sqmi ] (or 0.53%) is water. This is the longest ongoing streak of any county voting Democratic in the United States. { Dave Leip&apos;s Atlas of U.S. Presidential Elections } Elliott County was the second whitest county in the country, at 99.04%, to vote for Barack Obama in the 2008 Presidential election, the whitest being Mitchell County, Iowa. Obama garnered 61% of the vote, while John McCain received 36%. In 2008, Elliott county provided Obama with the highest percentage of the winning vote out of all Kentucky counties. { NYT Electoral explorer } As of 2012, Elliott county had the fewest number of registered Republicans, 187, out of all counties in Kentucky. { VOTER REGISTRATION STATISTICS REPORT, Kentucky Secretary of State } [ Demographics ] As of the census [ 2 ] of 2000, there were 6,748 people, 2,638 households, and 1,925 families residing in the county.
