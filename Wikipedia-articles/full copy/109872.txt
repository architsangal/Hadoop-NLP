[ 2 ] { GNIS feature ID } { 0331228 } [ 3 ] Braselton is a town in Barrow, Gwinnett, Hall, and Jackson Counties in the U.S. state of Georgia, about 40 miles northeast of Atlanta. As of the 2010 census, the city had a population of 7,511. { http://factfinder2.census.gov/faces/tableservices/jsf/pages/productview.xhtml?pid=DEC_10_SF1_GCTP2.ST13 &amp; prodType=table } The Gwinnett and Barrow county portions of Braselton are part of the Atlanta – Sandy Springs – Marietta, GA- AL Metropolitan Statistical Area, and the Hall County portion is part of both the Atlanta and Gainesville, GA Metropolitan Statistical Area. The remaining Jackson County portion of Braselton is not part of any Core Based Statistical Area. Braselton is the largest city in Jackson County by population. [ Geography ] Braselton is located at [ 34 5 56 N 83 47 52 W type:city ] (34.098764, -83.797814) [ 1 ]. According to the United States Census Bureau, the town has a total area of [ 12.41 sqmi km2 ]. All of it is land and none is covered by water. Braselton has seen its growth from 7.20 to 12.41 square miles from annexations into surrounding cities and areas. Braselton borders Gainesville (Candler), Flowery Branch, Oakwood (Chestnut Mountain side), Pendergrass, and Hoschton and is located in Hall and Jackson Counties. Braselton extends into four ZIP code areas, those being 30517 (Braselton), 30519 Buford (Duncan Corners area), 30542 ( Flowery Branch, Duncan Corners area), and 30548 ( Hoschton, Thompson Mill area). [ Town Facts / Economy ] Braselton is home to Chateau Elan Winery and Resort which is the largest winery in Georgia. Braselton is the largest city in Jackson County and the second largest in Barrow County by population. The town is home to Road Atlanta, which is used for a variety of events including all kinds of local and international car and motorcycle races, driving schools, and testing for motorsports teams. Also found here is many distribution centers for major companies and warehouses which serve as the southeastern distribution for the United States. Mayfield Dairy has a plant in Braselton. The visitors center closed down October, 2011. Braselton was ranked #1 for Recession-Resistant Growth in the Nation by the Gadberry group in 2009. { http://www.gadberry.net/GG%209from2009.cfm } Braselton was ranked #11 on America&apos;s Fastest-Growing Cities 2010 by Bloomberg Businessweek and the Fastest-Growing city in Georgia. { http://images.businessweek.com/ss/10/01/0128_americas_fastest_growing_cities/11.htm } The Town was ranked #13 in the State of Georgia for the median per capita being $39,135. Ripley&apos;s Believe It or Not! proclaims Braselton as the longest running claim of a mayor and most of the council with the same last name that has lasted from 1916-2002. It has not been broken since. Braselton was named after the Braselton family that has lived in the area for some time. The town was bought from the Braselton Families by a partnership between the Ameritech Pension fund and actress Kim Basinger in 1989 for $20 million. The Basinger-Braselton partnership hoped to establish Braselton as a tourist attraction with movie studios and a film festival. Basinger eventually sold her minor portion to the Ameritech Fund when she met financial difficulties. Much of the town&apos;s property is under the ownership of developer Wayne Mason. [ Headquarters ] Braselton is the headquarters of the American Le Mans Series, American Junior Golf Association, Elan Motorsport Technologies, IMSA, Denis Griffiths and Associates, Inc (Golf Course Architects), Macedonia World Baptist Missions, Atlantic Engineering Group, Hamilton State Bank, Chateau Elan Hotels and Resorts, the Panoz Motor Sports Group, Skip Barber Racing School, BBS of America, the Road Atlanta race track and Year One. In 2009, Carbon Motors Corporation announced that Braselton was one of 3 sites for their $350 million campus including a headquarters, manufacturing plant, sales office, production divisions, and a research and development center that would employ as many as 1,300 worker. That summer, Carbon Motors announced that Connersville, Indiana would receive the campus. Also found are the newly arrived major distribution warehouses for Petco Animal Supplies, Kichler Lighting, Safelite AutoGlass, Sears Appliance Division, Whole Foods Market, Year One, Tractor Supply, Carter&apos;s, Inc. Clothing, and Haverty&apos;s Furniture. In 2012, Carter&apos;s, Inc. announced that Braselton is going to receive their one-million-square-foot, $50 million distribution center that will support their e-commerce, retail, and wholesale businesses. The company hopes to create 1,000 jobs at the facilty by 2015. { http://www.gainesvilletimes.com/section/6/article/65675/ } [ Development ] Braselton boasts of two Publix super markets within the town&apos;s limits. In 2010, adjacent to the Kroger, the second Publix was opened on Spout Springs Road, which has small shops around it being Pizza Hut, AT &amp; T Store, Chase, Wells Fargo, Wilson Orthodontics, and many more shops coming soon such as Suntrust. In 2010, It was announced that a 216 acre retail complex is to be built across from Chateau Elan and behind the Publix anchored shopping center on H.W. 211. No tenents have been announced, yet it is said to include over 1.4 million sq. ft. of building area with a hotel, home improvement store, retail space, and a movie theater. A similar project built by the same company in the area is Stonebridge Village in neighboring Flowery Branch. { http://www.braseltonnewstoday.com/archives/4140-Largest-retail-project-about-to-get-bigger.html } In 2008, North Georgia Health System built the River Place Braselton. The campus is home to Medical Plaza 1, home to an imaging center, rehabilitation center, imaging center, a pharmacy and many more. In 2011, it was announced that work would begin on Medical Plaza 2. In the town the population was spread out with 30.2% under the age of 18, 5.0% from 18 to 24, 28.0% from 25 to 44, 31.3% from 45 to 64, and 7.3% who were 65 years of age or older. Males had a median income of $46,477 versus $27,292 for females. The per capita income for the town was $35,921. About 4.1% of families and 3.9% of the population were below the poverty line, including 6.9% of those under age 18 and 12.6% of those age 65 or over.
