[ April 2011 ] [ 1958 10 29 ] { Hackensack, } { New Jersey } { Magazine editor, journalist, writer and editor } { Editor-in-chief of } { The New Yorker } David Remnick (born October 29, 1958) is an American journalist, writer, and magazine editor. He won a Pulitzer Prize in 1994 for his book, Lenin&apos;s Tomb: The Last Days of the Soviet Empire. Remnick has been editor of The New Yorker magazine since 1998. He was named &quot; Editor of the Year &quot; by Advertising Age in 2000. Before joining The New Yorker, Remnick was a reporter and the Moscow correspondent for The Washington Post. He has also served on the New York Public Library &apos;s board of trustees. In 2010 he published his sixth book, The Bridge: The Life and Rise of Barack Obama. [ Early life and family ] Remnick was born in Hackensack, New Jersey, the son of a dentist and an art teacher. { Coussin, Orna. &quot; How to put a legendary magazine back on its feet, &quot; Ha&apos;aretz (February 9, 2006). } He was raised in Hillsdale, New Jersey, in a secular Jewish home with, he has said, &quot; a lot of books around &quot;. { Wood, Gaby. &quot; The quiet American &quot;, } { The Observer, September 10, 2006. Accessed April 10, 2011. &quot; David Remnick was born in 1958 and grew up in Hillsdale, New Jersey, where his father was a dentist and his mother an art teacher. &quot; } He is also childhood friends with comedian Bill Maher. He graduated from Princeton University in 1981 with an A. B. in comparative literature; there, he met writer John McPhee and helped found The Nassau Weekly. Remnick has implied that after college he wanted to write novels, but due to his parents&apos; illnesses, he needed a paying job—there was no trust fund to rely upon. { Bigthink Interview Series, YouTube, http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i87X_OE3Qac } Remnick wanted to be a writer, so he chose a career in journalism, taking a job at the Washington Post. He is married to reporter Esther Fein of The New York Times and has three children, Alex, Noah, and Natasha. He enjoys jazz music and classic cinema and is fluent in Russian. [ http://www.nytimes.com/2006/05/14/books/review/14hamill.html A Ringside Seat Hamill Pete May 14, 2006 The New York Times April 22, 2011 ] [ Career at The Washington Post ] He began his reporting career at The Washington Post in 1982 shortly after his graduation from Princeton. [ David Remnick http://www.albany.edu/writers-inst/webpages4/archives/remnickdavid.html State University of New York: New York State Writers Institute ] His first assignment was to cover the United States Football League. { &quot; The Tony Kornheiser Show &quot;, WTEM, April 13, 2010 } After six years, in 1988, he became the newspaper&apos;s Moscow correspondent, which provided him with the material for his Pulitzer Prize -winning book, Lenin&apos;s Tomb. He also received the George Polk Award for excellence in journalism. [ Career at The New Yorker ] Remnick became a staff writer at The New Yorker in September, 1992, after ten years at The Washington Post. [ David Remnick http://www.albany.edu/writers-inst/remnickdavid.html May 9, 2006 http://web.archive.org/web/20060422120443/http%3A//www.albany.edu/writers-inst/remnickdavid.html April 22, 2006 ] Remnick&apos;s 1997 New Yorker article, &quot; Kid Dynamite Blows Up &quot;, about boxer Mike Tyson, won a National Magazine Award. In 1998, he became editor, succeeding Tina Brown. Remnick promoted Hendrik Hertzberg, a former Jimmy Carter speechwriter and former editor of The New Republic, to write the lead pieces in “Talk of the Town,” the magazine’s opening section. In 2005, Remnick earned $1 million for his work as the magazine&apos;s editor. { &quot; Salary Guide: Who Makes How Much, &quot; &apos;&apos;New York&apos;&apos; magazine (2005). } In 2003, he wrote an editorial supporting the Iraq war in the days when it started. [ http://www.newyorker.com/archive/2003/02/03/030203ta_talk_remnick Making a Case Remnick David February 3, 2003 The New Yorker January 16, 2011 ] In 2004, for the first time in its 80-year history, The New Yorker endorsed a presidential candidate, John Kerry. [ http://www.newyorker.com/talk/content/?041101ta_talk_editors New Yorkermagazine endorsement of John Kerry May 9, 2006 ] In May 2009, Remnick was featured in a long-form Twitter account of Dan Baum&apos;s career as a New Yorker staff writer. The tweets, written over the course of a week, described the difficult relationship between Baum and Remnick, his editor. [ http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2009/05/08/dan-baum-fired-by-inew-yo_n_200457.html Dan Baum, Fired By New Yorker, Recounting His Story On Twitter Linkins Jason August 5, 2009 The Huffington Post April 22, 2011 ] Remnick&apos;s biography of President Barack Obama, The Bridge, was released on April 6, 2010. It features hundreds of interviews with friends, colleagues, and other witnesses to Obama&apos;s rise to the presidency of the United States. The book has been widely reviewed in journals. { McNeil D. The bridge-builders. Safundi: The Journal of South African and American Studies 2010, 11(4), 459–464. } In 2010, Remnick lent his support to the campaign urging the release of Sakineh Mohammadi Ashtiani, the Iranian woman sentenced to death by stoning after being convicted of committing adultery.
