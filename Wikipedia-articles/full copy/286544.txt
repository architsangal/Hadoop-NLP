[ Deep Impact (disambiguation) ] [ 1998 5 8 ] { 121 minutes } { English } { $ 75 million } { $349,464,665 } Deep Impact is a 1998 science-fiction disaster - drama film directed by Mimi Leder, written by Bruce Joel Rubin and Michael Tolkin, executive produced by Steven Spielberg, and stars Robert Duvall, Téa Leoni, Elijah Wood, Vanessa Redgrave, Maximillian Schell and Morgan Freeman. It was released by Paramount Pictures and DreamWorks Pictures in the United States on May 8, 1998. The plot describes the attempts to prepare for and destroy a 7-mile wide comet, which is expected to collide with the Earth and cause a mass extinction. Although Deep Impact had a greater scientific credibility, [ http://us.imdb.com/ReleasedInYear?year%3D1998%26amp%3Bcountry%3DUSA%26amp%3B%26amp%3Bnav%3D%2FSections%2FYears%2F1998%2Finclude-byreleasedate Release in 1998 USA Internet Movie Database 2008-03-23 ] [ September 2010 H3llBot ] [ http://www.space.com/opinionscolumns/opinions/plait_000217.html Space.com Hollywood Does the Universe Wrong Plait, Phil 2000-02-17 ] &apos;&apos;Armageddon&apos;&apos; fared better at the box office. Deep Impact grossed over $349 million worldwide on a $75 million production budget (making it a major financial success). [ http://www.boxofficemojo.com/genres/chart/?id%3Ddisaster.htm Disaster Movies Box Office Mojo 2008-03-23 ] Both films were similarly received by critics, with Armageddon scoring 41% and Deep Impact scoring 46% on Rotten Tomatoes. The movie grossed $140,000,000 in North America and an additional $209,000,000 worldwide for a total gross of $349,000,000. Despite competition in the summer of 1998 from the similar Armageddon (which cost almost twice as much as Deep Impact to make), Deep Impact was still a box office hit and was the higher opener of the two. [ http://boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id%3Ddeepimpact.htm Deep Impact (1998) Box Office Mojo 2008-02-22 ] Domestically, it became the highest grossing film directed by a woman and held that record for a decade until Twilight claimed the record in 2008. The film had a mixed critical reception. Based on 51 reviews collected by Rotten Tomatoes, 47% of critics enjoyed the film, with an average rating of 5.7/10. [ Deep Impact (1998) http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/deep_impact/?name_order%3Dasc December 23, 2009 ] Metacritic gave a score of 40 based on 20 reviews. Elvis Mitchell of The New York Times said that the film &quot; has a more brooding, thoughtful tone than this genre usually calls for &quot;, [ Movie Review  — Deep Impact http://movies.nytimes.com/movie/review?_r%3D2%26amp%3Bres%3D9407E6DB1231F93BA35756C0A96E958260 December 23, 2009 The New York Times ] [ September 2010 RjwilmsiBot ] however Rita Kempley and Michael O&apos;Sullivan of the Washington Post criticized what they saw as unemotional performances and a lack of tension for the scenario.
