[ January 2012 ] [ January 2012 ] [ SSC25569 Emerald (State Suburb) 2007-09-15 on ] { 20.3 } { Gembrook } { La Trobe } { 50 } { Melbourne } { 11 } { Belgrave } { Kallista } { Monbulk } { Macclesfield } { Avonsleigh } { Menzies Creek } { Cockatoo } { Clematis } { Dewhurst } { Mount Burnett } Emerald is a town and semi-rural locality in the Greater Melbourne area, Victoria, Australia, 44  km south-east from Melbourne&apos;s central business district, outside the urban area. Its Local Government Area are the Shires of Cardinia and Yarra Ranges. At the 2006 Census, Emerald had a population of 6135. Emerald also includes Cardinia Reservoir, Melbourne&apos;s second largest reservoir. [ History ] The Emerald Post Office opened on 22 December 1899. [ Premier Postal History Post Office List https://www.premierpostal.com/cgi-bin/wsProd.sh/Viewpocdwrapper.p?SortBy%3DVIC%26amp%3Bcountry%3D 2008-04-11 ] [ Attractions ] [ Puffing Billy and Emerald Lake ] Emerald is known for the Emerald Lake (Lake Treganowan). The lake offers barbecue facilities, children&apos;s playgrounds, a pool, paddle boats, walking tracks, fishing, Environment Centre, model railway, café and a railway station for Puffing Billy. The Puffing Billy Railway is a heritage steam operated railway opened in 1900, which runs between Belgrave and Gembrook, now recreating the heyday in the 1920s of this narrow-gauge line. It has three stations within Emerald—at Emerald, Nobelius and Lakeside, with Nobelius only being a &quot; Stops if required &quot; station. Also, Nobelius Siding is located between Emerald and Nobelius stations which is only stopped at for functions, or the regular &quot; Dinner Train &quot; at the former Nobelius Nurseries Packing Shed adjacent to the siding. [ Other attractions ] Also notable is the Gemco Hall, which has been home to the Gemco Players Community Theatre since the 1990s. Gemco offers a variety of plays as well as social activities, involving the Emerald community and abroad. Chris and Marie&apos;s (Wombat Gully) plant farm was another notable feature of Emerald until it closed down in April 2005. The areas around Emerald are excellent for finding chanterelle s and other edible mushrooms, and are a popular destination for mushroom hunters each autumn. [ Cardinia Reservoir ] [ Cardinia Reservoir ] Cardinia Reservoir is situated entirely in Emerald, Victoria. It is south of the main township and the southern border of the suburb follows the Reservoir Boundary. { Melway Street Directory, Edition 35, Page 126-127-210-211-311-313 } It is the second largest reservoir in Melbourne&apos;s water supply. [ Cardinia Reservoir Park ] On the Southern side of the Reservoir there is a large park run by Parks Victoria. Facilities include barbecues and toilets and is open every day of the year. The park is home to many species of wildlife including Kangaroos, Wombats and Possums. It is even possible to walk across one of the dam walls at the park. { Cardinia Reservoir Park, Parks Victoria. Accessed 9 January 2009 } Despite being predominately located in the suburb of Emerald, the entrance to the park is approximately 10 minutes drive out of Emerald in Narre Warren East. Cardina Reservoir is the designated place to hold water when the Desalination plant is operating. There is a pipe running between Wongthaggi and Cardina Reservoir to make this possible. [ http://www.emeraldsc.vic.edu.au/ Emerald Secondary College 2008-11-21 ] Emerald has its own permanent library at Worrell Reserve as part of the Casey Cardinia Library Corporation. The Australian College of Youth Training [ http://www.acyt.org.au/cgi-bin/content.pl?acytfacilities.htm Australian College of Youth Training 2009-04-28 ] is the only tertiary education provider based in Emerald. The Emerald Community House runs adult education, pre-accredited ACFE training courses and hobby courses. There are also childcare programs, out of school hours care and children&apos;s extension programs and disability services. The University of the Third Age (U3A) is located in the Emerald Community House. [ Emergency services ] Emerald has a part-time Police Station, a full-time Ambulance Station as well as a volunteer CFA Brigade and SES Unit. The Emerald Fire Brigade is located on Emerald-Monbulk Road near the roundabout of Belgrave-Gembrook Road, and attends approximately 190 Emergency Incidents per year. The Brigade trains on Wednesday evenings and Sunday mornings and new members are always welcome. The Emerald Volunteer State Emergency Service Unit is located on Old Gembrook Road near the Corner of Sherriff Road. It attends several hundred storm and wind damage incidents every year and also attends 20–30 road accidents a year for rescue purposes using such tools as the jaws of life. The Unit trains on Monday evenings and new members are very welcome to join. [ Demographics ] Emerald&apos;s population as of the 2006 Census was 6,135 (a decrease of 9 from the 2001 census), (3,050 male, 3,085 female), 4,662 people were aged 15 years and over (2,280 male, 2,382 female) and 460 were aged 65 years and over (200 male, 260 female). According to the 2001 Census, 17 people identified themselves as indigenous persons (comprises Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander). 5,461 are Australian Citizens, while 4,770 people were identified as having been born in Australia. 1,365 were born overseas. 5,608 people speak English alone. [ Television dramas set in Emerald ] Solo One was a TV series screened in 1976 set in Emerald, about a local (fictional) policeman dealing with crime in Emerald. Filming of A Country Practice in the fictional town of Wandin Valley was moved to Emerald when the show moved to Network Ten. Filming for the ABC children&apos;s drama Come Midnight Monday was effected in and around Emerald, Belgrave &amp; Cockatoo, depicting the fictional country town of Widgery. [ Notable people ] Emerald is the hometown of Vanessa Amorosi. Current AFL players, Kade Simpson, who plays for the Carlton Football Club and Matthew Lobbe, who plays for the Port Adelaide Football Club, grew up in Emerald. Red Symons was a resident of Emerald and attended Emerald Primary School. Alfred Gregory, the mountaineer, explorer and professional photographer, who was a member of the successful British team that made the first ascent of Mount Everest in 1953, lived his last 15 years in Emerald, dying on 9 February 2010. Round-the-world solo teenage sailor Jesse Martin grew up in Emerald.
