New Nelson County KY Courthouse.JPG Nelson County Judicial Center in Bardstown, Kentucky ] Nelson County is a county located in the U.S. state of Kentucky. As of 2010, the population was 43,437. Its county seat is Bardstown [ 6 ]. The county is part of the Louisville/Jefferson County, KY– IN Metropolitan Statistical Area. [ History ] The fourth county created in what is now Kentucky, it was formed from Jefferson County, Virginia in 1784 shortly after the Revolutionary War. The county is named for Thomas Nelson, Jr., a Virginia Governor who signed the Declaration of Independence. In 1808, another new Virginia county was named for Governor Nelson. (see Nelson County, Virginia ). [ Cities, towns and census-designated places ] Bardstown Bloomfield Boston Chaplin Coxs Creek Deatsville Fairfield Highgrove Howardstown Lenore Nazareth New Haven New Hope Samuels Trappist Woodlawn [ Education ] Two public school districts operate in the county: The Nelson County School District serves K-12 students throughout the county, with the exception of most of the city of Bardstown. The district operates two K-8 schools, two elementary schools, two middle schools, an alternative school, a vocational-technical school, and one high school. The district is building a second high school that is scheduled to open in 2012. The Bardstown City Schools serve students in most of the city of Bardstown. However, some areas of the city are instead served by the Nelson County district, and some of the Nelson County schools are physically within the Bardstown district. The district operates a preschool, an ungraded primary school (K-2), one elementary school, one middle school, and one high school. The preschool and primary school occupy separate buildings on adjacent plots of land in the north of the city, and the other schools are adjacent to one another near downtown. Several private schools also operate in the county. The Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Louisville operates five schools in all—three K-8 schools, a fourth that educates grades 1 through 8, and one high school. Several Protestant-affiliated schools also exist.
