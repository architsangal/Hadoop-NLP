[ the British newscaster other people named Jon or John Snow John Snow (disambiguation) ] [ January 2011 ] [ 1947 9 28 yes ] { Ardingly, } { Sussex, England } { Journalist, television presenter } { Peter Snow, } { Dan Snow } { English } { Channel 4 News } Jon Snow (born 28 September 1947) is an English journalist and presenter, currently employed by ITN. He is best known for presenting Channel 4 News. He was Chancellor of Oxford Brookes University from 2001 to 2008. [ Early life ] Snow was born in Ardingly, Sussex. He is the son of schoolmaster and Bishop of Whitby, George D&apos;Oyly Snow, grandson of First World War general Thomas D&apos;Oyly Snow (about whom he writes in his Foreword to Ronald Skirth &apos;s war memoir The Reluctant Tommy ), and cousin of retired BBC television news presenter Peter Snow. [ Snow Jon Shooting History Harper Perennial 3 May 2005 London 352 http://www.amazon.co.uk/dp/0007171854 0-00-717185-4 ] [ Education ] Snow was educated at independent school, Ardingly College, where his father was headmaster. He later attended the independent St Edward&apos;s School in Oxford. At age 18 he was for a year a VSO volunteer teaching in northern Uganda. [ http://www.davidrowan.com/2005/06/interview-jon-snow-channel-4-news_29.html Interview: Jon Snow, Channel 4 News David Rowan Evening Standard 2011-01-13 ] After mixed success in his first attempt to pass his A level qualifications he moved to the Yorkshire Coast College, Scarborough, where he later obtained the necessary qualifications to gain a place studying law at the University of Liverpool. However, he did not complete the degree, being rusticated for his part in a student protest. However he was awarded an honorary degree of Doctor of Letters at Liverpool University in 2011. { University of Liverpool Alumni - Jon Snow } [ Life and career ] After leaving Liverpool, Snow worked for New Horizon Youth Centre, a day centre for homeless young people in central London - an organisation with which he has remained involved and of which he subsequently became chairman. Snow was an assistant to Lord Longford. [ January 2010 ] [ Journalist ] Snow in June 2007. He served as ITN&apos;s Washington correspondent (1983–1986) and as diplomatic editor (1986–1989) before becoming the main presenter of Channel 4 News in 1989. In 1992 he was the main anchor for ITN&apos;s Election Night programme, broadcast on ITV; he presented the programme alongside Sir Robin Day, Alastair Stewart, and Julia Somerville. (Previously ITN&apos;s programme had typically been presented by Sir Alastair Burnet, who left ITN in 1991. The 1992 election night programme was the only one hosted by Snow. He was replaced by Jonathan Dimbleby from 1997 onwards.) He has won several RTS Awards - two for reports from El Salvador, one for his reporting of the Kegworth air disaster, and two as &quot; Presenter of the Year &quot;. [ November 2010 ] In 2002 he returned to radio, presenting Jon Snow Reports on Oneword Radio, a weekly show and podcast. He wrote regular articles for the Channel 4 News website, and writes &apos;Snowmail&apos; – a daily email newsletter on the big stories coming up on the evening edition of Channel 4 News. He is known for his vast collection of colourful ties and socks. { &quot; Why I love Jon Snow &quot;, Sarah Dempster, } { Guardian.co.uk, May 17, 2007; retrieved June 13, 2010 } On 14 June 2011, Snow presented the multiple award-winning investigative documentary Sri Lanka&apos;s Killing Fields, directed by Callum Macrae, which documented war crimes committed in the finals days of the Sri Lankan conflict in 2009. The program consisted of some of the most horrific footage ever broadcast in the UK; its second part, Sri Lanka&apos;s Killing Fields: War Crimes Unpunished was broadcast in March 2012. { http://www.channel4.com/programmes/sri-lankas-killing-fields/episode-guide/series-1/episode-1 } Snow declined an OBE because he believes working journalists should not take honours from those that they report on. He has recounted how whilst Amin appeared to be asleep he thought seriously about taking Amin&apos;s revolver and shooting him dead, but was worried about the consequences of firing a loose round in a jet. { Desert Island Discs interview, time:0:29:25, BBC Radio 4, 30 January 2011 } In 1976, Snow rejected an approach by the British intelligence services to spy on his colleagues. At first he was asked to supply information about the Communist Party, but he was then asked to spy on certain &quot; left-wing people &quot; working in television. He refuses to wear any symbol which may represent his views on air; in the run up to Remembrance Day he condemned what he called &apos;Poppy Fascism&apos; because &quot; in the end there really must be more important things in life than whether a news presenter wears symbols on his lapels &quot;. { Jon Snow Rails Against &apos;Poppy Fascism&apos; } On 28 February 2008 Snow courted controversy by claiming that the silence of the British media on the decision to allow Prince Harry to fight in Afghanistan was unacceptable with the following statement: { Channel 4 - News - Snowmail: Prince Harry in Afghanistan } &quot; I never thought I&apos;d find myself saying thank God for Drudge. The infamous US blogger has broken the best kept editorial secret of recent times. Editors have been sworn to secrecy over Prince Harry being sent to fight in Afghanistan three months ago. &quot; These remarks provoked criticism from some members of the public and other media outlets, [ http://www.dailymail.co.uk/pages/live/articles/news/news.html?in_article_id%3D522552%26amp%3Bin_page_id%3D1770%26amp%3Bct%3D5 London the Daily Mail Becky Barrow 2011-02-09 ] [ August 2010 RjwilmsiBot ] with some accusing Snow of wishing to undermine the safety of the Prince and his troops, and (according to Snow himself at the end of that day&apos;s bulletin, and later reported in the Daily Mail ) one captain in the armed forces called Snow&apos;s actions &quot; treason &quot;. { IMG.dailymail.co.uk } On 9 February 2009, Snow interviewed Lt-Col Yvonne Bradley, the military counsel for Binyam Mohamed, a British resident detained for five years at Guantanamo Bay. Snow asked if Mohamed&apos;s allegations of torture were justified. Lt-Col Bradley said there was no doubt at all that he had been tortured. [ November 2010 ] Mohamed was released and returned to Britain on 23 February 2009. In November 2010 Snow was sent to Haiti to report on the cholera outbreak. [ November 2010 ] [ Personal life ] Snow was briefly engaged in 1979 to fellow ITN journalist Anna Ford, who later became an equally high profile television news presenter. For 35 years his partner was human rights lawyer Madeleine Colvin, with whom he has two daughters. In March 2010 he married Precious Lunga, a ­Zimbabwe-born academic.
