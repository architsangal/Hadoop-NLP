Hawkins } { Henry Waxman } { Member of the } { California State Assembly } { 1976 } { 1991 } { Democratic } { Non-denominational } { Protestantism } { Sid Williams } { Los Angeles, California, United States } Maxine Waters (born Maxine Moore Carr; August 15, 1938) is the U.S. Representative for [ CA 35 ], and previously the 29th district, serving since 1991. She is a member of the Democratic Party. She is the most senior of the 12 black women currently serving in the United States Congress, and is a member and former chair of the Congressional Black Caucus. Before Congress she served in the California Assembly, where she was first elected in 1976. As an Assembly member Waters advocated for divestment from South Africa &apos;s apartheid regime. In Congress she was an outspoken opponent of the Iraq War. Waters was charged by the House&apos;s subcommittee on ethics with violations of the House&apos;s ethics rules in 2010. [ Early life, education, and early political career ] The fifth out of thirteen children, Waters was born 1938 in Kinloch, Missouri, [ http://www.stltoday.com/news/local/govt-and-politics/political-fix/article_cc2b7804-f3fc-11df-a219-0017a4a78c22.html Next up for House Ethics trial: St. Louis native Maxine Waters stltoday November 19, 2010 2 April 2011 ] to Remus and Velma Lee Carr Moore. She graduated from Vashon High School in St. Louis, and moved with her family to Los Angeles, California, in 1961. She worked in a garment factory and as a telephone operator before being hired as an assistant teacher with the Head Start program at Watts in 1966. She later enrolled at Los Angeles State College (now California State University, Los Angeles ), and graduated with a sociology degree in 1970. In 1973, she went to work as chief deputy to City Councilman David S. Cunningham, Jr.. Waters entered the California State Assembly in 1976. While in the assembly she worked for divestment of state pension funds from any businesses active in South Africa, a country then operating under the policy of apartheid and helped pass legislation within the guidelines of the divestment campaign &apos;s Sullivan Principles. [ Iraq War ] Waters voted against the Iraq War Resolution, the 2002 resolution that funded and granted Congressional approval to possible military action against the regime of Saddam Hussein. [ http://clerk.house.gov/evs/2002/roll455.xml Final Vote Results for Roll Call 455, H J RES 114 To Authorize the Use of United States Armed Forces Against Iraq 2009-03-14 Clerk of the United States House of Representatives 10-Oct-2002 ] She has remained a consistent critic of the subsequent war and has supported an immediate troop withdrawal from Iraq. Waters asserted in 2007 that President George W. Bush was trying to &quot; set [Congress] up &quot; by continually requesting funds for an &quot; occupation &quot; that is &quot; draining &quot; the country of capital, soldier&apos;s lives, and other resources. In particular, she argued that the very economic resources being &quot; wasted &quot; in Iraq were those that might provide universal health care or fully fund President Bush&apos;s own &quot; No Child Left Behind &quot; education bill. Additionally, Waters, representing a congressional district whose median income falls far below the national average, argued that patriotism alone had not been the sole driving force for those U.S. service personnel serving in Iraq. Rather, &quot; many of them needed jobs, they needed resources, they needed money, so they&apos;re there. &quot; In a subsequent floor speech, Waters told her colleagues that Congress, lacking the votes to override the &quot; inevitable Bush veto on any Iraq-related legislation &quot;, needed to &quot; better [challenge] the administration&apos;s false rhetoric about the Iraq war &quot; and &quot; educate our constituents [about] the connection between the problems in Pakistan, Turkey, and Iran with the problems we have created in Iraq. &quot; A few months prior to these speeches Waters became a cosponsor of the House resolution to impeach Vice President Dick Cheney for making allegedly &quot; false statements &quot; about the war. { Washington Times – Cheney ouster gains backers } [ International lending ] In August 2008, Waters introduced HR 6796, or the &quot; Stop Very Unscrupulous Loan Transfers from Underprivileged countries from Rich Exploitive Funds Act &quot;, also known as the Stop VULTURE Funds Act.
