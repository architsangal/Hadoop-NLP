The population was 500 at the 2000 census. Some blues scholars maintain that there is a &quot; Bentonia School &quot; of blues singing and guitar-playing, and that &quot; Bentonia-style &quot; is a distinct style of delta blues, but Bentonia lies outside the Mississippi Delta proper. The &quot; Bentonia School, &quot; &quot; Bentonia-style &quot; or &quot; Bentonia Blues &quot; is more accurately described as the unique, haunting, country blues style that originated in and immediately around the small town of Bentonia. [ http://bentoniablues.com Bentonia Blues - unique, haunting, country blues still heard in Bentonia, Mississippi bentoniablues.com 2009-08-20 ] The annual Bentonia Blues Festival is held the third Saturday of June in downtown Bentonia. [ http://bentoniablues.com/BentoniaBluesFestival.shtml The Official Bentonia Blues Festival Webpage bentoniablues.com 2009-08-20 ] The festival&apos;s Blues Stage is always set up in front of the world-famous Blue Front Cafe, which is still operated by proprietor Jimmy &quot; Duck &quot; Holmes who, as a young man, largely learned the local style from Bentonia Blues master Jack Owens. [ Demographics ] As of the census [ 2 ] of 2000, there were 500 people, 203 households, and 130 families residing in the town. The population density was 366.0 people per square mile (140.9/km²). There were 214 housing units at an average density of 156.6 per square mile (60.3/km²). The racial makeup of the town was 62.80% White, 35.80% African American, 0.20% Asian, 0.20% from other races, and 1.00% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 0.40% of the population. There were 203 households out of which 29.6% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 38.9% were married couples living together, 21.7% had a female householder with no husband present, and 35.5% were non-families. 33.5% of all households were made up of individuals and 16.3% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.46 and the average family size was 3.15. In the town the population was spread out with 25.2% under the age of 18, 7.8% from 18 to 24, 25.6% from 25 to 44, 23.8% from 45 to 64, and 17.6% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 40 years. For every 100 females there were 79.9 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 76.4 males. The median income for a household in the town was $21,458, and the median income for a family was $39,583. Males had a median income of $28,000 versus $26,875 for females. The per capita income for the town was $12,440. About 23.3% of families and 27.4% of the population were below the poverty line, including 36.5% of those under age 18 and 22.2% of those age 65 or over. [ Education ] The Town of Bentonia is served by the Yazoo County School District. [ Historical markers ] The Blue Front Cafe in Bentonia and native son Skip James have received historical markers by The Mississippi Blues Commission commemorating their importance in the history of the development of the blues in Mississippi. These markers are located in front of the Blue Front Cafe in downtown Bentonia and at the southeastern corner of the intersection of U.S. Route 49 and Mississippi Highway 433 West (one block from Osborne&apos;s Grocery), respectively. Both markers are officially on the Mississippi Blues Trail.
