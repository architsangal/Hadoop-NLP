[ September 2008 ] [ Dragon Ash Dragon Ash in 2005 group_or_band Tokyo, Japan Alternative rock, rap rock, hip-hop, drum and bass 1996–present Victor Entertainment/Mob Squad Kj Makoto Sakurai Bots Hiroki Dri-V Atsushi Iküzöne ] [ Dragon Ash ドラゴンアッシュ Doragon Asshu ] are a Japan ese rap rock group founded in 1996 by Furuya &quot; Kj &quot; Kenji and Sakurai Makoto. [ Dragon Ash Official Website : BIOGRAPHY 1996 Dragon Ash Official Website Japanese http://www.dragonash.co.jp/biography/?y%3D1996 2010-12-22 ] They are an icon in Japan and were one of the first groups to popularize hip hop in Japan. They brought a western flavor to Japanese music and helped to turn rap music mainstream, [ December 2010 ] with a mixture of reggae, rap, metal, punk, Spanish, and R &amp; B influences. Some of their best known songs include &quot; Shizuka na Hibi no Kaidan Wo, &quot; Grateful Days &quot;, &quot; Life Goes On &quot;, and &quot; I Love Hip Hop &quot;. [ December 2010 ] Kenji has stated in several interviews [ December 2010 ] that the reason Dragon Ash started out as a pseudo-punk band was because, at the time, punk was popular in Japan, and his handlers felt that the hip hop image wouldn&apos;t sell. He has also noted that hip hop has always been first in his book, but he did what was necessary to become successful in the industry. Dragon Ash is a member of Mob Squad, which is an affiliation with the bands Source and Endive. [ History ] Dragon Ash was formed by Kenji Furuya and Makoto Sakurai when the two were in junior high. [ Mills Ted Dragon Ash biography at Allmusic.com allmusic.com http://www.allmusic.com/artist/dragon-ash-p484032 2010-12-22 ] In 1996, they held an audition and eventually selected bassist Ikuzou Baba to join the band. At the time, Furuya and Sakurai were still only teenagers, while Baba was 30 years old, [ Dragon Ash（ドラゴンアッシュ）-　原点回帰ではない今のDAの音 - インタビュー Excite! Music Japanese http://www.excite.co.jp/music/close_up/interview/1012_dragonash/?lead%3D2 2010-12-22 ] and was likely selected to act as a mentor for the group. They debuted in February 1997 with the mini single The Day Dragged On. [ Dragon Ashのプロフィール・ヒストリーならオリコン芸能人事典-ORICON STYLE Oricon Style Japanese http://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/artist/7046/profile/history/ 2010-12-22 ] Later the same year, they released their first album, Mustang!. In 1999, Bots joined the group, and that March the band made their first big break with their 4th single, &quot; Let yourself go, let myself go, &quot; which hit #7 on the Oricon Weekly Singles chart, and eventually climbed to #4, selling a total of 640,000 copies. [ 検索結果-ORICON STYLE アーティスト/CD検索 Oricon Style Japanese http://www.oricon.co.jp/search/result.php?kbn%3Djs%26amp%3Btypes%3Drnk%26amp%3Byear%3D1999%26amp%3Bmonth%3D3%26amp%3Bweek%3D3%26amp%3Bsubmit4.x%3D17%26amp%3Bsubmit4.y%3D17 2010-12-22 ] In May they simultaneously released their 5th and 6th singles, &quot; Grateful Days &quot; and &quot; I Love Hip Hop, &quot; which debuted at #3 and #4, respectively. [ 検索結果-ORICON STYLE アーティスト/CD検索 Oricon Style Japanese http://www.oricon.co.jp/search/result.php?kbn%3Djs%26amp%3Btypes%3Drnk%26amp%3Byear%3D1999%26amp%3Bmonth%3D5%26amp%3Bweek%3D2%26amp%3Bsubmit4.x%3D15%26amp%3Bsubmit4.y%3D10 2010-12-22 ] When these two singles debuted, &quot; &quot; Let yourself go, let myself go &quot; had not yet dropped off the top 10 chart, giving Dragon Ash 3 top 10 singles at the same time. &quot; Grateful Days &quot; would eventually go on to #1, becoming both the first hip hop work and the first mixture band single to reach the #1 spot. The band finally reached the peak of their success when they released their third album, Viva la Revolution, in July and it hit #1 on its debut week. [ 検索結果-ORICON STYLE アーティスト/CD検索 Oricon Style Japanese http://www.oricon.co.jp/search/result.php?kbn%3Dja%26amp%3Btypes%3Drnk%26amp%3Byear%3D1999%26amp%3Bmonth%3D7%26amp%3Bweek%3D5%26amp%3Bsubmit5.x%3D22%26amp%3Bsubmit5.y%3D15 2010-12-22 ] The band continued to be successful throughout the turn of the century, collaborating with the hip hop group Rappagariya on Deep Impact in 2000 and consistently pulling in #1 spots on both the singles and albums charts with their new releases. In 2003 the group expanded to include it&apos;s full current roster of members, bringing in Hiroki, Atsushi, and Dri-V. In 2007 they celebrated their 10th anniversary by simultaneously releasing two best-of albums, The Best of Dragon Ash with Changes Vol.1 and The Best of Dragon Ash with Changes Vol.2. Both albums hit #1 upon their debut. [ Dragon Ashのベスト2作が初日1、2位独占！ Oricon Style Japanese http://www.oricon.co.jp/news/entertainment/47573/ 2010-12-22 ] [ Influences ] The group uses various samples, most notably from the famous American alternative rock group, The Smashing Pumpkins. [ December 2010 ] These songs include &quot; Cherub Rock &quot;, in which they sample the bass line from The Smashing Pumpkins song by the same name, and &quot; Grateful Days &quot; in which they sample the opening of &quot; Today &quot;; they also named the song &quot; Siva &quot; after the Pumpkins song. [ Side Projects ] Dragon Ash has gained some popularity outside Japan when the song Shizuka na Hibi no Kaidan Wo was used as the ending theme for the popular Japanese movie, Battle Royale. [ December 2010 ] Kenji Furuya has also worked on several side projects in recent years, including being the frontman of Steady &amp; Co., a Japanese hip hop and music production group consisting of himself, Bots, Ilmari of Rip Slyme, and Shigeo of SBK (Skebo King). [ December 2010 ] In addition to Steady &amp; Co., Furuya has also collaborated with other Japanese hip hop artists on the Mob Squad album and subsequent label. [ December 2010 ] [ Members ] Kenji &quot; Kj &quot; Furuya – vocals, guitar 1996–present Makoto Sakurai – drums 1996–present Bots – DJ 1999–present Hiroki – electric guitar 2003–present Dri-V – dance 2003–present Atsushi – dance 2003–present Former member Iküzöne (馬場育三, Baba Ikuzou) died on April 21, 2012 due to acute heart failure, he was 46. He was found by family members in his home studio and rushed to a central Tokyo hospital, but was pronounced dead at 22:55. A private funeral service for family was held.
