[ April 2012 ] [ April 2012 ] [ 1940 7 26 y ] { Belfast, } { United Kingdom } { Conservative } { Queen&apos;s University of Belfast } { Royal Free Hospital } { Christianity } Brian Stanley Mawhinney, Baron Mawhinney PC (born 26 July 1940) [ Sir Brian Mawhinney BBC News 18 October 2002 http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/2061231.stm 2008-04-23 Conservative Mp ] is a British Conservative Party politician. He was a member of the Cabinet from 1994 until 1997 and a Member of Parliament (MP) from 1979 until 2005. [ Early life ] Mawhinney was born in 1940 in Belfast and was educated at the Royal Belfast Academical Institution. [ Mawhinney, Brian Guardian Media Group http://politics.guardian.co.uk/person/parliament/0%2C%2C-3433%2C00.html 2008-04-23 London ] He studied physics at Queen&apos;s University of Belfast, gaining an upper second class degree in 1963 and obtained a Ph.D. in radiation physics at the Royal Free Hospital in London. He worked as assistant professor of radiation research at the University of Iowa from 1968 –70 and then returned to the Royal Free Hospital School of Medicine as a lecturer from 1970 –84. [ Political career ] Mawhinney was Member of Parliament for Peterborough from 1979 to 1997 and Member of Parliament for North West Cambridgeshire from 1997 to 2005. [ …with 27 new working peers… Telegraph Media Group http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/1489964/with-27-new-working-peers.html 2008-04-23 London ] Mawhinney campaigned prolifically against pornography. In 1979 one of his bills were in the Private Members’ Bills ballot, which attempted to ban indecent displays outside cinemas, sex shops and strip clubs. In early 1980, he called for Keith Joseph to launch an inquiry into certain pages on the Post Office’s Prestel viewdata service, calling it a &quot; buyers guide to dirty books &quot;. In the early 1980s he was secretary of the Conservative backbench Northern Ireland committee, during which time he showed wariness of the Rev Ian Paisley, warning his supporters that the British commitment to Ulster could be damaged by &quot; protestant ingratitude &quot;. He was PPS to John Wakeham from 1982 to 1983 and PPS to Tom King from 1984 to 1986. He became a junior minister at the Northern Ireland Office in 1986, and then became Minister of State at the Northern Ireland Office in 1990. In 1992, he became Minister of State at the Department of Health until 1994 when he entered the cabinet as Secretary of State for Transport. He served as Chairman of the Conservative Party and Minister without Portfolio for two years from 1995 until the 1997 election. He was appointed a Knight Bachelor in the dissolution honours list in 1997. He served as Shadow Home Secretary and spokesman for home, constitutional and legal affairs for a year under William Hague before returning to the back benches in June 1998. He stepped down from the House of Commons in May 2005. [ Mawhinney to leave Parliament 30 September 2003 BBC News http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/3152112.stm 2007-12-21 ] [ End of Commons road for four MPs 10 April 2005 BBC News http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/vote_2005/england/4430979.stm 2007-08-04 ] On 13 May 2005 it was announced that he would be created a life peer, [ Full list of new life peers 13 May 2005 BBC News http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/4544507.stm 2007-08-04 ] and on 24 June he was created Baron Mawhinney, of Peterborough, in the County of Cambridgeshire. [ Life baronies 06 August 2005 The Times http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/life_and_style/court_and_social/the_hitch/article552069.ece 2007-08-04 London ] [ September 2010 RjwilmsiBot ] [ Outside politics ] In 2003, he was appointed Chairman of The Football League, [ Mawhinney handed top post 19 December 2002 BBC Sport http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/2587671.stm 2007-08-04 ] and in 2004 oversaw a re-organisation of the league structure, renaming the former Division One as the Football League Championship. Deeply religious, Mawhinney is a leading member of the Conservative Christian Fellowship and was a member of the General Synod for five years.
