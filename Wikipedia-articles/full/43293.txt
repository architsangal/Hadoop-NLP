[ September 2010 ] [ 1947 10 11 y ] { Sydney, New South Wales, Australia } { University of Sydney } { journalist, historian } { Ros Kelly Margaret } { Joseph, Daniel } { 1971–present } Paul John Kelly (born 11 October 1947) is an Australian political journalist and historian from Sydney. He has worked in a variety of roles, principally for The Australian newspaper, and is currently its Editor-at-large. He has written several books on political events since the 1970s including on the Australian constitutional crisis of 1975. His latest book, The March of Patriots, chronicles the creation of a modern Australia during the 1991–2007 era of Prime Ministers, Paul Keating and John Howard. Kelly is a political commentator on radio and television and presented the Australian Broadcasting Corporation (ABC) TV documentary series, 100 Years – The Australian Story (2001) and wrote a book of the same title. In 2006, Kelly&apos;s work was described by fellow Australian journalists, Toby Creswell and Samantha Trenoweth, &quot; [It] is distinguished for his broad and deep grasp of the inter-relationship of economics and political shifts, and his ability to place Australian domestic developments into an international and historical context &quot;. [ 1001 Australians you should know Creswell Toby Toby Creswell Trenoweth Samantha Pluto Press Australia 2006 North Melbourne, Vic Media and Journalism 397 978-1-86403-361-8 ] [ Biography ] Paul John Kelly was born on 11 October 1947 in Sydney, New South Wales. [ Who&apos;s Who in Australia Crown Content 2009 Sullivan Leanne 45 978-1-74095-166-1 ] He is the son of Joseph Kelly and Sybil (née Mackenzie). He completed a Bachelor of Arts and Diploma of Education at the University of Sydney in 1969. He worked in the Prime Minister&apos;s Department in Canberra from 1969 to 1971 before changing to journalism. [ http://www.usyd.edu.au/senate/Kelly.shtml Honary awards – Paul John Kelly University of Sydney 14 May 2009 10 August 2010 ] He is a Doctor of Letters from the University of Melbourne. [ http://www.lowyinstitute.org/StaffBio.asp?pid%3D468 Paul Kelly – Lowy Institute Staff Member Lowy Institute for International Policy 12 August 2010 ] He joined the Canberra press gallery in 1971 and became chief political correspondent for The Australian from 1974 to 1975. From 1976 to 1978 he was chief political correspondent for the now-defunct Fairfax newspaper The National Times, then its deputy editor from 1978 to 1979. He became chief political correspondent for The Sydney Morning Herald from 1981 to 1984. He returned to The Australian and was its national affairs editor from 1985 to 1991, editor-in-chief from 1991 to 1996 and editor-at-large since 1996 to present. Aside from journalism, Kelly has written books describing political developments starting with The Unmaking of Gough (1976) on the Australian constitutional crisis of 1975 and Prime Minister Gough Whitlam (later titled The Dismissal : Australia&apos;s Most Sensational Power Struggle : The Dramatic Fall of Gough Whitlam). [ http://catalogue.nla.gov.au/Record/2035408 The dismissal : Australia&apos;s most sensational power struggle : the dramatic fall of Gough Whitlam / by Paul Kelly catalogue National Library of Australia 10 August 2010 ] He has written books on subsequent Prime Ministers, Bob Hawke (The Hawke Ascendency, 1984), Paul Keating (The End of Certainty, 1992) and John Howard (Howard&apos;s Decade, 2006). As from August 2010, his most recent book is The March of Patriots: The Struggle for Modern Australia (2009) which deals with economic and political developments under Keating and Howard as Australia entered the globalised age. [ http://www.lawbooks.com.au/book/the-keating-howard-years.do The March of Patriots LawBooks (Emporium Retail Group) 2009 12 August 2010 ] His book, The Dismissal was used as the basis of the television miniseries The Dismissal shown on Network Ten from 6 March 1983. Kelly is a political commentator on radio and television (including Insiders ) and presented the Australian Broadcasting Corporation (ABC) TV documentary series, 100 Years – The Australian Story (2001) and wrote a book of the same title. [ http://www.australian-politics-books.com/ccp0-prodshow/the-dismissal-paul-kelly-1975-tv.html The Dismissal – Paul Kelly 1983 Paperback Used – TV tie-in Australian Politics Books 12 August 2010 ] In November 1991, after the massacres at Santa Cruz (near Dili, East Timor ), Kelly had supported Indonesia n President Suharto and declared him to be a moderate with no alternative to his rule. [ http://www.smh.com.au/news/Books/In-praise-of-Soeharto-the-despot/2005/02/17/1108500195349.html In Praise of Soeharto the Despot The Sydney Morning Herald Fairfax Media 13 February 2005 12 August 2010 ] Kelly&apos;s support for Suharto continued to 1998 and earned criticism from fellow journalist John Pilger who compared it to the appeasement of Hitler in the 1930s. [ http://www.outlookindia.com/article.aspx?222768 Choskyist-Pilgerism Outlook Maheshwer Peri (The Outlook Group) 28 January 2004 12 August 2010 ] [ Personal life ] He is married to Margaret (née Leckie). They have two sons. Joseph and Daniel. Kelly was previously married to Australian Labor Party Federal Member of Parliament and Minister Ros Kelly (née Raw).
