Formed in 1995, the group incorporates aspects of progressive rock, punk rock, metal and post-hardcore. [ Coheed and Cambria Biography Allmusic January 25, 2008 ] [ http://www.scaruffi.com/vol7/coheed.html The History of Rock Music. Coheed and Cambria Scaruffi.com March 29, 2009 ] All of Coheed and Cambria&apos;s albums are concept album s that tell a science fiction storyline called The Amory Wars, a story written by lead singer Claudio Sanchez, which has been transcribed into a series of comic books, [ http://theamorywars.com/about-the-amory-wars/ The Amory Wars official website. ] as well as a full length novel. The band has released five studio albums, three live albums, and several special-edition releases. In 2008, the band held a four-day concert series, Neverender, during which they played their first four studio albums in succession. As of November 2011 Coheed and Cambria are currently working on an as-yet untitled sixth studio album. [ History ] [ Shabütie (1995–2001) ] In March 1995, after the split of Claudio Sanchez and Travis Stever &apos;s band called Toxic Parents, they formed a band with Nate Kelley called Beautiful Loser. The band featured Stever on vocals and guitar, Sanchez on guitar, Kelley on drums and Jon Carleo on bass. The group was short-lived, breaking up by June 1995 after an argument over gas money. [ http://dontpassthefence.org/shabutie.html Dontpassthefence.org Shabutie/Coheed and Cambria Knowlegebase January 27, 2008 ] Stever left the band, and the resulting trio was named Shabütie, [ http://www.adequacy.net/interview.php?InterviewID%3D56 Interview With Coheed and Cambria Delusions of Adequacy http://web.archive.org/web/20080202075046/http%3A//www.adequacy.net/interview.php?InterviewID%3D56 February 2, 2008 January 27, 2008 ] a word taken from African tribe chants that means &quot; naked prey &quot; in the film The Naked Prey. The band spent nearly a year experimenting with a multitude of different musical styles, including punk rock, indie rock, acoustic rock, funk, and heavy metal. When Carleo left the band in August 1996, Kelley recruited Michael Todd to take his place. Todd, who was primarily a guitarist, picked up the bass specifically for Shabütie. [ http://www.saveyourscene.com/web/view.php?table%3Dinterviews%26amp%3Bshow%3D4 Interview with Claudio SaveYourScene January 27, 2008 ] As Shabütie, the band wrote dozens of songs and released their first studio demo Plan to Take Over the World in 1999. The band also released The Penelope EP in 1999, shortly after which Stever rejoined the band. Kelley left the band during a performance in late 1999. Josh Eppard (then the drummer of his brother&apos;s, Joey Eppard, band 3 ) replaced him. The band went on to release Delirium Trigger in 2000, still featuring Kelley on the drums, but listing Eppard in the liner notes. [ shabutie.8m.com/custom.html http://web.archive.org/web/20010501174618/shabutie.8m.com/custom.html Home Page Shabutie January 27, 2008 May 1, 2001 ] [ Starting out (2001–2004) ] &quot; The Keywork &quot;, a commonly used logo for the band, symbolizes the energy stream between the planets in Coheed and Cambria&apos;s fictional universe. Several songs that appeared on Delirium Trigger were based on a series of science fiction comics written by Claudio Sanchez called The Bag.On.Line Adventures, which was later renamed The Amory Wars. Sanchez&apos;s side project originally developed during a 1998 trip to Paris, where the band members decided to rename themselves Coheed and Cambria, named after two of the story&apos;s protagonists, and adopted the concept story as a theme that would unify their future albums. [ Musical style and influences ] Sanchez has several times stated he is envious of his father&apos;s era of music, and that the band is influenced by groups of that era, such as Led Zeppelin, Pink Floyd, The Police, Misfits, Queen, and Thin Lizzy. Apart from these roots in classic rock, Sanchez also acknowledges an eclectic array of influences, including post-hardcore group At the Drive-In, and heavy metal pioneers Iron Maiden.
