[ Adams County Pennsylvania 1800 January 22 Gettysburg Gettysburg 522 1352 520 1347 2 5 0.29% 2010 101407 194.27 75 Eastern -5 -4 www.adamscounty.us John Adams, 2nd President of the United States Adams PA Courthouse 1.JPG 250 Adams County Courthouse ] Adams County is a county in the U.S. state of Pennsylvania. As of the 2010 census, the population was 101,407. It was created on January 22, 1800, from part of York County and named in honor of the second President of the United States, John Adams. Its county seat is Gettysburg. The Bourough of Gettysburg is located at the center of Adams County. This county seat community is surrounded on three sides by the Gettysburg National Military Park (GNMP). The Eisenhower National Historic Site adjoins GNMP on its southwest edge. Most of Adams County&apos;s rural landscapes and its mid-19th century roadway pattern remain intact today. Thirteen historic roadways converge at or near Gettysburg Borough. Two circular rings of towns surround Gettysburg; the first ring is typically found at a distance of about [ 7 mi km ] form Gettysburg. The second ring is found at a distance of 12 to [ 15 mi km ] from the County Seat. This &quot; spokes and wheel &quot; pattern represents one of the few examples of Central Place Theory in the Eastern United States. The county is in the watershed of the Chesapeake Bay and is drained by the Susquehanna River and Potomac River. [ Transportation ] [ Bus transportation ] Public bus service in Adams County is available through the Adams County Transit Authority. [ Road transportation ] [ Interstates ] No Interstates pass through Adams County. [ US Highway System ] U.S. Route 15 enters Adams County, Pennsylvania south of Gettysburg. Business Route 15 (Emmitsburg Road) goes through Gettysburg, while US 15 bypasses the borough. The bypass continues to York Springs. US 15 then leaves Adams County and passes through Dillsburg in York County. U.S. Route 30, the historic Lincoln Highway passes through Adams County.
