{ Oblast Duma } { legislature_ref } { http://www.region.tver.ru/ } { website_ref } { March 2011 } Tver Oblast ( [ Тверска́я о́бласть ], Tverskaya oblast) is a federal subject of Russia (an oblast ). Its administrative center is the city of Tver. From 1935 to 1990, it was named Kalinin Oblast ( [ ru Кали́нинская о́бласть ] ) after Mikhail Kalinin. Population: 1,353,392 ( 2010 Census ). [ 2010Census ] Tver Oblast is an area of lakes, such as Seliger and Brosno. Much of its area is occupied by the Valdai Hills, where the Volga, the Western Dvina, and the Dnieper have their source. Tver Oblast is one of the tourist regions of Russia with modernized tourist infrastructure. There are also many historic towns: Torzhok, Toropets, Zubtsov, Kashin, Vyshny Volochyok, and Kalyazin. The oldest of these is Rzhev, primarily known for the Battles of Rzhev of the World War II. Staritsa was a seat of the last appanage principality in Russia. Ostashkov is a major tourist center. [ Administrative divisions ] [ Administrative divisions of Tver Oblast ] [ Economy ] Tver Oblast has a very strong economy due to its machinery industry in the automobile and aeronautics sectors. [ Tver Karelians ] A branch of Karelians, known as Tver Karelians used to live in the oblast. They numbered 140,567 in 1926. Due to the heavy casualties suffered during the World War II, they vanished as a separate ethnic group from most parts of the oblast.
