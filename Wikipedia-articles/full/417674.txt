It is the commercial centre of Jasper National Park, located in the Canadian Rockies in the Athabasca River valley. Jasper is [ 362 km mi ] west of Edmonton. Jasper is [ 290 km mi ] north of Banff, Alberta, with the Icefields Parkway connecting the Jasper townsite to Lake Louise in Banff National Park. It was established as a specialized municipality on July 20, 2001 { Jasper municipal profile - } { Alberta Municipal Affairs } and the governance of Jasper is shared between the Municipality of Jasper and the federal Parks Canada agency. { Municipality of Jasper - About Jasper } [ History ] Established in 1813, Jasper House was first a North West Company, and later Hudson&apos;s Bay Company, fur trade outpost on the York Factory Express trade route to what was then called &quot; New Caledonia &quot; (now British Columbia ), and Fort Vancouver in Columbia District on the lower Columbia River. { Parks Canada - Jasper House National Historic Site } Jasper National Park was established in 1907. The railway siding at the location of the future townsite was established by Grand Trunk Pacific Railway in 1911 and originally named Fitzhugh after a Grand Trunk vice president { http://albertajasper.com/Jasper-Alberta-Information-Centre-History.htmlz } (along the Grand Trunk&apos;s &quot; alphabet &quot; line ). The Canadian Northern Railway also began service to Fitzhugh in 1912. [ A Brief History of Canada&apos;s National Parks Lothian, W.F. Environment Canada 1987 ] The townsite was surveyed in 1913 by H. Matheson, and subsequently renamed Jasper after the former fur trade post. By 1931, Jasper was accessible by road from Edmonton, and in 1940 the scenic Icefields Parkway opened, connecting Banff and Jasper. [ Geography ] Jasper town site Jasper is located in the Athabasca River valley, at the confluence with Miette River. It lies between the Victoria Cross Ranges (northwest), Pyramid Mountain (north), Maligne Range (southeast) and Indian Ridge (southwest). Jasper is connected to the west via the Yellowhead Highway and the Yellowhead Pass to Prince George and to the east to Edmonton. Toward the south, the Icefield Parkway leads to Banff and Banff National Park. Located near Jasper are Pyramid Lake and Patricia Lake, as well as Lake Annette, Lake Edith, Lac Beauvert, Maligne Lake, Medicine Lake and numerous other smaller lakes. The Jasper Tramway, which takes visitors to The Whistlers &apos; summit, and the Marmot Basin ski resort are also located near the town, as is the Fairmont Jasper Park Lodge. The Jasper-Yellowhead Museum and Archives is located in the Jasper town site, as is the Jasper Visitor Centre. [ Transportation ] { Jasper railway station, seen from Connaught Drive } Jasper railway station is served by Via Rail with two passenger services. The Canadian and the Jasper – Prince Rupert train both operate three times per week.
