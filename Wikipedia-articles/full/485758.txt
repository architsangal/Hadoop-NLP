She is the eighth ship to bear the name and is the fifteenth and penultimate ship of the &apos;Duke&apos; class of frigates. [ Operational history ] The ship was accepted into service by the Royal Navy on 15 December 2000 and was commissioned on 3 May the following year. Present at the commissioning ceremony was Portland s sponsor Lady Brigstoke, wife of Admiral Sir John Brigstoke, a former Second Sea Lord, who had also launched the ship in 1999. { http://www.btinternet.com/~warship/News/3may012.htm } &apos;&apos;Portland&apos;&apos; in Plymouth Naval Base, 2010 During sea trials Portland attained a top speed of [ 30.8 kn km/h ], the fastest speed attained by any Type 23 frigate. { http://www.royal-navy.mod.uk/operations-and-support/surface-fleet/type-23-frigates/hms-portland/index.htm } Portland assisted in the search for men lost from a capsized yacht on 3 February 2007. [ http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/devon/6326795.stm Man killed when vessel capsizes BBC News 2007-02-03 2007-02-03 ] She was deployed to the Caribbean for seven months in 2007, intercepting 3.5 tonnes of cocaine in cooperation with a United States Coast Guard Law Enforcement Detachment (LEDET) and conducting disaster relief in Belize following Hurricane Dean. [ http://www.mod.uk/DefenceInternet/DefenceNews/MilitaryOperations/HmsPortlandInDramaticAtlanticDrugsBustvideo.htm Minstry of Defense HMS Portland in dramatic Atlantic drugs bust 2010-04-30 ] In April 2008, Portland visited Liverpool with HMS &apos;&apos;Mersey&apos;&apos; and berthed at the cruise liner terminal at Princes Dock. [ http://www.shippingtimes.co.uk/itm164_MERSEY.htm Shipping Times HMS Portland and HMS Mersey to visit Liverpool 2008-04-07 http://web.archive.org/web/20080802032715/http%3A//www.shippingtimes.co.uk/itm164_MERSEY.htm 2008-08-02 ] In June 2009 while taking part in anti-piracy operations off the Horn of Africa, Portland intercepted ten pirates. However, as they were not caught in the immediate act of piracy the vessel was unable legally to detain them.
