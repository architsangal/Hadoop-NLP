[ February 2012 ] [ 1958 5 23 y ] { Belleville, Illinois } { Comedienne Actress } { Jazz musician } Lea DeLaria (born May 23, 1958) is an American comedienne, actress, and jazz musician. [ http://www.hollywood.com/celebrity/Lea_DeLaria/191438 Lea DeLaria Hollywood.com 2011-06-28 ] [ http://www.filmreference.com/film/88/Lea-DeLaria.html Lea DeLaria Biography Film Reference Advameg 2011-06-28 ] [ http://www.breakupgirl.net/guest/9907.html &quot; You &amp; A Guest &quot; Interview: Lea DeLaria Breakupgirl.net July 15, 1999 2011-06-28 ] The &quot; famously controversial &quot; DeLaria was &quot; the first openly gay comic to break the late-night talk-show barrier &quot; with her 1993 appearance on The Arsenio Hall Show. [ http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0%2C%2C308870%2C00.html Trend: Gay stand-up comedians Cagle Jess Entertainment Weekly Time Warner December 10, 1993 2011-06-28 ] DeLaria has performed stand-up comedy for over 25 years and is a prominent figure in the world of LGBT comedy. She began her career billing herself as &quot; That Fucking Dyke &quot;; she states &quot; I called myself that because I would walk down the street and people would yell (it) at me. But after two years of performing I would walk down the street and people would yell (it) at me and I wouldn&apos;t know if they were a fan or not! &quot; [ Early life ] The Italian American DeLaria was born in Belleville, Illinois, the daughter of Jerry, a homemaker, and Robert DeLaria, a jazz pianist and social worker. She attended kindergarten through 8th grade at St Mary&apos;s Elementary School in Belleville, and has referenced her Catholic upbringing in her performances. [ Career ] Of her watershed 1993 appearance on The Arsenio Hall Show, as the first openly gay comic to appear on a late-night talk-show, DeLaria said &quot; It&apos;s the 1990s... It&apos;s hip to be queer, and I&apos;m a bi-i-i-i-ig dyke! &quot; In December 1993 she hosted Comedy Central &apos;s Out There, the first all-gay stand-up comedy special. DeLaria is also known for her touring &quot; musical comedy about perverts, &quot; Dos Lesbos (1987–1989) as well as Girl Friday, a comedy she conceived, wrote, directed and starred in, and which won the 1989 Golden Gull for Best Comedy Group in Provincetown, Massachusetts. [ Lea DeLaria Post Laura AllMusic All Media Guide 2011-06-28 ] DeLaria has released two CD recordings of her comedy, Bulldyke in a China Shop (1994) and Box Lunch (1997). She has also written a humorous book entitled Lea&apos;s Book of Rules for the World. DeLaria appeared as Jane in the 1998 Off Broadway production of Paul Rudnick &apos;s The Most Fabulous Story Ever Told, a &quot; a gay retelling of the Bible. &quot; [ http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0%2C%2C20014533%2C00.html The Most Fabulous Story Ever Told Cagle Jess Entertainment Weekly Time Warner April 23, 1999 2011-06-28 ] Entertainment Weekly said &quot; a star is born with Lea DeLaria &quot; of her &quot; showstopping &quot; performance as Hildy Esterhazy in the 1998 Broadway revival of On the Town. [ http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res%3D9C06E1D71639F93AA15752C1A96E958260%26amp%3Bsec%3D%26amp%3Bspon%3D%26amp%3Bpagewanted%3D2 THEATER; An Exhilarating &apos;On the Town&apos; Spreads Some Joy Canby Vincent The New York Times The New York Times Company November 29, 1998 2011-06-28 ] [ http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0%2C%2C286011%2C00.html Little Me Cagle Jess Entertainment Weekly Time Warner December 4, 1998 2011-06-28 ] [ http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0%2C%2C273601%2C00.html Handicapping the 1999 Tony Awards Wontorek Paul Entertainment Weekly Time Warner June 4, 1999 2011-06-28 ] DeLaria subsequently played Eddie and Dr. Scott in the 2000 Broadway revival of The Rocky Horror Show, and can be heard on the cast recording. [ http://www.ibdb.com/person.php?id%3D69841 Lea DeLaria Internet Broadway Database The Broadway League 2011-06-28 ] DeLaria has also appeared in a number of films, including Edge of Seventeen and The First Wives Club. DeLaria integrates musical performance into her stand-up comedy, focusing on traditional and modern be-bop jazz. She has even said at her shows &quot; It is my personal goal to bring be-bop jazz back into the gay and lesbian community. &quot; [ August 2008 ] In 2001 she released a CD of jazz standards called Play It Cool. This was followed by the album Double Standards in 2003, [ http://www.bbc.co.uk/music/release/bh9x/ Lea DeLaria Double Standards Review Reynolds Nick BBC November 3, 2003 2011-06-28 ] [ http://www.amazon.com/Double-Standards-Lea-Delaria/dp/B0007QJ1F2/ref%3Dsr_1_1?ie%3DUTF8%26amp%3Bqid%3D1309283417%26amp%3Bsr%3D8-1 Double Standards Amazon.com 2011-06-28 ] and by The Very Best of Lea DeLaria in 2008. In 2001, DeLaria was the voice of Helga Phugly on the short-lived, animated sitcom, The Oblongs. In 1999 DeLaria played the recurring role of Madame Delphina on the ABC soap opera One Life to Live, returning in 2008 as both Delphina and Professor Delbert Fina. She continues to portray Delphina on a recurring basis as of 2011. [ http://soapnet.go.com/soapnet/print/path-contentID_7452?lid%3DprinterFriendly%26amp%3Blpos%3Darticle_7452 Madame Delphina Sees All Murray Jesse SOAPnet The Walt Disney Company July 1, 2008 2011-06-28 ] In 2008, Warner Records released The Live Smoke Sessions, DeLaria&apos;s first recording focused on &quot; timeless pop standards &quot; such as &quot; Down With Love, &quot; &quot; Night and Day, &quot; &quot; Love Me or Leave Me &quot; and &quot; Come Rain or Come Shine. &quot; She noted, &quot; I styled this CD on the old school live recordings... It is my hope that this CD will take you back to 1948 and the Village Vanguard. So please let me invite you to mix a cocktail and enjoy a smoke while you sit back and soak up the swing. &quot; [ http://broadwayworld.com/article/Lea_Delaria_Live_Smoke_Sessions_To_Be_Released_99_20080909 Lea Delaria: Live Smoke Sessions To Be Released 9/9 BroadwayWorld.com September 9, 2008 2011-06-28 ] In November 2008, DeLaria completed a tour of Australia, playing Sydney, Melbourne, Adelaide and Brisbane. She also frequently collaborates with comedian Maggie Cassella, most notably on an annual Christmas cabaret show in Toronto, Ontario which also sometimes tours to several other Canadian and US cities. In July 2010, DeLaria&apos;s version of &quot; All That Jazz &quot; was used on So You Think You Can Dance. DeLaria is currently performing in Prometheus Bound at The American Repertory Theater in Cambridge, Massachusetts.
