He has recorded prolifically for many record labels since the mid-1970s. [ http://bozemandailychronicle.com/articles/2006/03/16/features/music/02roberti.txt Best of the best, David Murray, presents workshop, concerts in Bozeman Staff Writer 2006-06-29 Bozeman Daily Chronicle June 29, 2006 ] [ Biography ] Murray was born in Oakland, California, USA. He was initially influenced by free jazz musicians such as Albert Ayler and Archie Shepp. He gradually evolved a more diverse style in his playing and compositions. Murray set himself apart from most tenor players of his generation by not taking John Coltrane as his model, choosing instead to incorporate elements of mainstream players Coleman Hawkins, Ben Webster and Paul Gonsalves into his mature style. [ http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res%3D9C00EFD8123BF934A15753C1A964948260 The Pop Life; David Murray Comes Into His Own Robert Palmer October 27, 1982 2006-06-29 New York Times ] Despite this, he recorded a tribute to Coltrane, Octet Plays Trane, in 1999. His 1996 tribute to the Grateful Dead, Dark Star, was also critically well received. [ http://www.musicbox-online.com/dm-dark.html Dark Star: The Music of the Grateful Dead John Metzger 2006-06-29 The Music Box Online ] Murray was a founding member of the World Saxophone Quartet with Oliver Lake, Julius Hemphill and Hamiet Bluiett. [ http://www.answers.com/topic/world-saxophone-quartet World Saxophone Quartet Chris Kelsey, Allmusic 2006-06-29 Answers.com ] He has recorded or performed with musicians such as Henry Threadgill, James Blood Ulmer, Olu Dara, Tani Tabbal, Butch Morris, Donal Fox, McCoy Tyner, Elvin Jones, Ed Blackwell, Johnny Dyani, and Steve McCall. David Murray&apos;s use of the circular breathing technique has enabled him to play astonishingly long phrases. [ http://www.bbc.co.uk/radio3/jazz/profiles/david_murray.shtml Jazz Profiles - David Murray Staff Writer 2006-06-29 BBC Radio 3 Jazz Profiles ] He is currently living in Sines, Portugal, and participates yearly in its FMM Music festival.
