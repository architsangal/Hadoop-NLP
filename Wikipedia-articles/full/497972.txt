[ 1951 10 28 y ] { Sliema, Malta } { Author, journalist } { British } { University of York } { Anglican Christian } { The Abolition of Britain } { The Broken Compass: How British Politics Lost its Way } { A Brief History of Crime } { The Rage Against God } { Christopher Hitchens (brother) } { http://hitchensblog.mailonsunday.co.uk } Peter Jonathan Hitchens (born 28 October 1951) is an award-winning [ http://www.newstatesman.com/blogs/cultural-capital/2010/05/orwell-prize-hitchens 20 May 2010 Peter Hitchens wins the Orwell Prize Daniel Trilling New Statesman 25 May 2010 ] British columnist and author, noted for his traditionalist conservative stance. He has published five books, including The Abolition of Britain, A Brief History of Crime, The Broken Compass and most recently The Rage Against God. Hitchens writes for Britain&apos;s The Mail on Sunday newspaper. A former resident correspondent in Moscow and Washington, Hitchens continues to work as an occasional foreign reporter, and appears frequently in the British broadcast media. He is the younger brother of the late US-based writer Christopher Hitchens. In 2010 Hitchens was described by Edward Lucas in The Economist as &quot; a forceful, tenacious, eloquent and brave journalist. Readers with long memories may remember his extraordinary coverage of the revolution in Romania in 1989, or more recently his intrepid travels to places such as North Korea. He lambasts woolly thinking and crooked behaviour at home and abroad &quot;. [ Early life ] Peter Hitchens was born in 1951 in Malta, where his father, who had been a commander during World War 2, [ http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b00t1v72#synopsis The House I Grew Up In – Peter Hitchens BBC Radio 4 19 July 2010 April 28 2012 ] was stationed with the Royal Navy. He was educated at The Leys School [ Hitchens 2010 8 ] and the Oxford College of Further Education [ http://www.pressgazette.co.uk/story.asp?storycode%3D34562 Toffs at the top Press Gazette 2006-06-16 2012-05-02 ] before doing a BA in Politics at the University of York, where he is said to have replied &quot; I was too busy starting a revolution &quot; when asked why he was late for a lecture. [ BBC News Online report: &quot; Veteran columnist quits Express &quot; http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk/1061015.stm 9 December 2000 2 November 2006 ] He married Eve Ross, the daughter of left-wing journalist David Ross, in 1983. [ December 2011 ] Hitchens originally hoped to become a naval officer, but an eye defect prevented him from doing so. [ http://nymag.com/nymetro/news/media/features/868/index3.html The Boy Can&apos;t Help It Nymag.com 1999-04-26 2012-04-23 ] [ Career in journalism ] Hitchens worked for the Daily Express between 1977 and 2000, initially as a reporter specialising in education and industrial and labour affairs, then as a political reporter, and subsequently as Deputy Political Editor. [ Silver James Look forward in anger The Guardian http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/2005/nov/14/mailonsunday.mondaymediasection 14 November 2005 13 April 2008 ] Leaving parliamentary journalism to cover defence and diplomatic affairs, he reported on the decline and collapse of communist regimes in several Warsaw Pact countries, which culminated in a stint as Moscow Correspondent and reporting on the final months of the Soviet Union in 1990/91. He then became the Daily Express Washington correspondent. Returning to London in 1995, he became a commentator and columnist. Hitchens continued to espouse a conservative viewpoint, despite the publication&apos;s move towards the political centre in the mid-90s and its decision before the 1997 general election to support the Labour Party under Tony Blair. In 2000, Hitchens left the Daily Express [ Hodgson, Jessica http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/2000/dec/07/pressandpublishing.dailyexpress1?INTCMP%3DSRCH Hitchens quits Express Guardian 7 December 2000 28 April 2012 ] after its acquisition by Richard Desmond; Hitchens stated that working for Desmond would have represented a moral conflict of interest. Hitchens joined The Mail on Sunday, where he has a weekly column and weblog in which he debates directly with readers. Hitchens has also written for The Spectator and The American Conservative magazines, [ http://www.amconmag.com/article/2008/sep/08/00014/ 8 September 2008 Back in the USSR Hitchens, Peter The American Conservative 25 May 2010 ] and occasionally for more left-leaning publications such as The Guardian, Prospect, and the New Statesman. After being shortlisted in 2007 and 2009, { Owen Amos announced for Orwell Prize for political writing &quot;, Press Gazette, 26 March 2009 } Hitchens won the Orwell Prize in political journalism in 2010.
