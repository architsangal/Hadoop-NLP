[ http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/c/cochrmi01.shtml Mickey Cochrane at Baseball Reference Baseball-reference.com 2010-11-23 ] He played in Major League Baseball as a catcher for the Philadelphia Athletics and Detroit Tigers. Cochrane was considered one of the best catchers in baseball history and is a member of the Baseball Hall of Fame. [ http://bioproj.sabr.org/bioproj.cfm?a%3Dv%26amp%3Bv%3Dl%26amp%3Bbid%3D139%26amp%3Bpid%3D2599 The Baseball Biography Project: Mickey Cochrane Bevis Charlie Society for American Baseball Research 13 April 2010 ] [ http://www.baseball-almanac.com/deaths/mickey_cochrane_obituary.shtml Mickey Cochrane Obituary at Baseball Almanac Baseball-almanac.com 2010-11-23 ] [ http://baseballhall.org/hof/cochrane-mickey Mickey Cochrane at The Baseball Hall of Fame Baseballhall.org 2010-11-23 ] [ Playing career ] [ Philadelphia Athletics ] { Jimmie Foxx, } { Babe Ruth and } { Lou Gehrig } Cochrane was born in Bridgewater, Massachusetts to Northern Irish immigrant John Cochrane, whose father had immigrated to Ulster from Scotland and Scottish immigrant Sadie Campbell. He was also known as &quot; Black Mike &quot;, because of his fiery, competitive nature. Cochrane was educated at Boston University where he played five sports, excelling at football and basketball. [ http://books.google.com/books?id%3DUJVas2JMjLUC%26amp%3Bpg%3DPA52%26amp%3Bdq%3Dmickey%2Bcochrane%26amp%3Bhl%3Den%26amp%3Bei%3DJRnES6m2MMH68AaG_onPDw%26amp%3Bsa%3DX%26amp%3Boi%3Dbook_result%26amp%3Bct%3Dresult%26amp%3Bresnum%3D6%26amp%3Bved%3D0CEUQ6AEwBQ#v%3Donepage%26amp%3Bq%3Dmickey%20cochrane%26amp%3Bf%3Dfalse &apos; &amp; #39;The Detroit Tigers Encyclopedia, Honoring a Detroit Legend&apos; &amp; #39;, by Jim Hawkins, Dan Ewald, George Van Dusen, Sports Publishing LLC, 2002, ISBN 1-58261-222-6, ISBN 978-1-58261-222-5 Books.google.com 2010-11-23 ] Although he considered himself better as a football player than as a baseball player, professional football wasn&apos;t as established as Major League Baseball at the time so, Cochrane signed a contract to play for the Portland Beavers of the Pacific Coast League in [ 1924 ]. [ http://www.baseball-reference.com/minors/player.cgi?id%3Dcochra001gor Mickey Cochrane minor league statistics Baseball-reference.com 2010-11-23 ] After just one season in the minor leagues, Cochrane was promoted to the major leagues, making his debut with the Philadelphia Athletics on April 14, 1925 at the age of 22. He made an immediate impact by earning the starting catcher&apos;s assignment over Cy Perkins, who was considered one of the best catchers in the major leagues at the time. [ Pick Mickey Cochrane As Biggest Find Of The Season The Southeast Missourian 9 20 August 1925 30 March 2011 http://news.google.com/newspapers?id%3DMndFAAAAIBAJ%26amp%3Bsjid%3DN8cMAAAAIBAJ%26amp%3Bpg%3D4445%2C1834410%26amp%3Bdq%3Dmickey%2Bcochrane%26amp%3Bhl%3Den ] A left-handed batter, Cochrane ran well enough that manager Connie Mack would occasionally insert him into the leadoff spot in the batting order. Most frequently, Cochrane would bat third, but wherever he hit, his primary job was to get on base so that hard-hitting Al Simmons and Jimmie Foxx could drive him in. In May, he tied a major league record by hitting three home run s in a game. [ Connie Mack Is Well Satisfied With Payouts The Miami News 3 5 June 1925 30 March 2011 http://news.google.com/newspapers?id%3D_EguAAAAIBAJ%26amp%3Bsjid%3DKdcFAAAAIBAJ%26amp%3Bpg%3D5860%2C5244789%26amp%3Bdq%3Dmickey%2Bcochrane%26amp%3Bhl%3Den ] He ended his rookie season with a.331 batting average and a.397 on base percentage, helping the Athletics to a second place finish. By the start of the 1926 season, Cochrane was already considered the best catcher in the major leagues. [ &apos;We&apos;re In&apos; Scribe Hears The &apos;Kid&apos; Say United Press International Palm Beach Daily News 19 March 1926 30 March 2011 http://news.google.com/newspapers?id%3Dtn8hAAAAIBAJ%26amp%3Bsjid%3DZ4sFAAAAIBAJ%26amp%3Bdq%3Dmickey%20cochrane%26amp%3Bpg%3D1437%2C1377292 ] Cochrane won the [ 1928 ] Most Valuable Player Award mostly due to his leadership and defensive skills, when he led the American League in putout s and hit for a.293 batting average along with 10 home run s and 58 runs batted in. [ http://www.baseball-reference.com/awards/awards_1928.shtml#ALmvp 1928 American League Most Valuable Player Award voting results Baseball-reference.com 2010-11-23 ] Cochrane was a catalyst in the pennant -winning years of 1929, 1930 and 1931 when he hit.331,.357 and.349 respectively. He played in three World Series with the Athletics, and was sometimes blamed for the loss of the 1931 World Series, when the St. Louis Cardinals led by Pepper Martin, stole eight bases in the series, although, in his book, The Life of a Baseball Hall of Fame Catcher, author Charlie Bevis cites the Philadelphia pitching staff&apos;s carelessness at holding baserunners close to their bases as a contributing factor. [ http://books.google.com/books?id%3DTt1ePW6HopEC%26amp%3Blpg%3DPP1%26amp%3Bdq%3Dmickey%20cochrane%26amp%3Bpg%3DPA84#v%3Donepage%26amp%3Bq%26amp%3Bf%3Dfalse The Life of a Baseball Hall of Fame Catcher Bevis, Charlie Books.Google.com 1998 30 March 2011 ] [ Dollars Rolling In For The Great &apos;Diz&apos; Associated Press Rochester Evening Journal 3 3 October 1934 5 April 2011 http://news.google.com/newspapers?id%3D5WRjAAAAIBAJ%26amp%3Bsjid%3DOUkNAAAAIBAJ%26amp%3Bdq%3Dpepper%20martin%26amp%3Bpg%3D3122%2C1301714 ] Regardless, the blame for the World Series loss would dog Cochrane for the rest of his life. &lt; ref name= &quot; The Life of a Baseball Hall of Fame Catcher &quot; / &lt; [ Detroit Tigers ] Mickey Cochrane in the cover of Time Magazine in 1935 In 1934, Connie Mack started to disassemble his dynasty for financial reasons and sold Cochrane to the Detroit Tigers, who made him player-manager. It was with the Tigers that Cochrane cemented his reputation as a team leader. His competitive nature drove the Tigers, who had been picked to finish in fourth or fifth place, to the [ 1934 ] American League championship, their first pennant in twenty-five years. [ http://www.baseball-reference.com/postseason/1934_WS.shtml 1934 World Series at Baseball Reference Baseball-reference.com 2010-11-23 ] [ http://books.google.com/books?id%3D5BRxw4nSqXUC%26amp%3Bpg%3DPA116%26amp%3Bdq%3Dmickey%2Bcochrane%26amp%3Bhl%3Den%26amp%3Bei%3Dqw3ES776EoO88gbXnKyrDw%26amp%3Bsa%3DX%26amp%3Boi%3Dbook_result%26amp%3Bct%3Dresult%26amp%3Bresnum%3D7%26amp%3Bved%3D0CEoQ6AEwBg#v%3Donepage%26amp%3Bq%3Dmickey%20cochrane%26amp%3Bf%3Dfalse &apos; &amp; #39;They Earned Their Stripes: The Detroit Tigers&apos; All-Time Team&apos; &amp; #39;, Detroit News, Sports Publishing LLC, 2001, ISBN 1-58261-365-6, ISBN 978-1-58261-365-9 Books.google.com 2010-11-23 ] Cochrane&apos;s leadership skills won him the 1934 Most Valuable Player Award, remarkable considering that Lou Gehrig won the Triple Crown and also finished with a much higher W.A.R. (10.7 versus 4.3). [ http://www.baseball-reference.com/awards/awards_1934.shtml#ALmvp 1934 American League Most Valuable Player Award voting results Baseball-reference.com 2010-11-23 ] He followed this by leading the Tigers to another American League pennant in [ 1935 ] and a victory over the Chicago Cubs in the 1935 World Series. [ http://www.baseball-reference.com/postseason/1935_WS.shtml 1935 World Series at Baseball Reference Baseball-reference.com 2010-11-23 ] Due in part to his high strung nature, he suffered a nervous breakdown during the 1936 season. Cochrane&apos;s playing career came to a sudden end on May 25, 1937 when he was hit in the head by a pitch by Yankees pitcher Bump Hadley. Hospitalized for seven days, the injury nearly killed him. His accident generated a call for batter helmets, but tradition won out. { &quot; Helmet for Baseball Batters is Urged as Safety Measure &quot; &apos;&apos;Popular Mechanics&apos;&apos;, July 1937 } Ordered by doctors not to play baseball again (he was just 34 years old), Cochrane returned to the dugout but had lost his competitive fire. He managed for the remainder of the 1937 season and was replaced midway through the 1938 season. Cochrane&apos;s all-time managerial record was 348-250, for a.582 winning percentage. [ http://www.baseball-reference.com/managers/cochrmi01.shtml Mickey Cochrane manager statistics at Baseball Reference Baseball-reference.com 2010-11-23 ] Despite his head injury, Cochrane served in the United States Navy during World War II, as did Bill Dickey of the Yankees, giving the Navy the two greatest catchers baseball had yet seen; with Yogi Berra also serving but not yet having reached the major leagues, there were actually three possible &quot; greatest catchers ever &quot; in the WWII-era Navy. Having been a heavy smoker, Cochrane died in 1962 in Lake Forest, Illinois of lymphatic cancer, at the age of 59. [ Career statistics and honors ] [ Detretck.PNG Mickey Cochrane Detroit Tigers 2000 ] Cochrane compiled a.320 batting average while hitting 119 home runs over a 13 year playing career. His.320 batting average was the highest career total for catchers until being surpassed by Joe Mauer in [ 2009 ]. [ http://members.tripod.com/bb_catchers/catchers/8coffavg.htm Career Batting Averages at The Encyclopedia of Baseball Catchers Members.tripod.com 2010-11-23 ] His.419 on-base percentage is among the best in baseball history, and is the highest all-time among catchers. [ http://members.tripod.com/bb_catchers/catchers/obp_car.htm On Base Percentages at The Encyclopedia of Baseball Catchers Members.tripod.com 2010-11-23 ] In [ 1932 ], Cochrane became the first catcher in Major League Baseball history to score 100 runs and have 100 RBI in the same season. [ http://books.google.com/books?id%3DySoDAAAAMBAJ%26amp%3Bpg%3DPA47%26amp%3Bdq%3D100%2Bruns%2B100%2Brbi%2Bby%2Bcatcher%2Bbaseball%2Bdigest%26amp%3Bhl%3Den%26amp%3Bei%3DXRvES7jzBoGB8gb2r8mhDw%26amp%3Bsa%3DX%26amp%3Boi%3Dbook_result%26amp%3Bct%3Dresult%26amp%3Bresnum%3D1%26amp%3Bved%3D0CDQQ6AEwAA#v%3Donepage%26amp%3Bq%26amp%3Bf%3Dfalse Baseball Digest, September 1995, Vol. 54, No. 9, ISSN 0005-609X Books.google.com 2010-11-23 ] He hit for the cycle twice in his career, on July 22, 1932 and on August 2, 1933. [ http://members.tripod.com/bb_catchers/catchers/cycle.htm Catchers Hitting for the Cycle at The Encyclopedia of Baseball Catchers Members.tripod.com 2010-11-23 ] [ http://www.thehitters.com/profile.asp?i%3D1118%26amp%3Be%3D1 Mickey Cochrane at www.thehitters.com Thehitters.com 2010-11-23 ] In his first 11 years, he never caught fewer than 110 games. Cochrane led American League catchers six times in putouts, and twice each in double play s, assists and fielding percentage. [ http://books.google.com/books?id%3DLSsDAAAAMBAJ%26amp%3Bpg%3DPA86%26amp%3Bdq%3Djuly%2B2001%2Bfielding%2Bleaders%2Bbaseball%2Bdigest%26amp%3Bcd%3D4#v%3Donepage%26amp%3Bq%3Djuly%202001%20fielding%20leaders%20baseball%20digest%26amp%3Bf%3Dfalse Baseball Digest, July 2001, P.86, Vol. 60, No. 7, ISSN 0005-609X Books.google.com 2010-11-23 ] In 1947, Cochrane became the second catcher enshrined into the Baseball Hall of Fame, after Roger Bresnahan. [ http://members.tripod.com/bb_catchers/catchers/cochrane.htm Mickey Cochrane at The Encyclopedia of Baseball Catchers Members.tripod.com 2010-11-23 ] With the Athletics having moved out of Philadelphia in 1954, and never retiring the uniform number 2 he wore with them, the Philadelphia Phillies honored Cochrane by electing him to the Philadelphia Baseball Wall of Fame at Veterans Stadium. [ http://mlb.mlb.com/phi/history/wall_of_fame.jsp Philadelphia Baseball Wall of Fame at mlb.com Mlb.mlb.com 2010-11-23 ] The Athletics&apos; plaques from that display have been moved to the Philadelphia Athletics Museum in Hatboro, Pennsylvania. The Tigers honored him by renaming National Avenue, behind the third-base stands at Tiger Stadium, Cochrane Avenue, but have never retired the uniform number 3 he wore with them. In 1999, he ranked number 65 on The Sporting News list of the 100 Greatest Baseball Players, and was a nominee for the Major League Baseball All-Century Team.
