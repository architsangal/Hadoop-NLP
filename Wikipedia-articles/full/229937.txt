The former Hoechst AG and today&apos;s &apos;&apos;Industriepark Höchst&apos;&apos; Hoechst AG ( [ ˈhøːçst ] ) was a German chemicals then life-sciences company that became Aventis Deutschland after its merger with France&apos;s Rhône-Poulenc S.A. in 1999. With the new company&apos;s 2004 merger with Sanofi-Synthélabo, it became a subsidiary of the resulting Sanofi-Aventis pharmaceuticals group. [ History ] Building and operating licence for the chemical plant Meister, Lucius &amp; Co. by the administration of the Duchy of Nassau in 1862 The company was founded in 1863 as &quot; Teerfarbenfabrik Meister, Lucius &amp; Co. &quot; in Höchst, near Frankfurt and changed its name some years later to &quot; Teerfarbenfabrik Meister Lucius &amp; Brüning &quot;. In 1880 it became a stock company &quot; Farbwerke vorm. Meister Lucius &amp; Brüning AG &quot;. For the international market the name was simplified to &quot; Farbwerke Hoechst AG &quot;. Until 1925 the Hoechst AG was independent. In 1916, the Hoechst AG was one of the co-founders of IG Farben, a pressure group of Germany&apos;s chemicals industry to gain industrial power during and after World War I. In 1925, IG Farben turned from a pressure group into the well-known conglomerate. World War II - Various Hoechst facilities were bombed during the Oil Campaign of World War II 1951 - Hoechst AG was re-founded on December 7 in Frankfurt when IG Farben was split into its founder companies. The original capitalization of the company was 100,000 Deutsche Mark. By 1953 Hoechst had acquired parts of Knapsack-Griesheim, Kalle AG, Behring Werke, Wacker Chemie and Ruhr Chemie, among others.
