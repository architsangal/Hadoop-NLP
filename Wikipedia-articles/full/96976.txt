Mineral County is the second least populous of the 64 counties of the State of Colorado of the United States. The county was named for the many valuable minerals found in the mountains and streams of the area. The county population was 712 at 2010 census. [ United States Census Bureau 2010 Census Data http://2010.census.gov/2010census/data/ United States Census Bureau 11 February 2012 ] The county seat and the only municipality in the county is the Town of Creede.
