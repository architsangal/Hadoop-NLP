[ Frederick County Maryland Frederick County md seal.png 225 1748 Frederick 667.34 662.88 4.46 0.67% 2010 233385 135.9 FrederickCountyMD.gov ] Frederick County is a county located in the western part of the U.S. state of Maryland, bordering the southern border of Pennsylvania and the northeastern border of Virginia. As of the 2010 Census, the population was 233,385. [ http://quickfacts.census.gov/qfd/states/24/24021.html Frederick County QuickFacts U.S. Census Bureau 2010 2011-08-01 ] It is a part of the Washington-Baltimore Metropolitan Area, and is often recognized as part of Western Maryland. The county is home to Catoctin Mountain Park (encompassing the presidential retreat Camp David ) and to the U.S. Army&apos;s Fort Detrick. The county seat is Frederick, which was home to several celebrated historical figures like Francis Scott Key, Thomas Johnson (governor), Roger B. Taney, and Barbara Fritchie. The county (and the county seat) may have been named for Frederick Calvert, 6th Baron Baltimore. [ http://www.msa.md.gov/msa/mdmanual/36loc/fr/html/fr.html Frederick County, Maryland — Government Maryland State Archives March 5, 2008 2008-08-16 ] [ History ] [ April 2010 ] Frederick County was created in 1748 from parts of Prince George&apos;s County and Baltimore County. In 1776, Frederick County was divided into three parts. The westernmost portion became Washington County, named after George Washington, the southern most portion became Montgomery County, named after another Revolutionary War general, Richard Montgomery. The northern portion remained Frederick County. In 1837 a part of Frederick County was combined with a part of Baltimore County to form Carroll County which is east of current day Frederick County. The county has a number of properties on the National Register of Historic Places. [ 2008a ] [ Law, government, and politics ] Frederick County is governed by county commissioner s, the traditional form of county government in the state of Maryland. Frederick County is one of the most historically Republican counties in Maryland. It has not voted for a Democrat for president since 1964 when it voted for Lyndon B. Johnson. In 2004 George W. Bush defeated John Kerry 59-39%. { 2004 election results } Democrats came closer in 2008, when John McCain defeated Barack Obama by a mere 1,157 votes (49.62-48.58). { 2008 election results } Nevertheless, Republicans in Frederick rebounded to more historical levels in the 2010 Maryland Gubernatorial &amp; Senatorial Elections, giving the Republican Ehrlich/Kane ticket 55% to Democrat O&apos;Malley/Brown&apos;s 45. Frederick voters also supported Republican Senate challenger Eric Wargotz over incumbent Democratic Senator Barbara A Milkulski by a margin of 51-46, even as Mikulski was winning statewide by a landslide 61-37. Frederick County&apos;s fire and rescue service is handled by a combination career and volunteer service delivery system. Frederick County employs over 350 firefighters. Volunteers of the 26 volunteer fire and rescue corporations number approximately 800 active operational members. Emergency Medical Services, including Advanced Life Support is integrated with the fire service. Career and volunteers alike work side-by-side to provide fire and emergency medical services. Frederick County has a Maryland State Police Medevac located at the Frederick Municipal Airport and is designated &quot; Trooper 3 &quot;. Trooper 3 handles calls all throughout the state, but provides immediate assistance to local police, fire and rescue services. The official language of Frederick County is English. [ County Makes English Official Language Pete McCarthy February 22, 2012 Frederick News-Post http://www.fredericknewspost.com/sections/news/display.htm?StoryID%3D132165 ] [ Geography ] A farm in Frederick County, Maryland Frederick County straddles the boundary between the Piedmont Plateau Region and the Appalachian Mountains. The county&apos;s two prominent ridges, Catoctin Mountain and South Mountain, form an extension of the Blue Ridge. The Middletown Valley lies between them. [ Economy ] The median income for a household in Frederick county is $60,276, and the median income for a family was $67,879. Males had a median income of $42,378 versus $30,564 for females. The per capita income for the county was $25,404. About 2.90% of families and 4.50% of the population were below the poverty line, including 4.90% of those under age 18 and 6.00% of those age 65 or over. Fort Detrick is the largest employer in Frederick County.
