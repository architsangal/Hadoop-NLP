[ July 2009 ] [ 1968 2 18 ] { Roseville, } { California, United States } { Actress, singer, dancer } { 1977–present } { Valery Lameignère (1999–2002) } { Panio Gianopoulos (2007–present; 3 children) } Molly Kathleen Ringwald (born February 18, 1968) is an American actress, singer and dancer. Having appeared in the John Hughes films Sixteen Candles (1984), The Breakfast Club (1985), and Pretty in Pink (1986), Ringwald has been frequently named the greatest teen star of all time. [ Early life ] Molly Kathleen Ringwald was born in Roseville, California, just outside of Sacramento, the daughter of Adele Edith (née Frembd), a housewife and chef, and Robert Scott &quot; Bob &quot; Ringwald, a blind jazz pianist. { Molly Ringwald Biography (1968–). Filmreference.com. Retrieved on 2011-05-29. } { Ancestry of Molly Ringwald (1968–). Freepages.genealogy.rootsweb.com. Retrieved on 2011-05-29. } Ringwald has two siblings, Elizabeth and Kelly. She started her acting career at age five, starring in a stage production of Alice in Wonderland as the Dormouse. The next year, she recorded &quot; I Wanna Be Loved by You, &quot; a music album of Dixieland jazz with her father and his group, the Fulton Street Jazz Band. { Sacramento&apos;s Fulton Street Jazz Band&apos;s Recordings. Fultonstreetjazz.com. Retrieved on 2011-05-29. } [ Acting career ] In 1978, at the age of 10, she was chosen to play Kate in the West Coast production of Annie, performing in Los Angeles. { Karlyn, Kathleen Rowe, &quot; &apos;Too Close for Comfort&apos;: American Beauty and the Incest Motif &quot;, Cinema Journal, 44, Number 1, Fall 2004, pp. 69–93. University of Texas Press. } In 1979, Ringwald appeared in the TV series Diff&apos;rent Strokes and was selected to become a cast member of the spin-off Facts of Life. She played &quot; Molly Parker, &quot; a perky, fun-loving student at Eastland Girls School. Although she had essentially a supporting role, one entire episode, &quot; Molly&apos;s Holiday &quot; revolved around her character dealing with the effects of her parents&apos; divorce. In 1980, Ringwald performed as a lead vocalist on two Disney albums. On the patriotic album Yankee Doodle Mickey, Ringwald sang &quot; This Is My Country &quot;, &quot; The Star-Spangled Banner &quot; and &quot; God Bless America &quot;. She later performed one track on a Disney Christmas album. { Disney Family Christmas: Various Artists, Molly Ringwald, Larry Groce: Music. Amazon.com. Retrieved on 2011-05-29. } Turning toward motion pictures, she found her breakout role in Sixteen Candles (1984). Molly Ringwald was a member of the so-called Brat Pack of 1980s teen actors. [ Lurie Karen Brat Pack St. James Encyclopedia of Popular Culture http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_g1epc/is_bio/ai_2419200128 Gale Group ] Among Ringwald&apos;s films are The Breakfast Club, Pretty in Pink, Sixteen Candles, The Pick-up Artist and Fresh Horses. In the early 1990s, Ringwald reportedly turned down the female lead roles in Pretty Woman and Ghost. [ Monica Corcoran http://www.latimes.com/features/lifestyle/la-ig-molly29-2008jun29%2C0%2C7763315.story Molly Ringwald: Pretty in Pucci Los Angeles Times 2009-03-06 29 June 2008 ] In 1994, she starred in the TV adaptation of Stephen King &apos;s The Stand. In 1996, she played a leading role in the film Malicious as Melissa Nelson, a disturbed woman who has an affair with a college star baseball player. 1996 saw her return to television, starring on the ABC sitcom Townies. She also made one appearance a blind woman on the critically acclaimed cable series Remember WENN. She starred with Lara Flynn Boyle and Teri Hatcher in the 1998 made for television film Since You&apos;ve Been Gone. In 2000, she appeared in an episode of Showtime&apos;s The Outer Limits. In 2000, Ringwald appeared in the ensemble restaurant-themed film, In the Weeds; in 2001, she had a cameo in Not Another Teen Movie. In late 2004, she starred in the play Modern Orthodox on Broadway, opposite Jason Biggs and Craig Bierko. [ http://www.haaretz.com/culture/arts-leisure/a-comic-jewish-duel-1.143554 A comic Jewish duel Saul Austerlitz December 13, 2004 Haaretz May 24, 2011 ] In 2006, she starred in the television film The Wives He Forgot. Ringwald has appeared in Cabaret, tick, tick... BOOM!, [ Tick, Tick...Boom (2001) http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0%2C%2C253731%2C00.html Melissa Rose Bernardo November 2, 2001 Entertainment Weekly 2010-11-27 ] and Enchanted April on Broadway, and in the fall and winter of 2006, she starred as Charity Hope Valentine in the national tour of the Broadway revival of the musical Sweet Charity. { AP. &quot; Molly Ringwald to take &apos;Sweet Charity&apos; on the road this fall &quot;, } { USA Today, February 27, 2006. } Ringwald is currently starring in the ABC Family network&apos;s series The Secret Life of the American Teenager, which debuted on July 1, 2008, { &quot; Molly Ringwald&apos;s Not A Teenager Anymore! &quot;, TV Guide, July 1, 2008. } playing the title teenager&apos;s mother. [ Personal life ] Ringwald was married to Valery Lameignère, a French writer, in Bordeaux, France, on July 28, 1999; they divorced in 2002. [ http://nymag.com/nymetro/arts/theater/10522/ Don’t You Forget About Me Michael Agger May 21, 2005 nymag.com May 24, 2011 ] She married Panio Gianopoulos, a Greek-American writer and book editor, in 2007. They have a daughter, Mathilda Ereni (born October 22, 2003), and twins, Adele Georgiana and Roman Stylianos (born July 10, 2009). Her pregnancy was written into the storyline of The Secret Life of the American Teenager. { Molly Ringwald Expecting Twins People, January 23, 2009 } In April 2010, Ringwald published her first book, Getting the Pretty Back with It Books ( HarperCollins Publishers ).
