[ February ] [ Lexington CV-2 ] [ Saratoga CV-3 ] February 6 – The Washington Naval Conference concludes in Washington, D.C., with an agreement on the Five-Power Treaty (often referred to as the Washington Naval Treaty ), which places limits on naval forces, including aircraft carrier s. It limits the aircraft carrier forces of the United Kingdom and United States to 135,000 displacement ton s each, of Japan to 81,000 tons, and of France and Italy to 60,000 tons each. No aircraft carrier may mount guns of greater than 8  inches (203  mm) in bore or exceed 27,000 displacement tons except for the planned United States Navy carriers and, which the treaty permits to displace 33,000 tons each. Potter, E. B., Sea Power: A Naval History, Second Edition, Annapolis, Maryland: Naval Institute Press, 1981, ISBN 0-87021-607-4, p. 233. The treaty will remain in force until December 31, 1936.
