[ History ] Finneytown is named for Ebenezer Ward Finney, whose burial site is located just south of the current township. { WMWKQ Ebenezer Ward Finney, waymarking.com. } The land was originally purchased from John Cleves Symmes by Rev. Finneytown is the home of the annual Panegyri Greek Festival, the largest annual ethnic food celebration in the Cincinnati area. The Holy Trinity-Saint Nicholas Greek Orthodox Church started the yearly festival in 1975. It has included authentic Greek food, music, dancing, and an art show. The festival has been held during June in recent years. [ Cultural influences ] Pringles potato crisps were named after Pringle Drive, a street in Finneytown. BF Pringles, PG.com, 2012.
