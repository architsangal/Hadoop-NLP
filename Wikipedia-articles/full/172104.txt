[ yes 1941 9 24 ] { New York City, } { New York, U.S. } [ yes 1998 4 17 1941 9 24 ] { Tucson, } { Arizona, U.S. } { Activist, photographer, singer } { * } { Vocals * } { keyboard * } { accordion * } { piano * } { organ * } { harpsichord * } { mellotron } { * } { Wings * } { Denny Laine * } { Paul McCartney * } { Suzy and the Red Stripes } { 1971–1998 } Linda Louise McCartney, Lady McCartney (née Eastman; formerly See; September 24, 1941 – April 17, 1998) was an American photographer, musician and animal rights activist. Her father and mother were Lee Eastman and Louise Sara Lindner Eastman. In 1969 she married Beatle Paul McCartney and later joined McCartney&apos;s band Wings. In the same year McCartney adopted her daughter, Heather Louise, from her first marriage to John See. The McCartneys had three children together: Mary Anna, Stella Nina, and James Louis. She became Lady McCartney when her husband was knighted in 1997. She wrote several vegetarian cookbooks, became a business entrepreneur (starting the Linda McCartney Foods company) and was a professional photographer, publishing Linda McCartney&apos;s Sixties: Portrait of an Era. McCartney was diagnosed with breast cancer in 1995, and died at the age of 56 on 17 April 1998 in Tucson, Arizona, where the McCartneys had a ranch. [ Early years ] Linda Louise McCartney was born Linda Louise Eastman, the second-eldest of four children, to Jewish-American parents in New York City. She had one older brother, John (10 July 1939), and two younger sisters, Laura (born 1947) and Louise Jr. (born 1950). She grew up in the wealthy Scarsdale area of Westchester County, New York and graduated from Scarsdale High School in 1959. [ http://www.sfmuseum.org/hist6/lindabio.html Linda McCartney The Virtual Museum of San Francisco 9 June 2011 ] Her father was the son of Jewish-Russian immigrants. He changed his name from Leopold Vail Epstein to Lee Eastman, and was not related to George Eastman of Eastman Kodak fame. [ http://geocities.com/helenwheels_99/pup.html When I Was A Pup 9 June 2011 http://web.archive.org/web/20091022062105/http%3A//geocities.com/helenwheels_99/pup.html 2009-10-22 ] He was songwriter Jack Lawrence&apos;s attorney, and at his request in 1942, Lawrence, while in the army, wrote a song called &quot; Linda &quot; in honor of the one-year-old. It was published in 1946, and recorded by Buddy Clark in 1947. John Eastman later became Paul McCartney&apos;s lawyer and manager, taking over from his father, Lee Eastman. [ http://www.sundaymail.co.uk/news/editors-choice/2008/11/02/the-beatles-in-scotland-paul-mccartney-s-story-78057-20862290/ The Beatles in Scotland: Paul McCartney&apos;s story Sunday Mail 2 November 2008 9 June 2011 ] Her mother, Louise Sara Lindner Eastman—the daughter of Max J. Lindner, founder of the Lindner Company clothing store in Cleveland, Ohio —died in the crash of American Airlines Flight 1 in Queens, New York, in 1962. [ http://aviation-safety.net/database/record.php?id%3D19620301-0 Accident description Aviation Safety Network 1996 9 June 2011 ] [ Nigel Slater http://shopping.guardian.co.uk/food/story/0%2C%2C2068978%2C00.html When the McCartneys came for lunch The Guardian 29 April 2007 9 June 2011 London ] McCartney later said that because of her mother&apos;s death, she hated travelling by air. McCartney studied for a Fine Art major at the University of Arizona. Her first marriage was to Joseph Melvin See Jr., whom she met at college. They married on June 18, 1962, and their daughter Heather Louise was born on December 31, 1962. They were divorce d in June 1965. McCartney later commented that See was a &quot; nice man, a geologist, an Ernest Hemingway type &quot;. He committed suicide with a self-inflicted gunshot wound on March 19, 2000, at his home in Tucson. [ Jill Smolowe http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0%2C%2C20130865%2C00.html Starting Over People 3 March 2000 9 June 2011 ] [ Photography ] McCartney started work as a receptionist for the Town &amp; Country magazine, and was the only unofficial photographer on board the SS Sea Panther yacht on the Hudson River who was allowed to take photographs of The Rolling Stones during a record promotion party. [ Danny Fields http://www.wingspan.ru/bookseng/linda/01.html Linda McCartney &quot; The Biography &quot; Chapter 1 Wingspan Russia 9 June 2011 ] Although she had previously only studied the photography of horses in Arizona at an arts centre with a teacher, Hazel Archer, she was later asked to be the house photographer at the Fillmore East concert hall, and supposedly became a popular groupie. [ Danny Fields http://www.wingspan.ru/bookseng/linda/02.html Linda McCartney &quot; The Biography &quot; Chapter 2 Wingspan Russia 9 June 2011 ] She photographed artists such as Aretha Franklin, Grace Slick, Jimi Hendrix, Bob Dylan, Janis Joplin, Eric Clapton, Simon &amp; Garfunkel, The Who, The Doors, The Animals, John Lennon, and Neil Young. She photographed Young in 1967—the picture was used for the front cover of Sugar Mountain: Live at Canterbury House 1968, in 2008. She photographed Clapton for Rolling Stone magazine, becoming the first woman to have a photo featured on the front cover (11 May 1968). She and McCartney also appeared on the cover of Rolling Stone on January 31, 1974, making her the only person both to have taken a photo, and to have been photographed, for the front cover of the magazine. Her photographs were later exhibited in more than 50 galleries internationally, as well as at the Victoria and Albert Museum in London. A collection of photographs from that time, Linda McCartney&apos;s Sixties: Portrait of an Era, was published in 1993. [ McCartney, Linda Linda McCartney&apos;s sixties: portrait of an era Bulfinch Press Book Boston 1992 0-8212-1959-6 ] She also took the photograph for the cover of McCartney and Michael Jackson &apos;s single, &quot; The Girl Is Mine &quot;. [ Raymond Newman http://www.revolverbook.co.uk/beatleslondon.html The Beatles&apos; London, 1965-66 Abracadabra 9 June 2011 ] She was in the UK on an assignment to take photographs of &quot; Swinging Sixties &quot; musicians in London. The two later went to the Speakeasy Club on Margaret Street to see Procol Harum. Pepper&apos;s Lonely Hearts Club Band at Brian Epstein &apos;s house in Belgravia. When her assignment was completed she flew back to New York City. [ Miles 1997 117 ] In May 1968, they met again in New York, as John Lennon and McCartney were there to announce the formation of Apple Corps. [ Spitz 2005 761 ] In September of the same year, he phoned her and asked her to fly over to London. They were married six months later in a small civil ceremony (when she was four months pregnant with their daughter Mary ) at Marylebone Registry Office on 12 March 1969. She became Lady McCartney when her husband was knighted in 1997. Her brother, entertainment lawyer John Eastman, has represented McCartney since the break-up of The Beatles. [ Music ] [ Wings (band) ] After the breakup of The Beatles in 1970, her husband taught her to play keyboards and recorded an album with her, Ram, as a duo. Afterwards, he included her in the lineup for his subsequent group, Wings. [ Ray Bonici http://beatles.ncf.ca/mpl.html Paul McCartney Wings it alone Music Express &quot; (Canada) issue #56 (GG70470) 1982 9 June 2011 ] The group garnered several Grammy Awards, becoming one of the most successful bands of the 1970s, but had to endure jibes regarding Linda McCartney&apos;s singing. [ Germaine Greer http://www.independent.co.uk/opinion/commentators/germaine-greer-pop-bitch-479025.html Germaine Greer: Pop bitch The Independent 21 May 2006 9 June 2011 London ] She later admitted that the early accusations about her singing out of tune in the early days with Wings were true. In 1977, a reggae-inspired single entitled &quot; Seaside Woman &quot; was released by an obscure band called Suzy and the Red Stripes, on Epic Records in the United States. In reality, Suzy and The Red Stripes were Wings, with Linda McCartney (who also wrote the song) on lead vocals. [ Graham Calkin http://www.jpgr.co.uk/col_ams7461.html Seaside Woman b/w B-Side To Seaside Graham Calkin&apos;s Beatles Pages 9 June 2011 ] The song was recorded by Wings in 1972, in response to a lawsuit by Northern Songs and Maclen Music alleging McCartney violated an exclusive rights agreement by collaborating on the song &quot; Another Day &quot;, which had the effect of transferring a 50% share of the publishing royalties to his own McCartney Music company. [ McCartney and Wife Sued on &apos;Another Day&apos; Recording New York Times July 23, 1971: 15 http://query.nytimes.com/mem/archive/pdf?res%3DFA0915FE355B1A7493C1AB178CD85F458785F9 ] The lawsuit, which alleged that Linda McCartney&apos;s co-writing credits were inauthentic and that she was not a real songwriter, was &quot; amicably settled, &quot; according to an ATV spokesman, in June 1972. { Brian Southall and Rupert Perry, Northen Songs: The True Story of The Beatles&apos; Song Publishing Empire (2007). } The McCartneys shared an Oscar nomination for the co-composition of the song, &quot; Live and Let Die &quot;. Her album Wide Prairie, which included &quot; Seaside Woman, &quot; was released posthumously in 1998. [ http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/163837.stm Linda&apos;s lone effort to be released BBC 3 September 1998 9 June 2011 ] McCartney worked with the help of The Beatles&apos; engineer, Geoff Emerick, to finish the album. Along with eight other British composers, he contributed to the choral album A Garland for Linda, and dedicated his classical album, Ecce Cor Meum, to his late wife. [ http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/345875.stm A Garland for Linda BBC 17 May 1999 9 June 2011 ] In January 1999, &quot; The Light Comes From Within &quot; single from the Wide Prairie album was banned by TV and radio stations in the UK. She explained her change to vegetarianism by saying that she did not &quot; eat anything with a face &quot;, and if &quot; slaughterhouse s had glass walls the whole world would be vegetarian &quot;. [ http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk/80494.stm Obituary: Linda McCartney BBC 19 April 1998 9 June 2011 ] [ Linda McCartney http://www.brainyquote.com/quotes/authors/l/linda_mccartney.html Linda McCartney Quotes Brainy Media 9 June 2011 ] The McCartneys became outspoken vegetarians and animal rights activist s. In 1991, she introduced a line of frozen vegetarian meals under the Linda McCartney Foods name, which made her wealthy independently of her husband. The H. J. Heinz Company acquired the company in March 2000, and the Hain Celestial Group bought it in 2007. As a strong advocate for animal rights, Linda lent her support to many organizations like People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals (PETA) as well as The Council for the Protection of Rural England, and Friends of the Earth. She was also a patron of the League Against Cruel Sports. Before her death, she narrated a TV advertisement for PETA, in which she said: &quot; Have you ever seen a fish gasping for breath when you take it out of the water? They’re saying, ‘Thanks a lot for killing me. It feels great, you know.’ No! It hurts! &quot; [ http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid%3D1955%26amp%3Bdat%3D19990909%26amp%3Bid%3DUSwiAAAAIBAJ%26amp%3Bsjid%3DWqYFAAAAIBAJ%26amp%3Bpg%3D1193%2C5116144 Activists Target Fish Menus Reading Eagle September 9, 1999 9 April 2012 ] After her death, PETA created the Linda McCartney Memorial Award. [ http://www.peta2.com/outthere/o-morrissey_accept.asp News PETA 9 April 2012 ] In 1984, she was arrested in Barbados for possession of marijuana; her husband had been arrested in Los Angeles for possession of marijuana in 1975. She later commented that hard drugs were disgusting, but marijuana &quot; is pretty lightweight &quot;.
