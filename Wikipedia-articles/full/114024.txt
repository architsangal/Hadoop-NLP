[ Demographics ] [ right Rock Rapids Population History 1394 1766 2005 2172 2221 2556 2640 2780 2632 2693 2601 2573 2549 Iowa Data Center ] [ 2010 census ] As of the census [ http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Census_Bureau United States Census Bureau ] { 2012-5-11 } of 2010, there were 2,549 people, 1,083 households, and 689 families residing in the town. The population density was 644.2 people per square mile (248.7/km²). There were 1,207 housing units at an average density of 305 per square mile (117.8/km²). The racial makeup of the town was 98.2% White, 0.2% African American, 0.1% Native American, 0.1% Asian, 0.8% from other races, and 0.6% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 1.8% of the population. There were 1,083 households out of which 27.8% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 53.2% were married couples living together, 8.0% had a female householder with no husband present, 2.4% had a male householder with no wife present, and 36.4% were non-families. 33.7% of all households were made up of individuals and 18.4% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.28 and the average family size was 2.92. In the city the population was spread out with 24.4% under the age of 18, 6.2% from 18 to 24, 23% from 25 to 44, 24.6% from 45 to 64, and 21.9% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 42.1 years. The gender makeup of the city was 47.1% male and 52.9% female. The median income for a household in the city was $35,135, and the median income for a family was $47,688. Males had a median income of $30,691 versus $19,425 for females. The per capita income for the city was $18,035. About 3.4% of families and 7.5% of the population were below the poverty line, including 7.0% of those under age 18 and 12.5% of those age 65 or over. [ Parks and recreation ] One of the highlights of Rock Rapids is Island Park, so named because part of the park is completely surrounded by the Rock River. Island Park features a Rock Island railroad trestle that has been resurfaced as part of a new recreation trail and a museum in the nearby retired depot. There are two small dams in the park, and one can drive across the river via a small bridge. [ Notable people ] Leave It to Beaver star Jerry Mathers resided in Rock Rapids as a youngster prior to achieving fame as a child actor Bruce B. Brugmann, editor and publisher of the San Francisco Bay Guardian (a progressive newspaper), grew up in Rock Rapids and graduated from Central Lyon High School.
