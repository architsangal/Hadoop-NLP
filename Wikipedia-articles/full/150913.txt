[ 3 ] { 238 } { 781 } { inline,title } { region:US_type:city } { 41 } { 9 } { 33 } { N } { 83 } { 24 } { 55 } { W } { ZIP code } { 44830 } { 419 } { FIPS code } { 39-28014 } [ 2 ] { GNIS feature ID } { 1064668 } [ 3 ] { http://www.ci.fostoria.oh.us/ } Fostoria is a city located at the convergence of Hancock, Seneca, and Wood counties in the northwestern part of the U.S. state of Ohio. It is approximately [ 40 mi ] south of Toledo and [ 90 mi ] north of Columbus. The population was 13,441 at the 2000 census. [ Name and general vbackground ] The city is named for Charles W. Foster and not, as many believe, { February 2012 } his son Charles Foster, the 35th governor of Ohio and U.S. Secretary of the Treasury under Benjamin Harrison. Fostoria is known for its glass heritage and railroad ing : two CSX main lines and one NS main line intersect in downtown Fostoria, an area called the &quot; Iron Triangle &quot; by railfans. Originally there were 5 railroads that serviced the city. They were meant to service glass companies that were located in the city. The city of Fostoria is raising funds to build a railroad park inside the &quot; Iron Triangle &quot; for railroading fans to view trains. [ History ] Fostoria was established in 1854. { Columbia-Lippincott Gazeteer, p. 633 } [ Geography ] Fostoria is located at [ 41 9 33 N 83 24 55 W type:city ] (41.159243, -83.415239). [ 1 ] According to the United States Census Bureau, the city has a total area of 7.5  square miles (19.4  km²), of which, 7.3  square miles (18.8  km²) of it is land and 0.2  square miles (0.6  km²) of it (2.94%) is water. Fostoria was home to a Chrysler plant which closed in the 1970s. [ Demographics ] [ 1027 1733 3569 7070 7730 9597 9987 12790 13453 14351 15732 16037 15743 14983 13931 13441 ] As of the 2010 census Fostoria had a population of 13,441. The ethnic and racial composition of the population was 78.3% non-Hispanic white, 5.9% non-Hispanic black, 0.1% Native American, 0.4% Asian, 0.3% non-Hispanic from some other race, 4.7% from two or more races and 11.5% Hispanic or Latino of any race. { 2010 general profile of population and housing chatracteristics for Fostoria from the US Census } As of the census [ 2 ] of 2000, there were 13,931 people, 5,565 households, and 3,628 families residing in the city. The population density was 1,917.6 people per square mile (740.9/km²). There were 6,024 housing units at an average density of 829.2 per square mile (320.4/km²). The racial makeup of the city was 87.32% White, 5.75% African American, 0.19% Native American, 0.48% Asian, 3.60% from other races, and 2.66% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 7.92% of the population. There were 5,565 households out of which 32.0% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 44.9% were married couples living together, 15.1% had a female householder with no husband present, and 34.8% were non-families. 29.6% of all households were made up of individuals and 12.7% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.46 and the average family size was 3.03. In the city the population was spread out with 27.0% under the age of 18, 8.8% from 18 to 24, 27.7% from 25 to 44, 20.8% from 45 to 64, and 15.6% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 36 years. For every 100 females there were 90.3 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 87.0 males. The median income for a household in the city was $31,166, and the median income for a family was $38,427. Males had a median income of $31,476 versus $22,016 for females. The per capita income for the city was $15,568. About 9.0% of families and 11.2% of the population were below the poverty line, including 14.1% of those under age 18 and 7.0% of those age 65 or over.
