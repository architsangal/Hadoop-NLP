[ Greene County Mississippi Map of Mississippi highlighting Greene County.png 150 1811 Leakesville Leakesville Town 718.69 712.82 5.87 0.82% 2010 14400 7 ] Greene County is a county located in the U.S. state of Mississippi. It was established in 1811 and is named for General Nathanael Greene. As of 2010, the population was 14,400. Its county seat is Leakesville [ 6 ]. [ Geography ] According to the 2000 census, the county has a total area of [ 718.69 sqmi ], of which [ 712.82 sqmi ] (or 99.18%) is land and [ 5.87 sqmi ] (or 0.82%) is water. The population density was 19 people per square mile (7/km²). There were 4,947 housing units at an average density of 7 per square  mile (3/km²). The racial makeup of the county was 72.79% White, 26.18% Black or African American, 0.23% Native American, 0.07% Asian, 0.03% Pacific Islander, 0.31% from other races, and 0.38% from two or more races. 0.80% of the population were Hispanic or Latino of any race. The average household size was 2.67 and the average family size was 3.12. In the county the population was spread out with 24.10% under the age of 18, 13.10% from 18 to 24, 32.10% from 25 to 44, 20.60% from 45 to 64, and 10.10% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 32 years. For every 100 females there were 130.00 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 141.00 males. About 16.50% of families and 19.60% of the population were below the poverty line, including 25.30% of those under age 18 and 21.10% of those age 65 or over.
