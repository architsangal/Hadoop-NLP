They secrete mucus to lubricate the vagina and are homologous to bulbourethral gland s in males. However, while Bartholin&apos;s glands are located in the superficial perineal pouch in females, bulbourethral glands are located in the deep perineal pouch in males. [ Function ] They secrete mucus to provide vaginal lubrication. [ http://health.discovery.com/centers/sex/sexpedia/bartholins.html Bartholin&apos;s Gland Discovery Health ] The minute droplets of fluid were once believed to be important for lubricating the vagina, but research from Masters and Johnson demonstrated that vaginal lubrication comes from deeper within the vagina. The fluid may slightly moisten the labial opening of the vagina, serving to make contact with this sensitive area more comfortable for the woman. [ Pathology ] Although unusual, it is possible for the Bartholin&apos;s glands to become irritated or infected, resulting in pain. If the duct becomes obstructed, a Bartholin&apos;s cyst can develop, and a Bartholin&apos;s cyst in turn can become infected and form an abscess. Adenocarcinoma of the gland is rare, but benign tumors and hyperplasia are even more rare.
