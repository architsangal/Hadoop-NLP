[ Eevee 150px National Pokédex Ditto - Eevee (#131)- Vaporeon &apos;&apos;Pokémon&apos;&apos; series &apos;&apos;Pokémon Red&apos;&apos; and &apos;&apos;Blue&apos;&apos; Ken Sugimori Mika Kanai (Gary&apos;s) Yumi Tōma (Sakura&apos;s) Kayzie Rogers (May&apos;s) Mika Kanai (Shigeru&apos;s) Yumi Tōma (Sakura&apos;s) Megumi Hayashibara (Haruka&apos;s) ] [ Eevee イーブイ Ībui Eievui ] is a Pokémon species in Nintendo and Game Freak &apos;s Pokémon franchise. Created by Ken Sugimori, Eevee first appeared in the video games &apos;&apos;Pokémon Red&apos;&apos; and &apos;&apos;Blue&apos;&apos; and subsequent sequels. They have later appeared in various merchandise, spinoff titles and animated and printed adaptations of the franchise. Known as the Evolution Pokémon, { The Official Pokemon Handbook, Maria S. Barbo, Scholastic } Eevee is a fennec -like Pokémon with an unstable genetic code, which allows it to evolve into seven different Pokémon depending on the situation. Three of these evolutions are the result of Eevee being exposed to either a Fire Stone, Thunder stone or Water Stone. Two more of the evolutions involve maximum affection with the Trainer and accordance to the time of day. The final two evolutions are the result of training in specific areas. [ Conception and creation ] The design and art for Eevee and its evolutions were provided by Japanese video game designer and illustrator Ken Sugimori, [ http://www.computerandvideogames.com/article.php?id%3D91965 Game Freak on Pokémon! Stuart Bishop CVG 2003-05-30 2008-02-07 http://www.webcitation.org/5VSJaR6xT 2008-02-08 ] a friend of the Pokémon franchises&apos; creator, Satoshi Tajiri. In the original Japanese games, the Pokémon was known by its name Eievui a name which has similar prefixes to its current English name. However, before the English versions of the games were released, Eevee was originally going to be named Eon rather than Eevee. [ http://guides.ign.com/guides/16708/page_40.html IGN Guides: Pokemon Blue Version Gameboy IGN September 30, 1998 2-11-10 ] It was later renamed shortly before the English releases of &apos;&apos;Pokémon Red&apos;&apos; and &apos;&apos;Blue&apos;&apos;. [ Characteristics ] Eevee is a mammal ian creature with brown fur, a bushy tail that has a cream-colored tip, and a furry collar that is also cream-colored. Eevee has brown eyes, big ears, and pink paw pads. Eevee is said to have an irregularly shaped genetic structure, enabling it to evolve into multiple Pokémon. [ red Its genetic code is irregular. It may mutate if it is exposed to radiation from element stones. ] Eevee are quite rare, [ firered An extremely rare Pokémon that may evolve in a number of different ways depending on stimuli. ] but are able to live almost anywhere, as they may evolve to suit their surroundings. [ gold It has the ability to alter the composition of its body to suit its surrounding environment. ] While appearing very simple in appearance, Eevee is best known for its unstable genetic code which allows it to change and mutate into different forms (through evolution) depending on its environment. [ yellow Its genetic code is unstable, so it could evolve in a variety of ways. There are only a few alive. ] [ Gold It has the ability to alter the composition of its body to suit its surrounding environment. ] As a result of its evolution, Eevee would become better suited to living in its surrounding environment. [ Gold Its ability to evolve into many forms allows it to adapt smoothly and perfectly to any environment. ] All of Eevee&apos;s evolutions are of unique typing in comparison to each other. In &apos;&apos;Pokémon Red&apos;&apos; and &apos;&apos;Green&apos;&apos;, the player was able to evolve his or her Eevee into one its first three evolutions ( Vaporeon, Jolteon, or Flareon ) by the use of an elemental stone (though only one can be obtained at a time). Starting from &apos;&apos;Pokémon Gold&apos;&apos; and &apos;&apos;Silver&apos;&apos;, the player could further evolve his or her Eevee into either an Espeon or an Umbreon by making the Eevee to achieve maximum happiness (though the evolution depended on what time of day it was as Eevee evolved into Espeon at daytime while Eevee evolved into Umbreon at night). The final two evolutions of Eevee were obtainable upon the release of &apos;&apos;Pokémon Diamond&apos;&apos; and &apos;&apos;Pearl&apos;&apos;. Leafeon, Eevee&apos;s grass type evolution is obtainable upon leveling up near a moss rock, meanwhile Eevee&apos;s ice type evolution, Glaceon, is achieved by leveling up near an ice rock. About.com 15 October 2010 ] These rocks are found in the Sinnoh and Unova regions. [ Appearances ] [ In the video games ] In the Red, Blue, Yellow versions, the player receives one Eevee at the Pokémon Mansion in Celadon City, and they must trade to receive the Pokédex info on the other evolutions (Red, Blue, and Yellow only). In Pokémon Yellow, the player was to receive an Eevee from Professor Oak at the beginning of the game as the player&apos;s starter. However, the player&apos;s rival decides to take the Eevee before the player can obtain it. Due to this, the player is forced to choose the wild Pikachu that Professor Oak had caught earlier as a starter. The player&apos;s rival meanwhile evolves his Eevee into any of the three evolutions available, depending on the outcomes of the player&apos;s encounters with him in the early parts of the game. [ http://guidesarchive.ign.com/guides/12045/viridiancity.html Pokémon Yellow Strategy Guide - Viridian City and Route 22 IGN 12 June 2006 ] In Gold, Silver, and Crystal, after finding Bill in Ecruteak City, the player can go back to his house in Goldenrod City to receive an Eevee from him. In &apos;&apos;Pokémon Diamond&apos;&apos; and &apos;&apos;Pearl&apos;&apos; as well as Platinum, Eevee can be obtained by Bebe after getting the National Dex (In Platinum, Eevee can be obtained pre-National Dex). In &apos;&apos;Pokémon FireRed&apos;&apos; and &apos;&apos;LeafGreen&apos;&apos; as well as &apos;&apos;Pokémon HeartGold&apos;&apos; and &apos;&apos;SoulSilver&apos;&apos;, Eevee can be found in the same areas that it was obtained from the original games. In Pokémon Stadium 2, Eevee stars in its own minigame called &quot; Eager Eevee &quot;. Players have to run around in circles while Aipom raises and lowers a cover on berries. The object is to be among the first to grab some of the berries. [ http://ign64.ign.com/articles/089/089326p1.html IGN 2010-09-29 IGN Staff December 21, 2000 IGN: The Games of Pokemon GS: Part 2 ] In Pokémon XD, Eevee is featured as the main character&apos;s starter Pokémon. [ http://gc.kombo.com/article.php?artid%3D5882 Pokémon XD: Gale of Darkness Impressions Matthew Green gc.kombo.com October 9, 2005 10-15-10 ] The player also has the option of evolving their Eevee into one of its evolutions that appeared prior to Pokémon Diamond and Pearl. In &apos;&apos;Pokémon Mystery Dungeon: Blue Rescue Team&apos;&apos; and &apos;&apos;Red Rescue Team&apos;&apos;, Eevee is available as the main protagonist, in the naive nature if the player chooses female. Eevee reappears as a starter Pokémon in Pokémon Mystery Dungeon Sky. [ In anime ] In the anime, Eevee first appeared in The Battling Eevee Brothers. A little boy named Mikey was hiding the Evolution Pokémon from his three older brothers because they wanted him to evolve it. However, when Mikey&apos;s Eevee single-handedly defeated Team Rocket, they were able to accept the fact that Mikey wanted to keep his Eevee just the way it is. [ The Battling Eevee Brothers Pokémon Atsuhiro Tomioka (writer) Various October 27, 1998 Indigo League 40 ] Ash&apos;s longtime rival Gary Oak uses an Eevee of great quality that eventually evolves into Umbreon. May has an Eevee that hatched from an egg, which she used in Pokémon Contest s all across the Kanto and Johto region. When May traveled to Sinnoh, she took it to Route 217 to evolve into a Glaceon. [ Espeon, Not Included Pokémon Kiyotaka Itani (writer) Various February 1, 2001 The Johto Journeys 226 ] [ In other media ] In Pokémon Adventures, Red is in possession of an Eevee which had been experimented on by Team Rocket. As a result, it could transform back and forth from the three evolutions Vaporeon, Jolteon, and Flareon along with its base form, allowing it greater tactical ability in fighting other Pokémon. Eventually, it evolved into an Espeon, losing its special ability to interchange abilities. In the Electric Tale of Pikachu manga, the character Mikey (who appeared in the anime episode: &quot; The Battling Eevee Brothers &quot; ) makes an appearance with his own Eevee and within the chapter that he appears in, Mikey attempts to prove to his brothers that he doesn&apos;t need to evolve his Eevee to win battles.
