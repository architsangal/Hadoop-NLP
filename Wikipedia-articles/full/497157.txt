[ yes 1973 12 27 ] { Brooklyn, New York, U.S. } { 1994 –present } Wilson Cruz (born Wilson Echevarría; December 27, 1973) is an American actor, known for playing Rickie Vasquez on My So-Called Life [ ABC Leads Nominees for GLAAD Awards http://www.foxnews.com/story/0%2C2933%2C324371%2C00.html 8 February 2011 Fox News 21 May 2008 Associated Press ] and the recurring character Genito on Noah&apos;s Arc. [ http://movies.nytimes.com/person/199888/Wilson-Cruz The New York Times The New York Times 2012-01-13 ] As an openly gay person of Afro-Puerto Rican ancestry, he has served as an advocate for gay youth, especially gay youth of color. [ http://www.talkinbroadway.com/regional/dallas/dallas79.html Talkin&apos; Broadway Talkinbroadway.com 2012-01-13 ] [ http://www.atlantaboy.com/gay_atlanta/2006/12/wilson_cruz_exc.html Atlanta Boy Atlantaboy.com 2012-01-13 ] [ http://www.oasismag.com/Issues/9512/fea0895-cruz.html Oasis Magazine Oasis Magazine 2012-01-13 ] [ Early life ] Wilson Cruz was born in Brooklyn, New York to parents of Afro-Puerto Rican descent. At age 19, Cruz came out to his parents as gay, first to his mother and then his father. While his mother was initially hurt and shocked, she eventually accepted the news. His father, however, threw him out of the house, and Cruz spent the next few months living in his car and at the homes of friends. He later reconciled with his father. [ October 2010 ] [ Acting career ] After coming out to his parents, Cruz went to Hollywood to seek work as an actor, intending to be open about his sexuality from the beginning of his career. In 1994 he was cast as Enrique &quot; Rickie &quot; Vasquez, a troubled gay teen, in the short-lived critically acclaimed cult classic TV series My So-Called Life. In one episode (entitled &quot; So-Called Angels &quot; ) drawn from Cruz&apos;s own life, Rickie comes out to his family, who throw him out of the house. Following My So-Called Life&apos;s cancellation, Cruz went on to play J. Edgar Hoover &apos;s servant Joaquin in Oliver Stone&apos;s film Nixon and had a small role in the television movie On Seventh Avenue. In 1996, he appeared with David Arquette as Mikey in Johns, about the day-to-day struggles of male prostitute s. In 1998, he portrayed Angel in the Broadway production of RENT and in 2000 played Victor in the final season of Party of Five. He also had a recurring role as Rafael de la Cruz on the series, Raising the Bar. Cruz&apos;s other acting credits include the films Joyride (1996), All Over Me (1997), Supernova (2000), Party Monster (2003), Coffee Date (2007) and Margaret Cho&apos;s &quot; Bam Bam &amp; Celeste (2008) He&apos;s Just Not That Into You (2009); the television film The Perfect Pitch (2002); and guest appearances on the series Great Scott!, Sister, Sister, ER, Ally McBeal, The West Wing, Noah&apos;s Arc, and Grey&apos;s Anatomy. Cruz starred as Adrian in the film Ode (2007), based on the novel Ode to Lata by Ghalib Shiraz Dhalla.He also appeared/starred in the 2009 movie &quot; The People I&apos;ve Slept With &quot; He plays the openly gay best friend of a promiscuous woman who tries to find out who got her pregnant so that she can get married. Also starring Chris Zylka and Archie Kao. He is currently guest starring as Jonni in &quot; The Finder. &quot; [ Involvement in LGBT community ] Cruz works with and advocates on behalf of LGBT youth, especially youth of color. He has volunteered his time as host for the Youth Zone, an online community at Gay.com for LGBT youth. He was the Grand Marshal of the 1998 West Hollywood Gay Pride parade and the 2005 Chicago Pride Parade. In 2008, he was the keynote speaker at the University of Illinois at Chicago &apos;s Lavender Graduation and Rainbow Banquet honoring graduating LGBT students.
