[ Telfair County Georgia 1807 December 10 McRae 444.08 441.09 2.99 2010 16500 Eastern -5 -4 1st telfaircounty.georgia.gov/ Edward Telfair ] Telfair County is a county located in the U.S. state of Georgia. As of the 2010 census, the population was 16,500. [ United States Census Bureau 2010 Census Data http://2010.census.gov/2010census/data/ United States Census Bureau 6 March 2012 ] The county seat is McRae. [ 6 ] [ History ] Chevron beads Archaeologists associated with Atlanta&apos;s Fernbank Museum of Natural History have been excavating a [ 2000 acre km2 on ] plot near McRae, approximately a mile away from the Ocmulgee River. They have found evidence of a Spanish settlement dating back to the first half of the 16th century. [ http://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2009/11/091105084838.htm Archaeologists Track Infamous Conquistador Through Southeast Science Daily 2009-11-09 ] The archaeologists suspect that the artifacts originated from a settlement founded by Lucas Vázquez de Ayllón in 1526. { Davis, Mark, &quot; What Lies Beneath, &quot; Atlanta Journal-Constitution, December 17, 2007, page C1. } Additional research suggested that the site was one visited by the de Soto Expedition of 1541. They recovered Murano glass beads, pottery fragments, and iron weapons. Some of the beads bear a chevron pattern believed to be hallmarks of the de Soto expedition, due to the limited timespan in which they were produced. Excavations have also produced six metal objects, including three iron tools and a silver pendant. { Pousner, Howard, &quot; Fernbank archaeologist confident he has found de Soto site, &quot; Atlanta Journal-Constitution, November 6, 2009. } This is further west than the de Soto expedition was earlier believed to have traveled. [ What we have now is the best-documented collection of Spanish artifacts in Georgia, many are unique, and they are the only examples of certain artifacts ever found outside Florida. 20px 20px - Archaeologist Dennis Blanton 2009 ] Telfair County was established on December 10, 1807, and is named for Edward Telfair, the sixteenth governor of Georgia and a member of the Continental Congress. The racial makeup of the county was 59.71% White, 38.44% Black or African American, 0.03% Native American, 0.20% Asian, 1.16% from other races, and 0.47% from two or more races. 1.82% of the population were Hispanic or Latino of any race. The average household size was 2.48 and the average family size was 3.01. In the county the population was spread out with 22.50% under the age of 18, 10.30% from 18 to 24, 30.10% from 25 to 44, 22.30% from 45 to 64, and 14.90% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 37 years. For every 100 females there were 110.90 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 113.20 males. The median income for a household in the county was $26,097, and the median income for a family was $32,513. Males had a median income of $26,444 versus $19,970 for females. The per capita income for the county was $14,197. About 17.30% of families and 21.20% of the population were below the poverty line, including 26.40% of those under age 18 and 23.70% of those age 65 or over.
