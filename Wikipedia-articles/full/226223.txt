Dial Square is the name of one of their workshops. At the end of the year, the players hold a pub meeting and change the club&apos;s name to Royal Arsenal. Five years later, the name is changed to Woolwich Arsenal. The club&apos;s present name will be adopted in 1914 after the move to Islington. 13 March — Tinsley Lindley scores in the first of his record 9 consecutive England games 10 May — The Football Association approves a proposal made by N. FA Cup final – Blackburn Rovers 2–0 West Bromwich Albion at The Oval; replay after 0–0 draw at The Oval. This is Blackburn&apos;s third successive victory and the final is the first to feature two extant members of The Football League. Hampshire ceases to be a first-class county after years of difficult circumstances and poor results. They do play matches against Surrey and Sussex in 1886 but these matches are not recognised as first-class.
