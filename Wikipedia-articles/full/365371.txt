[ August 2011 ] [ Danny Wallace ] [ 1976 11 16 y ] { Dundee, } { Scotland } { British } { Kingdom of Lovely } { Join Me; } { Yes Man; } { How to Start Your Own Country } { Media studies } { University of Westminster } { Charlie Chucky&apos;s Balloon } { Broadcaster } { London } { Greta Elizabeth &quot; Lizzie &quot; Wallace (née McMahon) } { 1 son } { http://www.dannywallace.com/ } Daniel Frederick Wallace (born 16 November 1976) is a Scottish filmmaker, comedian, writer, actor, and presenter of radio and television. His notable works include the books Join Me, Yes Man, voice acting for the Assassin&apos;s Creed game series, and the TV series How to Start Your Own Country. He lives in London, with his wife, an Australian publicist. Throughout Danny&apos;s books, his wife is referred to as &quot; Lizzie &quot;, at her request. [ Biography ] [ Early life ] Wallace was born in Dundee, Scotland and attended Park Place Primary School but also grew up in Loughborough and Bath, England, though thinks of himself as Scottish. [ May 2012 ] He began writing reviews for video game magazines at the age of 13 for school work experience: a reviewer had become ill and so Wallace was given the opportunity to review a game. At 18 he started writing comedy, mainly through the magazine Comedy Review. He specialised in media studies at the University of Westminster. [ Early career and journalism ] At 22, he became a BBC producer. He was part of the production team behind British Comedy Award -winning Dead Ringers, the original producer of the critically acclaimed cult hit The Mighty Boosh, and the creator and producer of Ross Noble Goes Global. As a journalist, Wallace has worked for The Scotsman, The Guardian, The Independent, Elle, Cosmo, The Times and other publications. Since 2007, Wallace has written a column in the free weekly magazine ShortList, circulation 520,000. { http://www.shortlist.com/ ShortList website } In September 2009 it was announced in his column that he and his wife were expecting their first child together. Their son was born on 29 December 2009. [ Books ] As an author, Wallace&apos;s best-selling books have been translated into more than a dozen languages. { &quot; Danny Wallace website &quot;. Dannywallace.com. Retrieved on 26 August 2011. } [ Are You Dave Gorman? ] [ Are You Dave Gorman? ] In 1999, Wallace challenged comedian Dave Gorman, who at the time was his flatmate, to find 54 other people called Dave Gorman ( &quot; one for every card in the deck, including the Jokers &quot; ). Wallace accompanied Gorman on his quest and the men created Are You Dave Gorman?, an award-winning comedy stage show about what happened during their journey. { Comedy CV, Dave Gorman } A BBC series, also co-written and co-produced by Wallace, followed, as did a book, written by both men. [ Join Me ] [ Join Me ] In 2003, Wallace&apos;s book Join Me was published. The book explains how he &quot; accidentally started a &apos; cult &apos; &quot; called Join Me. The movement would go global, with each member committing to undertaking one random act of kindness for a stranger every Friday ( &quot; Good Fridays &quot; ). Tens of thousands joined. Join Me celebrates &quot; Karmageddon 10 &quot; in December 2011. Traditionally, hundreds of members travel to London for the meet-up and undertake good deeds for strangers, with Wallace present. The movement is now generally referred to as the &quot; Karma Army &quot;, although members are still typically &quot; Joinees &quot;. He became a minor celebrity in Belgium whilst on his quest for Joinees. While on a book tour through America, Wallace was dubbed a &quot; Generation X legend &quot; by the Wisconsin State Journal. { Simon &amp; Schuster } [ August 2011 ] {, &quot; Yes Man (Trade Paperback) – Praise &quot; } [ Random Acts of Kindness: 365 Ways To Make the World A Nicer Place ] Wallace next wrote a short book called Random Acts of Kindness: 365 Ways To Make the World A Better Place, with the help of submissions from Joinees. It includes many humorous Random Acts of Kindness (RAoK) ideas, such as &quot; Contradict Demeaning Graffiti &quot;, and &quot; Make An Old Man Very Happy. &quot; [ Yes Man ] [ Yes Man (book) ] Wallace&apos;s second solo book, Yes Man was published in July 2005. In it, he describes how he spent six months &quot; saying Yes where once I would have said No &quot;, to make his life more interesting and positive. In this book he shows the tribulations and mischief that he got up to while he said yes to any question or proposal. The book was described as &quot; one of those rare books that actually has the potential to change your life &quot; by the San Francisco Bay Guardian and as &quot; a fascinating book and a fascinating experiment &quot; by David Letterman. { Books section of Danny&apos;s website. Dannywallace.com. Retrieved on 26 August 2011. } A film adaptation of Yes Man was developed with Warner Bros. and stars Jim Carrey and Zooey Deschanel. It was released on 19 December 2008 in the US and 26 December 2008 in the UK. { IMDb movie profile Yes Man 16 December 2007 } Wallace appeared on screen in a cameo in a bar scene in the last ten minutes of the film, holding a British pint glass. The film made almost $100  million at the US Box Office. { Box Office Mojo } The DVD of Yes Man was released in April 2009 and received a 1 out of 5 star review from The Independent. Two days later, it received 4 out of 5 from The Independent on Sunday. It is linked with World Book Day which in 2006 was on Thursday 2 March. The cover identifies it as a &quot; Quick Read &quot;; the price and length of the book have been curbed in order to encourage people who may not often read books to purchase it. [ Friends Like These ] Wallace&apos;s book, Friends Like These, was released on 3 July 2008, and tells the story of how he spent a summer trying to track down his old school friends from his days in Dundee, Scotland, and beyond. { Amazon.com,Friends Like These was released 3 July 2008 } The film rights went to Miramax and a screenplay is currently underway. [ Awkward Situations for Men ] Wallace&apos;s book Awkward Situations for Men was released on 3 June 2010 and reached number 3 in the best-sellers list. The follow-up, More Awkward Situations for Men, was released on 30 June 2011. [ Charlotte Street ] Wallace&apos;s latest book and debut novel, Charlotte Street, was released on 10 May 2012 under Ebury Publishing.
